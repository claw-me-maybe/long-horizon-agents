How to read "uplift"

  • Scope discipline: did swarm make `v1` smaller and more believable?
  • Role fidelity: does the artifact sound like the right job now?
  • Measurability: are inputs, outputs, and success criteria easier to score?
  • Iteration value: do later rounds add real signal instead of padding?

This is intentionally hand-wavey. I used qualitative judgment first, with changed-line counts only as a churn proxy, not as the score itself.

Punchline: most churn did not equal most uplift. The biggest winners were the artifacts that became narrower, more checkable, and more occupationally faithful.

Where swarm helped most

Most uplift overall
Software Developers
  • `v1` stopped pretending to be a full service build and became a genuine 15-minute first pass.
  • Later iterations added explicit Redis payloads, ACK semantics, retry and DLQ behavior, and fixed-window throttling.
  • The artifact now has crisp interfaces, crisp tests, and a believable iteration ladder.

Translation: swarm works very well when the job already exposes concrete contracts.

Biggest role-fit jump
Executive Assistants
  • The artifact moved down from a chief-of-staff voice into an actual EA pre-brief.
  • Confirmed facts got separated from guidance; logistics became owned actions with deadlines.
  • The Q&A got harder without turning into investment-bank cosplay.

Translation: swarm can rescue artifacts that are close, but written at the wrong seniority layer.

Where uplift was weakest

Least uplift
Instructional Coordinators
  • The artifact improved operationally, but it still wants to become a district strategy packet, board memo, and implementation framework all at once.
  • Every good addition risks making the benchmark less bounded.
  • Even after trimming evaluator leakage, it remains the most likely artifact to over-coach the model.

Why this domain is harder

  • Political surface area: many stakeholders, many constraints, many "correct" scopes.
  • Weak natural stopping point: the artifact keeps asking for another section.
  • Judgment is diffuse: success is less about one crisp deliverable and more about balanced planning taste.

Management was also middling: lots of cleanup value, less true transformation.

Best artifact vs. worst artifact

Best artifact after swarm
Webhook Dispatcher Spec
  • Inputs are concrete.
  • Output is easy to imagine and easy to score.
  • Each iteration adds one visible layer of real engineering work.
  • The artifact invites improvement without bloating.
Weakest artifact after swarm
K-5 Literacy Adoption Framework
  • Still too broad for a clean benchmark target.
  • Still tempted to drift into theory-of-action and political messaging.
  • Improved content did not fully solve the scoping problem.
  • The model can look smart here while still being hard to evaluate.

Takeaways from each domain

Computer & Mathematical
The biggest win was turning a fake `v1` into a real `v1`.
Business & Financial Ops
Swarm helped by making review logic deterministic, not by making it more creative.
Educational Instruction
Scope explosion is the enemy. More content can make the benchmark worse.
Management
Useful cleanup domain: better boundaries, less leakage, modest transformation.
Office & Admin
Role fidelity matters as much as detail. Wrong seniority layer kills the artifact fast.
Meta
The swarm did best when it narrowed the task, not when it made the document longer.
