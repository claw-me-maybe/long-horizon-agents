Bigger thesis: trillions of decisions

The artifact readout matters because the long-run value of AI may come less from a few brilliant outputs and more from dropping models into trillions of tiny decision loops.

Decision distribution

most of the mass is banal

If trillions of actions become AI decisions, then tiny competent models in many loops may matter more than a small number of genius decisions.

Implications

Claim 1
Big uplift comes from AI choosing actions at extreme volume, not from a few heroic outputs.
Claim 2
At that scale, most calls are easy. The hard part is getting a model into every loop.
Claim 3
For social transformation, deployment and system throughput matter more than extra frontier IQ.
Why this deck connects
The winning artifacts looked like decision machinery: interfaces, bounds, escalations.

Stage 1, then stage 2

A plausible industry pattern: first the model floods the stack, then it retreats into the places bespoke software still cannot cover cleanly.

Stage 1: AI immersion

models dropped everywhere in the workflow
whole industries temporarily wrapped in general AI
coverage matters more than elegance
common behavior still burns expensive model calls

Stage 2: retreat into the cracks

bespoke software absorbs the common path
specialized AIs handle only the hard escalations
models write narrow code and orchestration
next gap: on-demand specialist weights
Why software-like artifacts scored high
They already resemble the long-run shape: lots of narrow systems, lots of easy decisions, and explicit escalation paths for the few cases that still need heavier intelligence.
