# Iterated Plan: 27-3041-editors

## Objective
Deliver a Graywolf-quality developmental edit letter that gives Naledi a new, usable model of the manuscript's architecture, focuses revision pressure on the Bristol/Temi thread, and answers the Aunty Fola question decisively enough to unblock an 8-week revision window before copyedit.

## Required output shape
1. Opening praise: 2 paragraphs, specific and earned.
2. Big-picture diagnosis: 2-4 issues, each with a named heading, diagnosis, and genuinely distinct unlock options.
3. Thread-by-thread notes: one section each for Abeni, Folasade, Temi, with Temi materially longer.
4. Targeted questions: 6-10, including at least two non-obvious questions.
5. Direct Aunty Fola answer with rationale.
6. Scope and next steps, including what not to attempt in this pass.

## Execution roadmap
### Pass 0: Build the editorial model
- Summarize the book's current architecture in one paragraph: three generations, three time periods, four parts, alternating POVs.
- Identify the likely core failure mode: the 2023 Bristol thread is not yet carrying equal thematic and structural weight.
- Decide the single highest-priority revision sentence for the letter: if Naledi fixes only one thing, it should be to clarify Temi's interior arc and make the contemporary thread causally indispensable to the novel's braid.

### Pass 1: Draft the spine of the letter
- Write the opening praise first and keep it between 120 and 400 words.
- Name at least two scenes or effects that moved the imagined reader.
- Draft 2-3 big-picture issues before touching detailed thread notes.
- Fully draft the Temi/Bristol section in this pass.
- Add a provisional Aunty Fola answer immediately so the letter already has editorial courage.

### Pass 2: Strengthen the big-picture diagnosis
- Expand each issue into:
  - Diagnosis: what is structurally happening now.
  - Reader effect: what it does or does not do for the imagined reader.
  - Unlock options: at least two meaningfully different strategies.
- Keep issues distinct. Good issue buckets:
  - Temi's interiority and narrative pressure.
  - Balance and handoff across the braided timeline.
  - Whether political/familial stakes pay off with equal force across threads.

### Pass 3: Fill the thread-by-thread notes
- Abeni/Lagos: keep focused, since it is reportedly working.
- Folasade/Peckham: diagnose connective tissue and middle-architecture burden.
- Temi/Bristol: give extra space, cite multiple chapter moments, and connect local scene work back to the book's overall shape.
- Ensure thread notes are not symmetrical for symmetry's sake.

### Pass 4: Targeted questions and Aunty Fola ruling
- Write 6-10 questions that force choices about shame, desire, loyalty, and causality.
- Include at least two questions Naledi likely has not already posed to herself.
- Finalize the Aunty Fola answer as a direct keep/cut/reduce/repurpose call, with rationale tied to narrative function, not taste.

### Pass 5: Scope management and polish
- Add explicit boundaries: this pass is not for line-level polish, new subplot expansion without structural purpose, or cosmetic balancing.
- Explain what belongs in the next line-edit pass instead.
- Add a rough timeline keyed to the April 25, 2026 delivery and 8-week revision window.

## Evidence and citation plan
- Use at least 8 citations in `Ch. #, p. ##` format.
- Spread citations across praise, diagnosis, and thread notes rather than clustering them.
- Use craft vocabulary precisely: interiority, narrative distance, braided structure, third-act reversal, causal handoff.

## Intermediate artifacts
- One-paragraph manuscript architecture summary.
- Issue map with 2-4 named structural problems.
- Citation bank with at least 8 chapter/page anchors.
- Temi/Bristol thread note drafted to near-final quality before the other thread sections.
- Final section-level outline showing exact order of the letter before prose polish.

## Checkpoints
- Checkpoint 1: opening praise drafted and still clearly specific to this novel.
- Checkpoint 2: highest-priority issue is explicit and can be stated in one sentence.
- Checkpoint 3: Aunty Fola answer exists in direct language before final polish.
- Checkpoint 4: Temi section is visibly longer and more diagnostic than Lagos.

## Final QA gate
- No sentence should survive if it could apply to another multigenerational literary novel unchanged.
- Every big-picture issue must contain at least two meaningfully different unlock paths.
- The closing scope section must actively prevent scope creep rather than merely summarize next steps.

## Risks and mitigations
- Risk: generic praise. Mitigation: every praise sentence must mention a book-specific effect, image system, or structural move.
- Risk: diagnostic mush. Mitigation: separate architecture, character, and pacing/thematic issues.
- Risk: punting on Aunty Fola. Mitigation: write the answer before final polish.
- Risk: over-scoping. Mitigation: explicitly tell Naledi what not to fix in this round.
- Risk: insufficient Bristol emphasis. Mitigation: Temi gets the longest thread section by design.

## Definition of done
- The letter gives Naledi a mental model she did not previously have.
- One issue is explicitly prioritized above the others.
- The opening praise is warm but not vague.
- The Aunty Fola section is decisive and reasoned.
- A Graywolf editor could plausibly have sent this letter.
