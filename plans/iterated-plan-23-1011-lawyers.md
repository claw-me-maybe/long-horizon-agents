# Iterated Plan: 23-1011-lawyers

## Objective
Deliver a predictive office memorandum that tells the partner whether the non-compete is enforceable, where reformation risk sits, whether the USB-copy events support stronger injunctive leverage under trade-secret theories, and what filing or settlement posture is most defensible this week.

## Required output shape
1. Heading block.
2. Question Presented.
3. Brief Answer.
4. Statement of Facts.
5. Discussion / Analysis.
6. Recommendation.
7. Open issues / further investigation.

## Execution roadmap
### Pass 0: Build the issue map
- Separate threshold validity under `G.L. c. 149, § 24L` from reasonableness and remedy analysis.
- Required statutory buckets:
  - notice timing,
  - independent consideration,
  - garden leave / mutually agreed consideration,
  - duration,
  - geographic scope,
  - activity scope,
  - legitimate business interest,
  - choice-of-law / Massachusetts nexus.
- Then add:
  - PI factors under `Packaging Industries`,
  - DTSA/MUTSA theory from the USB-copy events.

### Pass 1: Draft the predictive front end
- Write a one-sentence Question Presented with embedded sub-issues.
- Draft a Brief Answer that is useful, not mushy:
  - likely enforceable in substantial part,
  - real reformation risk on US/Canada geography,
  - strongest near-term injunction theory may rest on trade-secret misuse.

### Pass 2: Build the neutral fact section
- Anchor the chronology:
  - hire date,
  - promotion and $90,000 retention bonus,
  - delivery and signing dates,
  - resignation and last day,
  - Kettlepoint announcement and start date,
  - USB-copy events,
  - Massachusetts office-of-record and Providence remote-work fact.
- Keep argument out of this section.

### Pass 3: Analyze MNAA validity first
- Notice: assess 10 business days against statutory requirement.
- Independent consideration: treat the promotion-era bonus as central.
- Garden leave / other consideration: address whether the agreement's 40% monthly payment term is compliant.
- Duration: 12 months is facially within the cap.
- Geography and activity scope: analyze overbreadth and blue-pencil risk separately.
- Legitimate business interest: tie to trade secrets, confidential pipeline and ForecastIQ materials, and goodwill with named accounts.

### Pass 4: Common-law reasonableness and remedy
- Apply `Boulanger` / `Marine Contractors` after the statutory analysis, not inside it.
- Run the `Packaging Industries` PI factors explicitly:
  - likelihood of success,
  - irreparable harm,
  - balance of harms,
  - public interest.
- Integrate USB copying into both likelihood and irreparable-harm analysis.

### Pass 5: DTSA/MUTSA and strategy
- Treat the forensic evidence as an independent or parallel path.
- Explain the practical strategic menu:
  - full PI on covenant plus trade secrets,
  - narrower TRO / PI focused on confidential-information misuse,
  - settlement via customer-specific non-solicit or named-account standstill.

## Open-fact development

- Need revenue-footprint facts if proposing geographic reformation.
- Need more detail on what was in `Pipeline_Q2` and `ForecastIQ_tuning`.

## Intermediate artifacts
- Statutory issue matrix keyed to each `§ 24L` subsection.
- Chronology of operative facts and documents.
- Separate PI-factor grid mapping facts to each factor.
- Trade-secret theory memolet focused on the USB-copy events and protected categories.
- Recommendation matrix comparing full PI, narrowed injunction, and settlement paths.

## Checkpoints
- Checkpoint 1: Brief Answer is predictive and specific enough to guide partner action.
- Checkpoint 2: MNAA threshold-validity analysis is complete before common-law reasonableness discussion begins.
- Checkpoint 3: USB-copy facts appear both in covenant analysis and independent trade-secret analysis.
- Checkpoint 4: at least three actionable recommendation paths are ranked with tradeoffs.

## Final QA gate
- If any section sounds like a plaintiff's brief, neutralize it.
- If any `§ 24L` element is implied but not expressly concluded, add a holding-level sentence.
- If the remote-work fact is not surfaced as a real uncertainty, the memo is underdeveloped.

## High-risk failure modes
- Conflating statutory validity with common-law reasonableness.
- Writing like an advocate instead of a predictor.
- Underplaying the geography problem.
- Treating the USB evidence as a side note instead of leverage.
- Ignoring the Massachusetts/Rhode Island employment-residency wrinkle.

## Definition of done
- Each required `§ 24L` element gets a holding-level conclusion.
- The PI analysis maps facts to all four factors.
- The partner can choose among at least three ranked options.
- The memo reads like internal work product, not a brief.
