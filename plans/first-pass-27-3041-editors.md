# First-Pass Plan: 27-3041-editors

## Goal
Produce a developmental edit letter that gives the author a clear structural model of the manuscript, prioritizes the highest-leverage revision work, and answers the Aunty Fola question directly.

## Working assumptions
- The manuscript itself is not available, so execution depends on synthesizing the provided scenario, margin-note count, author letter, comps, and known thread imbalance.
- The Bristol/Temi thread is the likely center of gravity for diagnosis and therefore should receive the most space in both analysis and revision planning.
- The output should feel like a Graywolf editorial letter: craft-forward, specific, warm, and decisive.

## First-pass execution sequence
1. Extract the non-negotiable constraints from the prompt.
   - Letter format, 2,200-3,200 words, direct address to Naledi.
   - Opening praise, big-picture diagnosis, thread-by-thread notes, targeted questions, Aunty Fola answer, scope/next steps.
2. Build a one-page issue map before drafting prose.
   - Core architecture problem.
   - Bristol/Temi diagnosis.
   - Braided-structure and causality concerns.
   - Character/interiority question around Aunty Fola and Temi.
3. Draft the opening first.
   - Name what the novel uniquely does.
   - Identify at least two genuinely moving moments.
4. Draft 2-3 big-picture issues with named headings.
   - Each issue needs diagnosis plus an unlock paragraph with real alternatives.
5. Fully draft the Temi/Bristol thread section.
   - This is the one section that cannot be skeletal in v1.
6. Answer the Aunty Fola question decisively.
7. Leave placeholders or compressed notes for the Lagos and Peckham threads, targeted questions, and scope section if time runs short.

## What must be true in v1
- The opening praise is concrete, not generic.
- There is an explicit “if you only fix one thing” priority.
- The Bristol thread gets materially more attention than Lagos.
- The Aunty Fola answer is a reasoned yes/no/third-option, not a punt.

## Main risks
- Writing a letter that could apply to any literary novel instead of this one.
- Being too vague about how to strengthen Temi.
- Turning the unlock paragraphs into disguised single-solution prescriptions.
- Sounding teacherly or punitive instead of collegial and direct.

## Immediate iteration queue
- Add chapter/page citations throughout.
- Expand thread-by-thread sections for Abeni and Folasade.
- Sharpen targeted questions so at least two probe hidden desire/shame or causality.
- Tighten tonal balance between praise and critique.
