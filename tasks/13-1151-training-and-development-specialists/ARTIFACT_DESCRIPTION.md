# "De-escalation for New Frontline Managers" — 3-Day Blended Curriculum for Rushford Health Network

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 13-1151
- **Title:** Training and Development Specialists

## Scenario
Rushford Health Network, a 14-hospital system in Upstate New York, logged a 42% increase in verbal aggression incidents against front-desk and admissions staff between 2023 and 2025, much of it escalating at the supervisor hand-off. Learning & Development Director Priya Manickavasagar has asked you to design a 3-day blended curriculum for newly-promoted frontline managers (target cohort: 84 managers across emergency, ambulatory, and admissions), to launch on June 2, 2026. The executive sponsor (COO Sylvain Waterstrade) has approved a $180K pilot and expects a measurable reduction in assault-related lost-time incidents and in clinician turnover linked to conflict. The curriculum must meet NY OSHA Healthcare Workplace Violence Prevention requirements and be audit-defensible.

## Inputs

**Needs analysis findings (conducted Jan–Mar 2026)**
- 22 manager interviews, 6 observations, 2 focus groups
- Top skill gaps identified: verbal de-escalation (95%), setting/holding limits (88%), managing team trauma after an incident (74%), documenting and debriefing (65%), recognizing own physiological stress response (61%)
- 68% of current managers had been promoted within the past 18 months; 44% had no prior management training
- 2025 incident data: 1,412 verbal-aggression incidents; 87 resulted in lost time; median escalation time 4.2 min

**Stakeholder asks**
- Union (1199SEIU): No punitive documentation language; training time must be paid
- Legal: Must align with NYS Labor Law §27-b and OSHA Pub 3148-06R
- Clinical Education Council: must be co-facilitated by a clinician on Days 2–3
- Patient Experience: content on bias, psychological safety, and trauma-informed language

**Modality constraints**
- Day 1: virtual synchronous (4 hrs) — for shift coverage
- Day 2: in-person simulation (8 hrs) at the Rushford Learning Lab (capacity 16/session)
- Day 3: on-the-unit coaching (4 hrs) with a Master Preceptor
- Learners can retain at most 60 minutes of pre-work
- LMS: Cornerstone; xAPI supported; video hosted on Kaltura

**Business outcomes to improve**
- Reduce assault-related lost-time incidents by 25% in 12 months
- Reduce 90-day new-manager attrition from 22% to < 10%
- Raise "my manager handles conflict well" survey score from 3.1 to 4.0 (5-pt)

## Artifact specification
Full curriculum package. Required deliverables (all within one document):
1. **Program charter** — business case, outcomes, sponsor, audience, constraints
2. **Learner personas** (2–3; e.g., "Dion — promoted from triage RN, 8 weeks in")
3. **Outcome map** — Kirkpatrick L1–L4 with measures and instruments
4. **Learning objectives** — 8–12 objectives, behavioral (action verb + condition + criterion)
5. **Curriculum architecture** — module map showing flow, modality, time, assessment
6. **Day-by-day agenda** — Day 1 virtual; Day 2 simulation; Day 3 on-the-unit; with activities, timings, facilitator notes
7. **Simulation scenarios** — at least 3 fully-scripted scenarios for Day 2 (scenario setup, learner role, confederate role, debrief prompts)
8. **Assessment plan** — pre/post self-assessment; simulation rubric; 30/60/90-day observed behavior checklist
9. **Facilitator guide summary** — prep checklist, required materials, contingency for triggering content
10. **Equity and trauma-informed design notes**
11. **Evaluation and measurement plan** — for L1–L4, including data source and timing
12. **Rollout plan** — cohort schedule, comms, manager pre-briefing
13. **Appendix** — references (citing OSHA 3148-06R, NYS Labor Law §27-b), reading list, glossary

Tone: instructional-design professional, learner-centered, trauma-informed. Use action verbs in objectives (Bloom-aligned); avoid "understand," "be familiar with."

## Output format
Markdown curriculum document. Target 3,200–4,200 words (dense; much of the length is agenda and scenarios).

## Iteration targets
1. Objectives often drift toward knowledge ("know the 5 stages"); rewrite to performance ("demonstrate active-listening response to a shouting patient in a scripted scenario").
2. Day 2 simulation scenarios typically lack debrief prompts tied to objectives; map each scenario to 2–3 objectives and write 4–5 debrief prompts per scenario.
3. Kirkpatrick L3 (behavior) measurement is usually a survey item; strengthen to include the 30/60/90-day observed checklist with defined trigger observations.
4. The trauma-informed design notes often appear as a disclaimer; move earlier and make them design choices (e.g., opt-out during intense simulation, co-regulation break).
5. The pre-work is often over-allocated; compress to ≤60 minutes and cut anything that doesn't enable Day 1.
6. Evaluation plan often lacks the lagging business metric link (assault lost-time, attrition); connect those explicitly to the training outputs with a reasonable causal path.

## Success criteria
- Every learning objective is behavioral and measurable; no "understand."
- Day 2 simulation scenarios are immediately runnable by a trained facilitator without further design work.
- Evaluation plan connects each Kirkpatrick level to an instrument and a timing, and includes a plausible link to the business outcomes.
- Program respects regulatory and CBA constraints — documentation isn't punitive; paid-time and OSHA/§27-b are addressed.
- Equity and trauma-informed practices are embedded in design, not appended.
- A newly-promoted frontline manager reading the Day-by-Day agenda knows what they'll do and how they'll be assessed.

## Scope target
v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: program charter, one learner persona, outcome map with L1–L4 entries, 8 draft learning objectives, module architecture, skeletal day-by-day agendas for all 3 days, one fully-scripted simulation scenario for Day 2, a draft assessment plan, and an evaluation/measurement outline. Roughly 1,900–2,300 words; additional simulations, facilitator guide details, and full appendix are iteration work.
