## 2. Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this report was to document the validation of analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, a reversed-phase HPLC-UV assay for quantitation of losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets. The validated method is intended for release and stability testing of commercial lots manufactured at Meridian Pharma Sciences, Cambridge, MA, in support of the Q3 2026 commercial launch. Validation was executed against pre-approved protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 in accordance with ICH Q2(R2), USP <1225>, and the FDA Guidance for Industry on Analytical Procedures and Methods Validation (July 2015). The scope encompasses specificity, linearity, range, accuracy, precision (repeatability and intermediate precision), robustness, solution stability, filter compatibility, and system suitability.

## 5. Summary of Method

Losartan potassium was quantitated by reversed-phase HPLC with UV detection at 254 nm. Chromatographic separation was performed on a Waters XBridge C18 column (150 x 4.6 mm, 3.5 um) maintained at 35 C with isocratic elution using 65:35 (v/v) 0.01 M potassium phosphate buffer pH 3.0 : acetonitrile, at 1.0 mL/min flow and 10 uL injection volume. Sample preparation comprised compositing 20 tablets, grinding, and extracting a portion equivalent to 50 mg losartan into 100 mL of diluent (mobile phase A : acetonitrile 50:50) with 15 min sonication, followed by 0.45 um PVDF filtration with the first 3 mL discarded. Losartan eluted at approximately 4.20 min (k' approximately 3.5). Quantitation was against an external single-point standard prepared at nominal 0.5 mg/mL. Full procedural detail is held in method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03; this report does not reproduce it.

## 6. Validation Results

**Procedure.** Five replicate injections of the 100% working standard (0.5 mg/mL losartan) were performed at the start of each validation sequence on each instrument.

**Acceptance Criteria.** USP tailing <= 2.0; theoretical plates (N, USP) >= 2000; RSD of peak area across five replicates <= 2.0%.

| Peak-area RSD, n=5 (%) | <= 2.0 | 0.42 |
| Resolution from impurity A | report | 5.6 |

**Result.** Pass.

### 6.2 Specificity

**Procedure.** Specificity was evaluated by (a) injection of diluent blank, placebo matrix, and a composite-excipient extract; (b) forced-degradation stress of drug substance and drug product per ICH Q1A(R2); and (c) DAD peak-purity assessment of the losartan peak under each stress condition. Mass balance was calculated as the sum of percent assay plus percent total degradants.

**Acceptance Criteria.** No interfering peak at the retention time of losartan from diluent, placebo, or excipient blank. Peak purity angle < purity threshold (Empower default) for losartan under each stress. Meaningful degradation (5-20% loss of parent) demonstrated in at least one stress condition. Mass balance 95-105%.

**Table 4. Placebo and Diluent Interference (Att. 1 p. 4-7)**

| Sample | Peak at RT 4.2 min | Interference at 100% response |
|---|---|---|
| Diluent blank | none detected | < 0.05% |
| Placebo matrix | none detected | < 0.10% |
| Excipient composite | none detected | < 0.10% |

**Table 5. Forced-Degradation Summary (Att. 1 p. 8-22)**

| Condition | Exposure | % Losartan Remaining | % Total Degradants | Mass Balance (%) | Purity Angle | Purity Threshold | Result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Acid | 1 N HCl, 60 C, 2 h | 96.4 | 3.2 | 99.6 | 0.142 | 0.312 | Pass |
| Base | 1 N NaOH, 60 C, 2 h | 88.7 | 10.6 | 99.3 | 0.188 | 0.327 | Pass |
| Peroxide | 3% H2O2, RT, 2 h | 94.1 | 5.4 | 99.5 | 0.161 | 0.318 | Pass |

**Result.** Pass. The base hydrolysis condition produced 11.3% loss of parent, bracketing the 5-20% target range; the principal degradant co-eluted with losartan Related Compound A (carboxylic acid impurity) at approximately 2.80 min. No interference was observed from placebo or diluent. Peak purity angle was less than purity threshold for losartan under every stress condition.

## 8. Conclusion

The HPLC-UV assay for losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets, as described in method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, has been validated against the acceptance criteria pre-approved in protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02. All ICH Q2(R2) characteristics required for a Category I assay — specificity, linearity, range, accuracy, precision (repeatability and intermediate precision), and robustness — passed their pre-stated acceptance criteria. Supporting characteristics (system suitability, solution stability, filter compatibility) also passed. The method is validated for use in release and stability testing of commercial 50 mg losartan potassium film-coated tablets. The method is not validated for content uniformity testing at the single-tablet level; that parameter is addressed under a separate protocol.
