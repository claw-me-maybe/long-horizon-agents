## 2. Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this report was to document the validation of analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, a reversed-phase HPLC-UV assay for quantitation of losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets, against the pre-approved acceptance criteria of protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02. The validated method is intended for commercial release and stability testing of finished-product tablets manufactured at Meridian Pharma Sciences (Cambridge, MA) in support of the Q3 2026 commercial launch and PAI anticipated August 2026. Validation was executed in accordance with ICH Q2(R2) (2023), USP General Chapters <1225> and <621>, and the FDA Guidance for Industry on Analytical Procedures and Methods Validation (July 2015). The validation parameters within scope were: specificity, linearity, range, accuracy, precision (repeatability and intermediate precision), robustness, solution stability, filter compatibility, and system suitability. LOQ/LOD were not within scope for this Category I assay; indicative signal-to-noise values at the 50% linearity level are reported for completeness.

## 5. Summary of Method

Losartan potassium was quantitated by reversed-phase HPLC with UV detection at 254 nm. Chromatographic separation was performed on a Waters XBridge C18 column (150 x 4.6 mm, 3.5 um) at 35 C under isocratic elution with 65:35 (v/v) 0.01 M potassium phosphate buffer pH 3.0 : acetonitrile, flow 1.0 mL/min, injection volume 10 uL, run time 10 min. Losartan eluted at approximately 4.20 min (k' approximately 3.5). Sample preparation comprised compositing 20 tablets, grinding, and extracting a portion equivalent to 50 mg losartan into 100 mL of diluent (mobile phase A : acetonitrile 50:50) with 15 min sonication, followed by 0.45 um PVDF filtration with the first 3 mL discarded. Quantitation was performed against an external single-point standard at 0.500 mg/mL (100% level). Full procedural detail resides in method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03; this report does not reproduce it.

### 6.2 Specificity

**Procedure executed.** Specificity was evaluated by (a) injection of diluent blank, placebo matrix, and composite excipient extract; (b) forced-degradation stress per ICH Q1A(R2) stress conditions applied to drug substance and drug product in parallel; (c) DAD peak-purity evaluation of the losartan peak under every stress condition, using purity angle vs purity threshold in Empower 3 (spectral range 200-400 nm, reference wavelength 254 nm); and (d) mass balance calculation as % assay of remaining losartan plus % total degradants (quantitated against the losartan external standard, correction factor 1.0).

**Pre-approved acceptance criteria** (MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 §7.2).
- No interfering peak co-eluting with losartan from diluent, placebo, or excipient extract.
- Peak purity angle < peak purity threshold (Empower default, noise-corrected) for losartan under every stress condition.
- At least one stress condition yielding 5-20% loss of parent.
- Mass balance 95-105%.
- Major base-hydrolysis degradant identified against ICH Q3B(R2) identification threshold for a daily dose of 50 mg (threshold 0.2% or 1.0 mg, whichever is lower; the 0.2% value applies here given a 50 mg dose).

## 8. Conclusion

Analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03 — reversed-phase HPLC-UV assay of losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets — has been validated against the acceptance criteria pre-approved in protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02, in accordance with ICH Q2(R2) (2023), USP <1225>, USP <621>, and the FDA July 2015 Guidance.
