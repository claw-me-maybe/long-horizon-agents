## 2. Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this report was to document the validation of analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, a reversed-phase HPLC-UV assay for quantitation of losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets, against the pre-approved acceptance criteria of validation protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02. The validated method is intended for commercial release testing and stability testing of finished-product tablets manufactured at Meridian Pharma Sciences (Cambridge, MA) in support of the Q3 2026 commercial launch and the FDA pre-approval inspection anticipated August 2026. Validation was executed in accordance with ICH Q2(R2) (2023), USP General Chapters <1225> and <621>, the FDA Guidance for Industry on Analytical Procedures and Methods Validation (July 2015), and 21 CFR 211.165(e) / 211.194(a)(2).

The validation parameters within scope were: system suitability, specificity (including forced-degradation DAD peak purity and mass balance), linearity, range, accuracy, repeatability, intermediate precision, robustness, solution stability, and filter compatibility. Inter-laboratory reproducibility was explicitly excluded as the method will be executed only at the Cambridge QC laboratory; justification is documented in §6.6. Detection and quantitation limits were excluded from formal validation as the method is a Category I assay for a major-component; indicative signal-to-noise at the 50% linearity level is reported for completeness.

## 5. Summary of Method

Losartan potassium was quantitated by reversed-phase HPLC with UV detection at 254 nm. Chromatographic separation was performed on a Waters XBridge C18 column (150 x 4.6 mm, 3.5 um) at 35 C under isocratic elution with 65:35 (v/v) 0.01 M potassium dihydrogen phosphate buffer (pH 3.0 +/- 0.05, adjusted with o-phosphoric acid) : acetonitrile, flow 1.0 mL/min, injection volume 10 uL, run time 10 min. Losartan eluted at approximately 4.20 min (k' approximately 3.5). Sample preparation comprised compositing 20 tablets, grinding, and extracting a portion equivalent to 50 mg losartan into 100 mL of diluent (mobile phase A : acetonitrile 50:50) with 15 min sonication, followed by 0.45 um PVDF filtration discarding the first 3 mL. Quantitation was performed against an external single-point standard at nominal 0.500 mg/mL (100% level), corrected for reference-standard potency (99.8% as-is, USP RS Lot R049F0). Full procedural detail resides in method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03; this report does not reproduce it.

### 6.2 Specificity

**Procedure executed.** Specificity was evaluated by (a) injection of diluent blank, placebo matrix, and composite excipient extract; (b) forced-degradation stress applied in parallel to drug substance and drug product per ICH Q1A(R2) and, for photolytic stress, per ICH Q1B Option 2; (c) DAD peak-purity evaluation of the losartan peak under every stress condition using Empower 3 peak-purity (spectral range 200-400 nm, reference wavelength 254 nm, noise-corrected purity threshold); (d) identification of the dominant base-hydrolysis degradant by co-injection and UV spectral match against Losartan Related Compound A USP RS (Lot F0J234); and (e) mass balance calculation as % assay of remaining losartan plus % total degradants (response-factor-normalized; correction factor 1.0 assumed for degradants per ICH Q3B(R2) §4.0).

**Pre-approved acceptance criteria** (MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 §7.2).
- No interfering peak co-eluting with losartan from diluent, placebo, or excipient extract (response < 0.2% of 100% standard at RT 4.20 +/- 0.10 min).
- Peak purity angle strictly less than peak purity threshold for losartan under every stress condition.
- At least one stress condition yields 5-20% loss of parent (meaningful degradation demonstrated).
- Mass balance 95-105% for every stress condition.
- The major base-hydrolysis degradant is identified where it exceeds the ICH Q3B(R2) identification threshold, which for a maximum daily dose of 50 mg is 0.2% (Table 1 of Q3B(R2)).

### 6.3 Linearity and Range

**Procedure executed.** Seven concentration levels (50, 75, 90, 100, 110, 125, 150% of nominal 0.500 mg/mL) were prepared gravimetrically in independent duplicates from USP RS Lot R049F0 (99.8% as-is, KF 0.42%, used as-is per protocol). Each preparation was injected in triplicate. Ordinary least-squares regression was performed using mean peak area per preparation (n = 14 regression points). Residual analysis, lack-of-fit F-test against triplicate pure-error, and confidence interval on the intercept were calculated in JMP 17.2. Range was confirmed by conjoint evaluation of linearity (here), accuracy (§6.4), and precision (§6.5-6.6) across 50-150%.

**Pre-approved acceptance criteria** (MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 §7.3).
- Correlation coefficient r^2 >= 0.999
- y-intercept <= 2.0% of the 100% level mean response (ICH Q2(R2) §2.3.2)
- 95% confidence interval on the intercept must include zero or otherwise not exceed the 2.0% threshold at its absolute bound
- Residuals distributed randomly about zero with no systematic pattern (visual plus runs test, p > 0.05)
- Lack-of-fit F-test p >= 0.05 against pure-error estimate
- Range confirmed as 50-150% of label claim

### 6.6 Precision — Intermediate Precision

**Procedure executed.** A fully crossed 2 x 2 x 2 factorial (2 analysts x 2 days x 2 instruments/columns) was executed on batch LP50-R-26-014. Analysts A. Chen and M. Okafor each prepared six independent preparations per day on two days, alternating between QC-HPLC-07 (column SN 0243) and QC-HPLC-11 (column SN 0251). All working standards were prepared independently each day. The total n = 24 assay values was decomposed by restricted-maximum-likelihood (REML) nested ANOVA with Analyst, Day(Analyst), and Instrument as random effects. Variance components were estimated and expressed both as SD (% LC) and as percent of total variance; the pooled RSD is also reported for continuity with protocol language. Analysis was performed in JMP 17.2.

## 8. Conclusion

Analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03 — reversed-phase HPLC-UV assay of losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets — has been validated against the acceptance criteria pre-approved in protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02, in accordance with ICH Q2(R2) (2023), USP <1225>, USP <621>, and the FDA July 2015 Guidance on Analytical Procedures and Methods Validation.

All ICH Q2(R2) characteristics required for a Category I assay — specificity (including DAD peak purity, mass balance 99.3-100.3%, and identification of the major base-hydrolysis degradant as losartan Related Compound A), linearity (r^2 = 0.99984; intercept 0.10% of 100% response), range (50-150%), accuracy (overall recovery 99.7%, n=9), repeatability (RSD 0.25%, n=6), intermediate precision (REML-nested pooled RSD 0.38% with no single between-group variance component exceeding 28.5% of total variance), and robustness (2^(5-2) fractional factorial; no factor critical at the 1.0% threshold) — passed their pre-stated acceptance criteria. Supporting characteristics (system suitability, solution stability, filter compatibility) also passed.

The method is validated and released for use in commercial release testing and stability testing of losartan potassium 50 mg film-coated tablets manufactured at Meridian Pharma Sciences, Cambridge, MA.

**Limitations and out-of-scope items.**
- The method has not been validated for content uniformity testing at the single-tablet level; that parameter is addressed under separate protocol MV-LP50-002.
- Inter-laboratory reproducibility is not addressed; any future site transfer shall trigger a transfer protocol per SOP QA-052.
- Detection and quantitation limits are not formally validated as the method is a Category I assay; indicative S/N 312:1 at the 50% linearity level is reported.
- Routine use of nylon 0.45 um filters is not authorized; PVDF is specified per §6.9.
