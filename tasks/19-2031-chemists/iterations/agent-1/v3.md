## 2. Purpose and Scope

This report documents the formal validation of analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, a reversed-phase HPLC assay with UV detection, against the pre-approved protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02. The method is intended for assay (content) determination of losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets in support of commercial release and ICH Q1A(R2) stability studies. Validation addresses the ICH Q2(R2) validation characteristics required for an assay (Category I per USP <1225>): specificity, linearity, range, accuracy, precision (repeatability and intermediate precision), and robustness. Solution stability and filter compatibility are reported as prerequisites for routine QC use; LOD/LOQ are reported for information only. This report will be filed with Module 3.2.P.5.3 of the Common Technical Document and is intended as the primary analytical-validation exhibit at the pre-approval inspection scheduled for August 2026.

**Exclusions.** This report does not validate the method for content uniformity at the single-tablet level (addressed by AM-LP50-008), for ICH Q3B(R2) related-substance quantitation at the reporting threshold (addressed by AM-LP50-009), or for cleaning-verification use (addressed by AM-LP50-014).

## 5. Summary of Method

Losartan potassium is determined by reversed-phase HPLC-UV on a Waters XBridge C18 column (150 x 4.6 mm, 3.5 um). The mobile phase is isocratic, 65:35 (v/v) 0.01 M KH2PO4 buffer adjusted to pH 3.00 +/- 0.05 with o-phosphoric acid (A) and acetonitrile (B), delivered at 1.0 mL/min with column temperature 35 C and injection volume 10 uL. Detection is UV at 254 nm; run time is 10 min; losartan retention time is approximately 4.20 min (k' approximately 3.5). Sample preparation consists of a 20-tablet composite ground to fine powder; a portion equivalent to 50 mg of losartan is transferred to a 100 mL volumetric flask, dissolved in diluent (mobile phase A : ACN 50:50, v/v) with 15 min ultrasonic extraction, made to volume, and filtered through 0.45 um PVDF with the first 3 mL discarded. The assay working concentration is 0.500 mg/mL losartan. Full procedural detail, reagent preparation, and calculation formulae are given in AM-LP50-007 Rev 03.

### 6.2 Specificity (ICH Q2(R2) Sec. 1.1)

**Procedure executed.** Specificity was established in four components, executed on QC-HPLC-07 with DAD (G7117B) across 210-400 nm:

1. **Blank and placebo.** Diluent blank and placebo solution (excipient matrix at 130.0 mg per 100 mL, representing the non-drug content of one 50 mg tablet: microcrystalline cellulose, pregelatinized starch, lactose monohydrate, magnesium stearate, Opadry II 85F18422 film coat) were injected in triplicate.
2. **Related-substance spiking.** Losartan Related Compound A (USP RS Lot F0J234) was spiked into a 100% working sample at 0.15% w/w (relative to losartan) to confirm chromatographic resolution.
3. **Forced-degradation per ICH Q1A(R2) / Q1B.** Stress conditions: 1 N HCl, 60 C, 2 h (acid hydrolysis, neutralized pre-injection); 1 N NaOH, 60 C, 2 h (base hydrolysis, neutralized); 3% H2O2, ambient, 2 h (oxidative); 80 C dry, 48 h (thermal); ICH Option 2 photostability (1.2 million lux-hours visible plus 200 W.h/m^2 UV-A). A dark control was run alongside the photostability sample per Q1B.
4. **Peak purity.** The purity angle / purity threshold test (Empower Peak Purity algorithm, SOP QC-033) was applied to the losartan peak in every stress sample. Spectral match factors (SMF) were computed against an in-sequence reference spectrum.

Mass balance was computed as (% parent remaining) + (% total related substances), each quantified against a freshly prepared losartan working standard. Response factors (RRF = 1.0) were assumed for unidentified degradants, which is conservative for mass-balance accounting.

**Acceptance criteria (MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 Section 7.2).** (i) Response at t_R(losartan) from diluent and placebo < 0.05% of the 100% standard response; (ii) peak purity angle < purity threshold for losartan in all stress samples; (iii) mass balance 95.0-105.0%; (iv) USP resolution between losartan and any adjacent peak >= 2.0; (v) at least one stress condition produces 5-20% parent loss; (vi) the major degradant above the ICH Q3B(R2) identification threshold for a 50 mg maximum daily dose (0.2%) is identified.

### 6.3 Linearity and Range (ICH Q2(R2) Sec. 1.2-1.3)

**Procedure executed.** Six concentration levels (50, 70, 100, 120, 140, 150% of the 0.500 mg/mL working concentration) were prepared by independent weighings from USP Losartan Potassium RS Lot R049F0 (99.8% as-is, water-corrected) and serial volumetric dilution. Each level was injected in triplicate (n = 18 total) on QC-HPLC-07, with a bracketing SST meeting criteria. Unweighted least-squares linear regression was performed on mean peak area (y) versus nominal concentration (x, mg/mL). Standardized residuals were calculated and plotted; a lack-of-fit F test was performed against pure-error variance (triplicate injections as replicate y for each x).

**Acceptance criteria (MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 Section 7.3).** r^2 >= 0.9990; y-intercept <= 2.0% of the mean response at 100%; 95% confidence interval on the intercept includes zero; residuals randomly distributed (no trend, curvature, or heteroscedasticity); lack-of-fit F test not significant at alpha = 0.05.

**Conclusion.** Linearity and range criteria were met: r^2 = 0.9998 (>= 0.9990); intercept = 0.19% of 100% response (<= 2.0%); 95% CI on intercept includes zero; residuals show no systematic pattern; lack-of-fit not significant. **PASS.**

## 7. Deviations and Investigations

All validation work was executed under SOP QA-021. Two deviations were logged and fully investigated; each is linked below to its investigation and disposition. No OOS or OOT events were recorded.

- **DEV-2026-087 / INV-2026-087.** Column temperature on QC-HPLC-11 excursed to 37 C for 14 min during the Day-2 intermediate precision run on 16 March 2026 (HVAC-driven TCC setpoint excursion). Two sample injections and one SST injection fell within the excursion window. Root cause (INV-2026-087, closed 21 March 2026): Lab 2E air-handler dew-point setpoint drift from 18.5 to 22.1 C during a planned BMS upgrade. Impact: robustness screen (Section 6.7) demonstrates assay insensitivity to T +/- 5 C; affected cell-mean assay was 99.70% LC versus the grand mean of 99.93% LC, a deviation well within the within-cell SD. Disposition: data retained. CAPA: Facilities opened FAC-CAPA-2026-011 to add an out-of-range BMS alarm on Lab 2E.
- **DEV-2026-092 / INV-2026-092.** One chromatogram (Sequence SEQ-MV-04, Vial 12) of the linearity set showed a baseline-integration anomaly attributed to a transient DAD lamp-current fluctuation (detector log event at 14:32, 13 March 2026). Reintegration per SOP QC-025 by a second reviewer yielded a peak area within 0.04% of the original automated integration (SOP QC-025 threshold for re-analysis is 0.5%). Root cause (INV-2026-092, closed 15 March 2026): transient lamp aging; preventive replacement scheduled under QC-PM-2026-34. Impact: none; the reintegrated result is within 0.04% of the original, an order of magnitude below the SOP threshold. Disposition: reintegrated result retained; the original value, the reintegration, and the justification are preserved in the Empower audit trail.
