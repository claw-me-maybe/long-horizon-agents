## 2. Purpose and Scope

This report documents the full validation of analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, a reversed-phase HPLC assay with UV detection, against protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02. The method is intended for assay (content) testing of losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets in support of commercial release and ICH Q1A(R2) stability studies. This validation addresses the ICH Q2(R2) validation characteristics required for a Category I assay procedure: specificity, linearity, range, accuracy, precision (repeatability and intermediate precision), and robustness; solution stability and filter compatibility are also reported as prerequisites for routine QC use. The report will be filed with CTD Module 3.2.P.5.3 and is intended to serve as the primary analytical-validation exhibit at the PAI scheduled for August 2026.

The method is not validated by this report for content uniformity at the single-tablet level, for related-substance quantitation at the ICH Q3B reporting threshold, or for cleaning verification; those intended uses are addressed under AM-LP50-008 and AM-LP50-009 respectively.

## 5. Summary of Method

Losartan potassium is quantified by reversed-phase HPLC-UV on a Waters XBridge C18 column (150 x 4.6 mm, 3.5 um). Mobile phase A is 0.01 M KH2PO4 adjusted to pH 3.00 +/- 0.05 with o-phosphoric acid; mobile phase B is acetonitrile. The system is operated isocratically at 65:35 A:B, 1.0 mL/min, 35 C column temperature, 10 uL injection volume, and 254 nm UV detection. Losartan elutes at approximately 4.20 min (k' approximately 3.5), with total run time of 10 min. Sample preparation consists of compositing 20 tablets, grinding, weighing a portion equivalent to 50 mg losartan into a 100 mL volumetric, extracting into diluent (mobile phase A : ACN, 50:50) with 15 min sonication, volumetric dilution, and filtration through 0.45 um PVDF (first 3 mL discarded). The working concentration of the assay standard and sample is 0.50 mg/mL. Full procedural detail, reagent preparation, and calculation formulae are specified in AM-LP50-007 Rev 03.

### 6.2 Specificity (ICH Q2(R2) Section 1.1)

**Procedure executed.** Specificity was demonstrated in four parts: (i) diluent blank; (ii) placebo (tablet excipient matrix at 130 mg per 100 mL, equivalent to the excipient load of one 50 mg tablet); (iii) forced-degradation samples prepared per ICH Q1A(R2); and (iv) spiking of Losartan Related Compound A (USP RS Lot F0J234) at 0.15% of the assay level to confirm chromatographic resolution. Peak purity was evaluated for the losartan peak on the DAD (QC-HPLC-07) across 210-400 nm using the Empower Peak Purity algorithm with the purity-angle / purity-threshold test as described in SOP QC-033.

Stress conditions applied to a composite tablet preparation at the assay working concentration were: 1 N HCl, 60 C, 2 h (neutralized prior to injection); 1 N NaOH, 60 C, 2 h (neutralized); 3% H2O2, ambient, 2 h; 80 C dry heat, 48 h; ICH Option 2 photostability (1.2 million lux-hours visible plus 200 Wh/m^2 UV). Samples were diluted to the working concentration prior to analysis. Mass balance was calculated as (% parent + % total related substances), with related substances quantified against the losartan standard response corrected by published relative response factors where available (RRF = 1.0 assumed for unknowns, a conservative approach).

**Acceptance criteria (MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 Section 7.2).** (i) Diluent and placebo responses at t_R(losartan) < 0.05% of 100% standard response. (ii) Peak purity angle < purity threshold for the losartan peak under each stress condition. (iii) Mass balance 95.0-105.0%. (iv) Resolution of losartan from the nearest impurity peak >= 2.0.

### 6.3 Linearity and Range (ICH Q2(R2) Section 1.2)

**Procedure executed.** A common stock solution of USP Losartan Potassium RS (Lot R049F0, 99.8% as-is, water-corrected) was serially diluted to five concentration levels nominally equal to 50, 75, 100, 125, and 150% of the assay working concentration (0.500 mg/mL). Each level was injected in triplicate on QC-HPLC-07. Least-squares regression of mean peak area (y) against nominal concentration (x, mg/mL) was performed with unweighted points. Residuals were plotted and inspected for systematic trend; the y-intercept was expressed as a percentage of the mean response at 100%.

**Acceptance criteria (MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 Section 7.3).** r^2 >= 0.9990; y-intercept <= 2.0% of 100% response; residuals randomly scattered about zero with no systematic curvature.

## 7. Deviations and Investigations

Two deviations were logged under SOP QA-021 during execution of the validation; each was investigated and closed prior to report issuance.

- **DEV-2026-087 / INV-2026-087.** Column temperature on QC-HPLC-11 excursed to 37 C for 14 min during the Day-2 intermediate precision run, triggered by an HVAC disturbance in Lab 2E. Root cause: facility air-handler dew-point setpoint drift. Impact: two injections were completed within the excursion window; results are within specification and within one SD of the cell mean. The robustness screen (Section 6.7) demonstrates insensitivity of the assay to T +/- 5 C, bounding the excursion. Disposition: data retained; CAPA opened with Facilities. Closure: 21 March 2026.
- **DEV-2026-092 / INV-2026-092.** One injection (Sequence SEQ-MV-04, Vial 12) in the linearity sequence exhibited a baseline-integration anomaly attributed to a transient detector lamp flicker. The injection was reintegrated per SOP QC-025 by the secondary reviewer; the reintegrated area differs from the original by 0.04%, within the SOP-specified 0.5% threshold. Impact: none. Disposition: reintegrated result retained; original value and reintegration justification captured in the Empower audit trail. Closure: 15 March 2026.

No other deviations or OOT / OOS results were recorded during validation execution.

## 8. Conclusion

Analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, a reversed-phase HPLC assay with UV detection for losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets, was validated against protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 and satisfies every pre-approved acceptance criterion for a Category I assay under ICH Q2(R2): system suitability, specificity (including forced-degradation peak-purity and identification of the major base-hydrolysis degradant as Losartan Related Compound A), linearity and range (50-150% of label claim), accuracy (spiked-placebo recovery at three levels), precision (repeatability n = 6 and nested intermediate precision with full variance decomposition), robustness (Plackett-Burman 8-run design), solution stability (48 h at both RT and refrigerated), and filter compatibility (PVDF 0.45 um).

The method is validated and declared fit for its intended use: release and ICH Q1A(R2) stability testing of commercial Losartan Potassium 50 mg Film-Coated Tablets manufactured under project LP-50-CMC-412. The method is not validated by this report for single-tablet content uniformity (see AM-LP50-008), for ICH Q3B(R2) related-substance quantitation (see AM-LP50-009), or for cleaning-verification use.
