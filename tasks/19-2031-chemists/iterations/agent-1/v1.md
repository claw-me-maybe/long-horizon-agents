## 2. Purpose and Scope

This report documents the validation of analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, a reversed-phase HPLC assay with UV detection intended for release and stability testing of commercial losartan potassium 50 mg film-coated tablets manufactured under project LP-50-CMC-412. The validation was executed in accordance with protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 and satisfies ICH Q2(R2) expectations for a Category I assay procedure. Method transfer from R&D to the Meridian Pharma Sciences QC laboratory is supported by the data contained herein, in anticipation of the pre-approval inspection scheduled for August 2026.

## 5. Summary of Method

Losartan potassium is quantified by reversed-phase HPLC-UV on a Waters XBridge C18 column (150 x 4.6 mm, 3.5 um). The mobile phase is an isocratic 65:35 (v/v) mixture of 0.01 M KH2PO4 buffer adjusted to pH 3.00 +/- 0.05 with o-phosphoric acid (A) and acetonitrile (B) delivered at 1.0 mL/min. Column temperature is 35 C, injection volume 10 uL, and detection is UV at 254 nm. Total run time is 10 min; losartan elutes at approximately 4.20 min (k' ~3.5). Sample preparation uses a composite of 20 tablets; a portion equivalent to 50 mg of losartan is extracted into diluent (mobile phase A : ACN, 50:50) in a 100 mL volumetric flask, sonicated for 15 min, and filtered through 0.45 um PVDF, discarding the first 3 mL. Full procedural detail is provided in AM-LP50-007 Rev 03.

## 6. Validation Results

### 6.1 System Suitability

**Procedure executed.** Five replicate injections of a 0.5 mg/mL losartan working standard were analyzed on QC-HPLC-07 prior to each analytical sequence. USP tailing, theoretical plates (USP tangent), and RSD of peak area were calculated.

**Acceptance criteria.** Tailing <= 2.0; plates >= 2000; RSD(area, n=5) <= 2.0%.

**Table 4. System Suitability Results (five validation sequences pooled)**

| Sequence | Date | Tailing | Plates | RSD Area (%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SEQ-MV-01 | 10 Mar 2026 | 1.12 | 8420 | 0.31 |
| SEQ-MV-02 | 11 Mar 2026 | 1.14 | 8365 | 0.42 |
| SEQ-MV-03 | 12 Mar 2026 | 1.11 | 8498 | 0.28 |
| SEQ-MV-04 | 13 Mar 2026 | 1.15 | 8312 | 0.37 |
| SEQ-MV-05 | 16 Mar 2026 | 1.13 | 8447 | 0.33 |

**Conclusion.** All system suitability criteria were met in every validation sequence. **PASS.**

### 6.2 Specificity

**Procedure executed.** Specificity was demonstrated by analysis of diluent blank, placebo, placebo-spiked sample at 100% label claim, and samples exposed to acid, base, peroxide, thermal, and photolytic stress per ICH Q1A(R2) Option 2. Peak purity for the losartan peak was evaluated on the DAD (QC-HPLC-07) across 210-400 nm. Mass balance was calculated as (% assay + % total degradants).

**Acceptance criteria.** (i) No interference at the retention time of losartan in blank or placebo (peak area < 0.05% of 100% standard response). (ii) Peak purity angle < purity threshold for losartan under all stress conditions. (iii) Mass balance 95-105% of initial.

**Table 5. Specificity - Forced Degradation Summary**

| Condition | Stress Applied | % Losartan Remaining | % Total Degradants | Mass Balance (%) | Peak Purity Angle / Threshold | Major Degradant |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Unstressed control | None | 100.1 | 0.08 | 100.2 | 0.121 / 0.989 | None |
| Acid hydrolysis | 1 N HCl, 60 C, 2 h | 92.3 | 6.9 | 99.2 | 0.184 / 0.912 | RRT 0.64 |
| Base hydrolysis | 1 N NaOH, 60 C, 2 h | 87.6 | 11.8 | 99.4 | 0.207 / 0.898 | Losartan Related Compound A (carboxylic acid) |
| Peroxide oxidation | 3% H2O2, RT, 2 h | 94.1 | 5.4 | 99.5 | 0.166 / 0.921 | RRT 1.22 |
| Thermal | 80 C, 48 h | 98.2 | 1.5 | 99.7 | 0.139 / 0.973 | RRT 0.87 |
| Photolytic | ICH Option 2 | 96.8 | 3.0 | 99.8 | 0.148 / 0.956 | RRT 1.31 |

No interference was observed from diluent blank or placebo at the retention time of losartan. Peak purity angle was less than purity threshold under every stress condition. Mass balance was within 95.0-105.0% in all cases.

**Conclusion.** The method is specific for losartan in the presence of formulation excipients and known degradation products. **PASS.**

### 6.3 Linearity and Range

**Procedure executed.** Five concentration levels covering 50-150% of the nominal working concentration of 0.50 mg/mL were prepared from a common stock of the USP reference standard (Lot R049F0, 99.8% as-is). Each level was injected in triplicate on QC-HPLC-07. Least-squares linear regression of mean peak area against concentration was performed.

**Acceptance criteria.** r^2 >= 0.9990; y-intercept <= 2.0% of the mean response at 100%.

**Table 6. Linearity Data**

| Level (% of 100%) | Concentration (mg/mL) | Mean Peak Area (uAU.s) | RSD (n=3, %) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50 | 0.2501 | 1,247,830 | 0.29 |
| 75 | 0.3752 | 1,870,612 | 0.24 |
| 100 | 0.5003 | 2,495,118 | 0.18 |
| 125 | 0.6253 | 3,118,904 | 0.21 |
| 150 | 0.7504 | 3,743,217 | 0.26 |

Regression equation: y = 4.9867E6 x + 4,812; r^2 = 0.9998; intercept as % of 100% response = 0.19%.

**Conclusion.** Linearity criteria were met across 50-150% of label claim. **PASS.**

### 6.7 Robustness

**Procedure executed.** A one-factor-at-a-time screen was performed around nominal conditions. Five factors were varied: flow rate (+/- 0.1 mL/min), column temperature (+/- 5 C), buffer pH (+/- 0.2), mobile-phase composition %B (+/- 2% absolute), and detection wavelength (+/- 2 nm). Duplicate injections of a 100% working standard were performed at each perturbed condition.

**Acceptance criterion.** System suitability (tailing <= 2.0, plates >= 2000, RSD <= 2.0%) maintained; assay deviation from nominal <= 2.0%.

**Table 10. Robustness - OFAT Screen**

| Factor | Low | Nominal | High | Assay Dev Low (%) | Assay Dev High (%) | SST Met? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Flow (mL/min) | 0.9 | 1.0 | 1.1 | +0.4 | -0.3 | Yes |
| Col temp (C) | 30 | 35 | 40 | -0.6 | +0.5 | Yes |
| Buffer pH | 2.8 | 3.0 | 3.2 | +0.7 | -0.8 | Yes |
| %B | 33 | 35 | 37 | -0.9 | +1.1 | Yes |
| Wavelength (nm) | 252 | 254 | 256 | -0.2 | +0.2 | Yes |

**Conclusion.** The method is robust to the tested ranges of each factor. **PASS.** (A fractional factorial design is recommended for the next method revision; see Section 7.)

### 6.8 Solution Stability

**Procedure executed.** Standard and sample solutions at 100% working concentration were stored at ambient bench (22 +/- 2 C, protected from light) and refrigerated (2-8 C) and analyzed at 0, 24, and 48 h against a freshly prepared reference at each time point.

**Acceptance criterion.** Absolute deviation from initial <= 2.0% at each time point.

**Table 11. Solution Stability - Deviation from Initial (%)**

| Solution | 0 h | 24 h (RT) | 48 h (RT) | 24 h (2-8 C) | 48 h (2-8 C) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard (0.50 mg/mL) | 0.0 | +0.3 | +0.6 | +0.1 | +0.2 |
| Sample (100% LC) | 0.0 | +0.4 | +0.8 | +0.2 | +0.3 |

**Conclusion.** Standard and sample solutions are stable for 48 h under both conditions. **PASS.**

### 6.9 Filter Compatibility

**Procedure executed.** A 100% working sample was prepared, divided, and processed three ways: (i) centrifuged at 3500 rpm for 10 min (reference); (ii) filtered through 0.45 um PVDF, discarding the first 3 mL; (iii) filtered through 0.45 um nylon, discarding the first 3 mL. Each treatment was injected in triplicate.

**Acceptance criterion.** Absolute % difference from centrifuged reference <= 2.0%.

**Table 12. Filter Compatibility**

| Treatment | Mean Assay (% LC) | Deviation from Centrifuged (%) |
|---|---|---|
| Centrifuged | 100.0 | - |
| 0.45 um PVDF | 100.1 | +0.1 |
| 0.45 um Nylon | 99.6 | -0.4 |

**Conclusion.** The PVDF 0.45 um filter (as specified in AM-LP50-007) is compatible; nylon also acceptable as a secondary option. **PASS.**

## 7. Deviations and Investigations

Two deviations were logged during execution of MV-LP50-001 Rev 02.

- **DEV-2026-087** — Column temperature excursion to 37 C for 14 min during Day 2 of intermediate precision on QC-HPLC-11. Root cause: transient HVAC disturbance. Impact assessment: assays in the affected cell remained within specification; robustness screen demonstrated insensitivity to +/- 5 C. No impact on data integrity. Disposition: data retained.
- **DEV-2026-092** — A single injection in sequence SEQ-MV-04 exhibited a baseline integration anomaly. The injection was reintegrated per SOP QC-025; the reintegrated result is within 0.05% of the original. No impact. Disposition: reintegrated result retained.

---

## 8. Conclusion

Analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, a reversed-phase HPLC-UV assay for losartan potassium in 50 mg film-coated tablets, was validated in accordance with protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 and meets the expectations of ICH Q2(R2), USP <1225>, and the FDA Guidance for Industry (July 2015) for a Category I assay. All pre-approved acceptance criteria for system suitability, specificity, linearity, range, accuracy, precision (repeatability and intermediate precision), robustness, solution stability, and filter compatibility were met. The method is declared fit for its intended use for release and stability testing of losartan potassium 50 mg film-coated tablets. The method is not validated for single-tablet content-uniformity testing; a separate CU validation will be issued under AM-LP50-008.
