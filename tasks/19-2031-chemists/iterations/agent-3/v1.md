## 1. Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this report was to document the validation of analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03, a reversed-phase HPLC-UV assay for the quantitative determination of losartan potassium in film-coated tablets at a label claim of 50 mg. The method is intended for release and stability testing of commercial 50 mg losartan potassium tablets manufactured under project LP-50-CMC-412. Validation was executed in accordance with protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 and assessed against the expectations of ICH Q2(R2) for a Category I assay.

## 4. Summary of Method

Twenty tablets were composited, ground, and an aliquot equivalent to 50 mg of losartan potassium was transferred to a 100 mL volumetric flask and dissolved in diluent (Mobile Phase A : acetonitrile, 50:50 v/v) with 15 min sonication. The preparation was filtered through a 0.45 um PVDF membrane, discarding the first 3 mL. Separation was achieved on a Waters XBridge C18 (150 x 4.6 mm, 3.5 um) column at 35 C using isocratic elution with 0.01 M KH2PO4 (pH 3.0) and acetonitrile (65:35 v/v) at 1.0 mL/min, with UV detection at 254 nm and 10 uL injections. Total run time was 10 min; losartan eluted at approximately 4.2 min (k' approximately 3.5). Full details are provided in AM-LP50-007 Rev 03.

## 6. Validation Results

### 6.1 System Suitability

**Procedure executed:** Five replicate injections of the 100% working standard (0.500 mg/mL losartan potassium, corrected for as-is potency) were performed at the start of each analytical session.

**Acceptance criteria (per protocol):** USP tailing <= 2.0; theoretical plates (USP) >= 2000; RSD of peak area for 5 replicate standard injections <= 2.0%.

**Table 3. System Suitability Results (Qualification Run, 12 March 2026)**

| Parameter | Result | Criterion | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| USP tailing factor | 1.18 | <= 2.0 | Pass |
| Theoretical plates (USP) | 8420 | >= 2000 | Pass |
| Retention time, losartan (min) | 4.21 | Report | Pass |
| Area RSD, n=5 | 0.42% | <= 2.0% | Pass |

**Conclusion:** System suitability criteria were met.

---

### 6.2 Specificity

**Procedure executed:** Specificity was demonstrated by (i) injection of diluent blank and placebo, (ii) injection of losartan standard, losartan Related Compound A standard, and a spiked mixture, and (iii) forced degradation of drug product under acid, base, peroxide, thermal, and photolytic stress per ICH Q1A(R2). Peak purity of losartan was assessed using the DAD (200-400 nm).

**Acceptance criteria:** No interfering peak from diluent or placebo at the retention time of losartan (tolerance +/- 0.1 min). Losartan peak purity angle less than purity threshold under each stress condition. Mass balance (% assay + % total degradants) 95-105%.

**Table 4. Forced Degradation Summary**

| Stress | Condition | % Assay Remaining | % Total Degradants | Mass Balance (%) | Peak Purity (angle / threshold) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Acid | 1 N HCl, 60 C, 2 h | 91.4 | 7.2 | 98.6 | 0.412 / 1.008, pass |
| Base | 1 N NaOH, 60 C, 2 h | 84.7 | 14.1 | 98.8 | 0.528 / 1.041, pass |
| Peroxide | 3% H2O2, RT, 2 h | 93.8 | 5.1 | 98.9 | 0.389 / 0.997, pass |
| Thermal | 80 C, 48 h | 98.2 | 1.1 | 99.3 | 0.301 / 0.962, pass |
| Photolytic | ICH Option 2 | 96.6 | 2.4 | 99.0 | 0.341 / 0.977, pass |

**Conclusion:** The method is specific for losartan potassium in the presence of excipients and degradation products.

### 6.3 Linearity and Range

**Acceptance criteria:** r^2 >= 0.999; y-intercept <= 2.0% of the 100% response.

**Table 5. Linearity**

| 100 | 0.5000 | 5,019,880 | 100.0 |
| 125 | 0.6250 | 6,274,410 | 125.1 |

### 6.9 Filter Compatibility

**Procedure executed:** A single sample preparation was split into three portions: centrifuged (4000 rpm, 5 min) as the untreated reference, filtered through 0.45 um PVDF, and filtered through 0.45 um nylon. The first 3 mL were discarded from each filter.

**Acceptance criteria:** Absolute difference between filtered and centrifuged reference <= 2.0%.

**Table 11. Filter Compatibility**

| Treatment | Assay (%) | Delta vs centrifuged (%) |
|---|---|---|
| Centrifuged | 100.0 | - |
| PVDF 0.45 um | 99.9 | -0.1 |
| Nylon 0.45 um | 98.4 | -1.6 |

**Conclusion:** PVDF 0.45 um is approved for routine use. Nylon is not approved.

## 7. Deviations and Investigations

- **DEV-2026-087:** Column oven excursion to 37 C for 14 min during Day 2 intermediate precision. Impact assessed via robustness data; no adverse effect on assay. Disposition: accept.
- **DEV-2026-092:** Single injection integration anomaly reintegrated per SOP QC-025. No change to overall result. Disposition: accept.

---

## 8. Conclusion

Analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03 was validated per protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 and meets the expectations of ICH Q2(R2) for a Category I assay in support of release and stability testing of losartan potassium 50 mg film-coated tablets. The method is approved for routine use. This validation does not cover content uniformity at the single-tablet level.
