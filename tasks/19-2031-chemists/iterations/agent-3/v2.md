## 4. Summary of Method

Twenty intact film-coated tablets were composited and ground to a homogeneous powder. A portion equivalent to 50 mg losartan potassium was transferred to a 100 mL Class A volumetric flask, approximately 60 mL of diluent (Mobile Phase A : acetonitrile, 50:50 v/v) was added, the flask was sonicated for 15 min, diluted to volume, and mixed. An aliquot was filtered through a 0.45 um PVDF membrane, discarding the first 3 mL. Separation was achieved on a Waters XBridge C18 column (150 x 4.6 mm, 3.5 um) at 35 C with isocratic elution using Mobile Phase A (0.01 M KH2PO4, pH 3.00 +/- 0.05) and Mobile Phase B (acetonitrile) at 65:35 v/v, flow 1.0 mL/min, injection volume 10 uL, UV detection at 254 nm, and a 10 min run time. Losartan eluted at approximately 4.21 min (k' approximately 3.5). The full procedure is provided in AM-LP50-007 Rev 03.

### 6.2 Specificity

**Procedure executed:** Specificity was demonstrated by (i) blank diluent and placebo injections, (ii) resolution of losartan from Related Compound A (losartan carboxylic acid impurity), and (iii) forced-degradation of drug product under acid, base, peroxide, thermal, and photolytic stress per ICH Q1A(R2). Peak purity of the losartan peak was evaluated using the DAD over 200-400 nm; purity angle and purity threshold were computed in Empower PDA Review. Mass balance was computed as (% assay remaining + % total degradants by area normalization at 254 nm relative to an unstressed control).

**Acceptance criteria:**
- No interfering peak from diluent or placebo at the losartan retention window (4.21 +/- 0.10 min); peak-to-peak noise only.
- Resolution between losartan and Related Compound A Rs >= 2.0.
- Losartan peak purity angle < purity threshold under each stress condition.
- Mass balance 95.0-105.0% under each stress condition.
- At least one stress condition produces meaningful degradation (5-20% loss of parent).

## 7. Deviations and Investigations

- **DEV-2026-087 / INV-2026-087:** During the second day of the intermediate precision study on QC-HPLC-07, the column oven sensor recorded an excursion to 37 C for 14 min against a setpoint of 35 C. The robustness study (Section 6.7) demonstrated assay recovery within 98.0-102.0% at 40 C, which exceeds the observed excursion. No impact to data integrity was identified. Disposition: ACCEPT with no retest required. (Att. 8, p. 2).
- **DEV-2026-092 / INV-2026-092:** A single injection in the repeatability sequence exhibited an integration artifact caused by a stray baseline event. The chromatogram was reintegrated by Analyst A. Chen using the default apex-to-valley method per SOP QC-025 Rev 05, with supervisor review and CDS audit-trail capture. The reintegrated assay value (100.1%) differs from the original automated result by 0.08%. Disposition: ACCEPT, reintegrated value used in all downstream calculations. (Att. 8, p. 4).

## 8. Conclusion

Analytical method AM-LP50-007 Rev 03 has been validated per protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02 and satisfies every ICH Q2(R2) validation characteristic applicable to a Category I assay (specificity, linearity, range, accuracy, repeatability, intermediate precision, and robustness). All pre-approved acceptance criteria were met; both logged deviations were investigated (INV-2026-087, INV-2026-092) and dispositioned without impact to the validation outcome. The method is approved for routine release and stability testing of losartan potassium 50 mg film-coated tablets manufactured under project LP-50-CMC-412.

**Limitations:** This validation covers assay at a single strength (50 mg, label claim 0.500 mg/mL working concentration) across a 50-150% range. It does not qualify the method for content uniformity at the single-tablet level, for related-substance quantitation, or for strengths other than 50 mg.
