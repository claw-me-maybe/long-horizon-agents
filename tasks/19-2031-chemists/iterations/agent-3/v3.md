## 1. Purpose and Scope

This report documents the formal validation of analytical method AM-LP50-007 Revision 03, a reversed-phase isocratic HPLC-UV procedure for the quantitative determination of losartan potassium in film-coated tablets at a label claim of 50 mg per tablet. The validated method shall be used for (i) release testing per 21 CFR 211.165(e) and (ii) stability testing per 21 CFR 211.166, of commercial losartan potassium 50 mg tablets manufactured at Meridian Pharma Sciences under project LP-50-CMC-412. The report is intended for filing in Module 3.2.P.5.3 of the CTD and for inspection review during the FDA pre-approval inspection anticipated in August 2026.

Validation activities were executed in accordance with pre-approved protocol MV-LP50-001 Revision 02 under the governance of SOP QA-040 Revision 11, and the validation characteristics evaluated conform to ICH Q2(R2) (adopted November 2023) for a Category I (assay) analytical procedure. ## 4. Summary of Method

Twenty intact film-coated tablets were composited and ground to a homogeneous powder. A portion equivalent to 50 mg losartan potassium was transferred to a 100 mL Class A volumetric flask, approximately 60 mL of diluent (Mobile Phase A : acetonitrile, 50:50 v/v) was added, the flask was sonicated for 15 min, diluted to volume, mixed, and filtered through a 0.45 um PVDF membrane with the first 3 mL discarded. Separation was achieved on a Waters XBridge C18 column (150 x 4.6 mm, 3.5 um) at 35 +/- 1 C with isocratic elution using Mobile Phase A (0.01 M KH2PO4, pH 3.00 +/- 0.05, prepared with o-phosphoric acid) and Mobile Phase B (acetonitrile) at 65:35 v/v, flow 1.0 mL/min, injection volume 10 uL, UV detection at 254 nm, run time 10 min. Losartan eluted at approximately 4.21 min (k' approximately 3.5). Full operating instructions are provided in AM-LP50-007 Revision 03.

**Assay calculation formula (reference) with explicit as-is potency flow-through:**

Assay (% LC) = (A_sample / A_std) x (C_std / C_sample) x (P_std / 100) x (1 / LC) x 100%

where A = peak area, C_std = nominal standard concentration (mg/mL), C_sample = nominal sample concentration (mg/mL), P_std = 99.8% (as-is potency of USP RS Lot R049F0; no further moisture correction, since the standard is used as-is), and LC = 50.0 mg / tablet equivalent. The 99.8% potency is applied consistently across all assay, accuracy, and recovery calculations in this report; see Att. 2 for the worked example.

### 6.2 Specificity

**Procedure executed:** Specificity was demonstrated through four complementary elements: (a) diluent blank and placebo matrix injections to exclude interference at the losartan retention window; (b) injection of a spiked standard mixture of losartan and Related Compound A (losartan carboxylic acid, USP RS Lot F0J234) to confirm chromatographic resolution; (c) forced degradation of drug product under acid, base, peroxide, thermal, and photolytic stress per ICH Q1A(R2) and Q1B; and (d) DAD peak-purity evaluation of losartan in each stressed sample using Empower PDA Review with a 200-400 nm spectral range, a 2.5-nm threshold bandwidth, solvent-corrected baseline, and a threshold angle reflecting noise and solvent variability. Mass balance was computed as (% assay remaining + % total degradants by area normalization at 254 nm) vs an unstressed control.

Stress conditions were calibrated in a pre-study to target 5-20% loss of parent at base hydrolysis (the primary indicating condition) and <=10% under the other conditions to avoid secondary degradation (protocol MV-LP50-001 Rev 02, Section 5.2).

**Acceptance criteria:**
- No interfering peak from diluent or placebo at the losartan retention window 4.21 +/- 0.10 min (tolerance <= 0.05% of 100% response).
- USP resolution (Rs) between losartan and Related Compound A >= 2.0.
- Under each stress condition, the losartan peak purity angle shall be less than the purity threshold (Empower DAD, solvent-corrected, noise-corrected).
- Mass balance 95.0-105.0% under each stress condition.
- At least one stress condition shall produce 5-20% loss of parent.
- Major degradant under base hydrolysis shall be identified (coelution + spectral match) and assessed against the ICH Q3B(R2) identification threshold corresponding to the 50 mg daily dose (0.2% of drug substance).

**Interpretation of peak-purity results:** In all six chromatograms evaluated, the purity angle was less than the purity threshold and also less than the minimum threshold (noise + solvent correction), indicating that spectra across the losartan peak are statistically indistinguishable from the apex spectrum at 254 nm resolution. No spectral inhomogeneity across the losartan peak was detected under any stress condition; the losartan peak is demonstrated to be free of co-eluting degradants to the detection limit of the PDA.

**Identification of the major degradant under base hydrolysis:** The principal degradation peak eluted at 2.86 min, co-eluted with the authentic losartan Related Compound A USP RS (Lot F0J234) to within 0.02 min, and matched its UV spectrum (200-400 nm) with an Empower spectral match factor of 0.9997 (Empower PDA 20260314-AC-LP50-FD-BAS-ID, Att. 1, p. 16). The peak was therefore confirmed as the losartan carboxylic acid degradant. At a 50 mg daily dose, the ICH Q3B(R2) identification threshold is 0.2% of the drug substance; the observed 14.1% total degradants under forced stress greatly exceeds that threshold, and identification is substantiated by authentic reference standard coelution plus spectral match.

**Pass/Fail:** PASS. No interference from diluent or placebo was observed; resolution from Related Compound A (3.82) exceeded the >= 2.0 criterion; peak purity angle was less than purity threshold in all cases; mass balance remained within 95.0-105.0% across all five stress conditions; base hydrolysis produced 15.3% loss of parent (within the targeted 5-20%); and the major base-hydrolysis degradant was identified as losartan carboxylic acid against the ICH Q3B(R2) threshold.

### 6.3 Linearity and Range

## 7. Deviations and Investigations

- **DEV-2026-087 / INV-2026-087:** During Day 2 of the intermediate precision study on QC-HPLC-07, the column oven sensor recorded an excursion to 37 C for 14 min against a setpoint of 35 C. Investigation included review of the oven calibration record and cross-reference against the robustness study (Section 6.7, Runs 3, 4, 7, 8 at 40 C) which demonstrated assay recovery within 98.0-102.0% at +5 C above setpoint; the 37 C excursion is well within the validated robust range. Data integrity and product impact assessments concluded no adverse effect. Root cause: transient HVAC fluctuation; trending CAPA not required per SOP QC-033 Rev 03. **Disposition: ACCEPT, no retest required.** (Att. 8, pp. 2-3).
- **DEV-2026-092 / INV-2026-092:** A single injection in the repeatability sequence (Prep 4, Run 17) exhibited an integration artifact caused by a stray baseline event at 1.2 min, approximately 3 min pre-elution of losartan. The chromatogram was reintegrated by Analyst A. Chen using the default apex-to-valley method per SOP QC-025 Rev 05, with supervisor review (S. Delgado) and CDS audit-trail capture. Reintegrated assay value: 100.1%, differing from the original automated result (100.0%) by 0.08%. No other injection in the sequence required reintegration. **Disposition: ACCEPT, reintegrated value used in all downstream calculations.** (Att. 8, pp. 4-5).

No out-of-trend or out-of-specification results were obtained during validation. No retests were performed on validation samples.

## 8. Conclusion

Analytical method AM-LP50-007 Revision 03 has been validated in accordance with protocol MV-LP50-001 Revision 02 and satisfies every ICH Q2(R2) validation characteristic applicable to a Category I assay: specificity, linearity, range, accuracy, repeatability, intermediate precision, and robustness. All pre-approved acceptance criteria were met with margin. Specificity was demonstrated against blank, placebo, a co-eluting-threat impurity (Related Compound A), and five ICH-compliant forced-degradation stresses, with DAD peak purity (angle < threshold) confirmed under each condition and mass balance within 95.0-105.0%. Linearity satisfied r^2 >= 0.9990 over 50-150% of label claim with an intercept of 0.82% of the 100% response. Accuracy was demonstrated by spiked-placebo recovery at three levels in triplicate plus a blinded confirmatory run, with grand mean 99.84% (95% CI 99.71-99.97%). Repeatability RSD (0.26%) and intermediate precision overall RSD (0.29%, decomposed by nested REML ANOVA) were well below their respective 2.0% and 3.0% limits. Robustness, evaluated via a 2^(5-2) fractional factorial with center points, identified no critical main effect on assay recovery within the tested ranges. Both protocol deviations (DEV-2026-087 and DEV-2026-092) were formally investigated (INV-2026-087, INV-2026-092) and dispositioned as ACCEPT without impact to the validation outcome.

**The method (AM-LP50-007 Revision 03) is validated and approved for routine release and stability testing of losartan potassium 50 mg film-coated tablets manufactured under project LP-50-CMC-412.**

**Limitations of validation:** This report qualifies the method for assay at a single strength (50 mg) across a 50-150% working range (0.250-0.750 mg/mL). The method is not validated for (i) content uniformity at the single-tablet level, which requires a dedicated CU-suitability study at 0.005-0.015 mg/mL; (ii) quantitation of related substances, which requires separate validation of the related-substances procedure; (iii) strengths other than 50 mg; or (iv) inter-laboratory reproducibility. Solution stability is established for up to 48 h at both bench (22-25 C) and refrigerated (2-8 C) conditions; hold times beyond 48 h are not supported by this validation.

Transfer outputs (routine SST block, Table 17; method operating limits, Table 18) have been forwarded to method change-control record CR-2026-041 for incorporation into AM-LP50-007 Revision 04.
