# 7th Grade Life Science 5E Unit: Cell Structure & Function with Models & Claims

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 25-2022
- **Title:** Middle School Teachers, Except Special and Career/Technical Education

## Scenario
Ms. Hartwell teaches four sections of 7th grade Life Science at Kennedy Middle School, a suburban Title I school of 720 students. Her focus section has 29 students: 5 on IEPs (including 2 with specific learning disabilities in reading), 7 multilingual learners (WIDA 2-4, mostly Arabic and Haitian Creole home languages), and a wide range of science-identity levels. The class meets 47 minutes/day, 5 days/week. The unit precedes the district's quarterly science benchmark and follows the district's commitment to three-dimensional NGSS-aligned instruction. The unit should center a **phenomenon**: tardigrades surviving extreme conditions.

## Inputs
- **Standards (NGSS, MS-LS1 bundle):**
  - **MS-LS1-1** (conduct an investigation providing evidence that living things are made of cells; either one cell or many different numbers and types of cells)
  - **MS-LS1-2** (develop and use a model to describe the function of a cell as a whole and ways parts of cells contribute to the function)
  - **MS-LS1-3** (use argument supported by evidence for how the body is a system of interacting subsystems composed of groups of cells)
  - **Science and Engineering Practices:** Developing and Using Models; Constructing Explanations; Engaging in Argument from Evidence
  - **Crosscutting Concepts:** Structure and Function; Systems and System Models
- **Literacy standards (CCSS-ELA-Literacy, Grade 7):**
  - RST.6-8.7 (integrate quantitative or technical info in text with a version expressed visually)
  - WHST.6-8.1 (write arguments focused on discipline-specific content)
- **Prior unit:** Characteristics of life (completed), pre-assessment showed 68% of students hold "cells are only in animals" or "cells = atoms" misconceptions.
- **Materials available:** Class microscope set (16 scopes, student pairs), prepared slides (cheek cell, Elodea, onion skin), methylene blue, yeast, sugar, balloons, cellular respiration sensor probes (Vernier) x 8, Chromebooks, district-adopted OpenSciEd 7.1 anchor unit (partial).
- **Student asset data:** Class has strong drawers/artists (use for modeling), one student with a parent who is a nurse (possible guest speaker).
- **Assessment infrastructure:** District exit-ticket tracker, NGSS-aligned 3D rubric template.

## Artifact specification
Unit plan using the **5E Instructional Model (Engage-Explore-Explain-Elaborate-Evaluate)** and **NGSS storyline pedagogy** (phenomenon -> student questions -> investigations -> figuring out). Must reflect **Universal Design for Learning (UDL)** and disciplinary literacy integration.

Required sections:
1. **Unit overview**: anchor phenomenon (tardigrades / water bears surviving extreme conditions), driving question board seed questions, Performance Expectations targeted (MS-LS1-1, 2, 3), duration (15 instructional days).
2. **Storyline / arc**: how each lesson's learning builds toward explaining the anchor phenomenon — include a visual or described progression.
3. **Daily lesson sequence (15 days)**, each day mapped to a 5E phase and including:
   - Lesson focus question (student-facing)
   - 5E phase
   - SEP / CCC / DCI tags
   - Opening routine (Notice & Wonder, Do-Now)
   - Core investigation or task
   - Discourse structure (think-pair-share, Science Talk, gallery walk)
   - Consolidation / what we figured out
   - Formative check
4. **Anchor investigations**:
   - Microscope lab comparing cheek and onion cells (MS-LS1-1)
   - Cell model construction — students choose medium (physical, digital, analogy) (MS-LS1-2)
   - Yeast respiration investigation with CO2 probe (MS-LS1-3 evidence for systems)
5. **Text-dependent questions** set for at least one informational text (e.g., tardigrade Scientific American article excerpt).
6. **Summative performance task**: students construct a scientific argument (CER: Claim, Evidence, Reasoning) explaining how tardigrades survive extreme dehydration, using cell-level evidence.
7. **Differentiation**:
   - IEP accommodations (graphic organizers, chunked texts, peer reading)
   - MLL supports (frontloaded vocabulary with cognates, sentence frames for CER, visuals, labeling in home language on diagrams)
   - Extensions (comparison of prokaryotic vs. eukaryotic organelles; primary-source reading)
8. **Vocabulary instruction plan** — tiered word list (Tier 2: model, function, evidence; Tier 3: organelle, mitochondria, ribosome) with Frayer models and cognate bridges.
9. **Assessment blueprint** — formative checkpoints, summative rubric aligned to 3D NGSS.

Pedagogical conventions: 5E; phenomenon-based learning; three-dimensional NGSS; CER argumentation; Science Talks (Michaels & O'Connor).

## Output format
Single Markdown file, ~2,200-3,200 words. Include tables for the daily sequence and for the CER rubric.

## Iteration targets
1. **Phenomenon -> daily lesson coherence** — v1 often has the phenomenon only in the Engage; revise so students revisit tardigrades at multiple points with new lenses.
2. **Balance across 3 dimensions** — ensure SEPs and CCCs are genuinely engaged, not just listed (e.g., students actually use a model, not just view one).
3. **Discourse quality** — move beyond "turn and talk" to named Science Talk protocols with sentence stems.
4. **Reading-science integration** — TDQs should require students to cross-reference text evidence with lab observations.
5. **Middle-school developmental fit** — include autonomy, identity, and peer structures (choice of model medium, student-generated questions, peer feedback protocols).
6. **Formative -> adjustment loop** — each formative check includes what the teacher does if most students miss it.

## Success criteria
- All three PEs (MS-LS1-1, 2, 3) are explicitly addressed across the 15 days, with at least one full investigation per PE.
- Each lesson names an SEP, CCC, and DCI (3-dimensional).
- Anchor phenomenon reappears on at least 4 different days as the class builds explanatory power.
- CER rubric distinguishes claim quality, evidence sufficiency, and reasoning connection.
- Differentiation plan names distinct supports for IEP, MLL, and extension — not one blanket accommodation.
- At least 2 Science Talks or structured discourse protocols are included with sentence frames.

## Scope target
At 15 minutes, v1 should include: anchor phenomenon introduction, driving question board seeds, a 15-day daily skeleton with 5E phase labeled and 3D tags, draft CER summative task, and initial differentiation section. Full text-dependent question sets, vocabulary Frayer cards, and fine-grained formative decision rules are expected to be partial.
