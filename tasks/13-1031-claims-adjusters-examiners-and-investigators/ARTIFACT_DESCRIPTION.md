# Coverage and Liability Analysis Memo — Claim KR-2026-114807, Kiln Fire at Heartwood Ceramics

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 13-1031
- **Title:** Claims Adjusters, Examiners, and Investigators

## Scenario
You are a senior commercial-property claims examiner at Pentacliff Insurance. On March 17, 2026, an electric kiln at insured Heartwood Ceramics LLC (Galena, IL) overheated, causing a structure fire that destroyed the 11,400 sqft production building and killed an employee's family dog that had been brought to work. Insured submitted a proof-of-loss on April 2, 2026, claiming $3.42M in property damage, $860K business interruption, $15,000 for the dog, and $240K extra expense. The Large Loss Committee will review your coverage and liability analysis memo on May 6, 2026 before reserves are finalized and before you respond to the insured's counsel (Quigley, Muldoon & Oshiro LLC) who have already sent a reservation-of-rights objection letter.

## Inputs

**Policy: Pentacliff CPP-44820-2025 (Commercial Package Policy)**
- Effective: 7/1/2025–7/1/2026
- Building limit: $3,200,000 (replacement cost, 90% coinsurance)
- BPP limit: $1,100,000
- BI/EE: $1,200,000 (12-month indemnity, 72-hour waiting period)
- Deductible: $25,000 per occurrence
- Perils: Special form (excluding flood, earthquake)
- Exclusions of note: faulty workmanship, wear and tear, pollution (with a fungi/bacteria sublimit)
- Endorsement CP 10 30 06 07 (Causes of Loss — Special Form)
- Endorsement PF-2098 (Manufacturers' Consequential Loss)

**Facts established so far**
- Origin & cause report (Dascomb Forensic Engineering, 4/12/26): Fire originated at kiln KM-21 (2018 Skutt KM1227, 240V). Cause: thermocouple failure → runaway heating → ignition of wooden ware cart adjacent to kiln. No indication of arson; no evidence of negligent maintenance *beyond* manufacturer's recommended 24-month thermocouple replacement interval, which had lapsed 7 months ago.
- Fire department report (Galena FD, 3/17): structure total loss; no suppression system in that zone
- Policy declaration has no protective safeguards warranty (PCSW-1 not scheduled)
- Insured's manager (Calla Hodgkiss) admits she "postponed" the thermocouple replacement "to save money after the January sales slump"
- Building was ACV at binding (RC election not made); 90% coinsurance clause applies
- BPP inventory list incomplete; estimated $840K of clay, glazes, in-process ware destroyed
- BI worksheet shows $74,000/mo net income + continuing expenses $28,000
- Dog ("Pixel") belonged to employee Thora Ubben; Ubben is filing a separate Workers' Comp claim for smoke inhalation

**Jurisdictional notes**
- Illinois law; Rockford Mut. Ins. Co. v. Reed case cited by insured's counsel re: faulty workmanship exclusion
- IL concurrent-causation doctrine applies
- No statutory bad-faith multiplier under 215 ILCS 5/155 yet triggered

**Questions from the Large Loss Committee chair (Desmond Varra-Linde)**
- Coverage under CP Special Form given lapsed thermocouple replacement?
- Is the faulty-workmanship exclusion triggered, and if so does the ensuing-loss provision preserve coverage?
- Coinsurance penalty?
- BI calculation and start date?
- Is the dog covered? (BPP? Personal property of others? Exclusion?)
- Appropriate reserve?

## Artifact specification
Coverage and liability analysis memo. Required sections:
1. **Header** — Claim #, Insured, DOL, Examiner, Reviewer, Date, Privilege marking
2. **Executive summary** — coverage conclusion, recommended reserve, key open items
3. **Facts** — chronological, neutral, citing source documents
4. **Policy provisions implicated** — quote the operative language, cite form numbers and endorsements
5. **Coverage analysis** — one sub-section per coverage (Building, BPP, BI, EE, PP of Others); apply facts to policy language; address exclusions and ensuing-loss analysis; state the conclusion with supporting IL authority where relevant
6. **Coinsurance calculation** — show the math
7. **Damages analysis** — RC vs. ACV reasoning; BPP valuation approach given inventory gaps; BI formula with waiting period
8. **Contested items** — the dog; the counsel's faulty-workmanship argument
9. **Reserve recommendation** — split by coverage; include ULAE/ALAE
10. **Next steps and ROR position** — proof-of-loss, EUO consideration, subrogation analysis (manufacturer, thermocouple vendor)

Tone: objective, evidentiary, jurisdictionally aware. Cite policy language verbatim where operative.

## Output format
Markdown memo formatted for Large Loss Committee package and potential claim-file inclusion. Target 2,200–2,800 words.

## Iteration targets
1. Faulty-workmanship analysis often conflates the exclusion with the ensuing-loss clause; v1 should be rewritten to address the exclusion first, then apply ensuing-loss separately to the resulting fire damage.
2. Coinsurance math is commonly glossed; show formula with the insured's reported building value vs. 90% requirement.
3. BI analysis often uses monthly averages without accounting for the 72-hour waiting period; fix the calculation.
4. The dog question frequently gets a snap answer ("not covered"); proper analysis walks through BPP definition, "personal property of others," and any applicable limit or exclusion.
5. Reserve recommendation often lacks ULAE/ALAE — add both with reasoned estimates.
6. Subrogation analysis is typically an afterthought; expand on potential claims against Skutt Ceramic Products and the thermocouple supplier, given the manufacturer's interval.

## Success criteria
- Coverage conclusions are supported by quoted policy language and Illinois authority where applicable.
- The faulty-workmanship / ensuing-loss analysis is doctrinally correct: the exclusion is applied to the defective component and the ensuing fire loss is analyzed separately.
- Coinsurance and BI calculations are arithmetically shown, not asserted.
- Reserves are internally consistent with the coverage conclusions and include ALAE and ULAE.
- The memo directly answers each question from Desmond Varra-Linde.
- The ROR and next-steps section preserves insurer options (EUO, POL) without prejudicing the claim.

## Scope target
v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: header with privilege mark, exec summary with preliminary coverage stance, chronological fact section, policy-provision citations for Building/BPP/BI/EE, a first-pass coverage analysis for Building and BI, a coinsurance calculation, draft reserve split, and initial list of next steps. Roughly 1,400–1,700 words; the fully-worked ensuing-loss analysis, dog-coverage treatment, and subrogation section are iteration work.
