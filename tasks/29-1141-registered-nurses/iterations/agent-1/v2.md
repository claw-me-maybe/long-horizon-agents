### Topic 1 — Daily Weight Monitoring

**Key message (plain language):**
Weigh yourself every morning. Your scale tells us early if extra water is building up in your body. Step on the scale **after you use the bathroom and before you eat or drink**, wearing about the same clothes each day. Write the number on the log sheet right away.

Your dry weight is **82 kg, which is about 181 pounds**. (Think of it like this: 2 pounds is about the weight of a large water bottle — if you gain a water bottle's worth overnight, your body is holding extra water.)

- Gained **2 pounds in 1 day** or **5 pounds in 1 week** → **call the HF clinic the same business day: 555-0133.**
- Gained **more than 5 pounds in 2 days** and feel more short of breath → **call 911.**

**Daughter's role:** Check the log when she gets home from work. If a number is missing, ask Dad to weigh the next morning — do not guess.
**Dad's role when daughter is at work:** Weigh first thing, write the number on the fridge log, circle it if it is 2 or more pounds higher than yesterday.

**Teach-back prompt:**
"I want to make sure I explained this clearly. Can you show me how you will use the scale tomorrow morning and tell me what number would make you pick up the phone to call the clinic?"

### Topic 2 — Low-Sodium Diet

**Key message (plain language):**
Salt makes your body hold water. Too much salt is one of the main reasons people with heart failure end up back in the hospital. Your goal is **less than 2,000 mg of sodium a day** — about one teaspoon of salt in everything you eat.

**What to do:**
- Do **not** add salt at the table. Take the salt shaker off the table.
- Canned soup and instant soup are **very high in salt** (often over 800 mg in one bowl). Switch to **"low-sodium"** or **"no salt added"** cans, or make soup at home with herbs.
- Read labels: look for **sodium less than 140 mg per serving** (that number is "low sodium").
- Watch out for deli meat, cheese, frozen dinners, pickles, soy sauce, and fast food.
- Use lemon, pepper, garlic, onion, and herbs instead.

**Daughter's role:** Help read labels at the grocery store. Swap favorite soup brand for a low-sodium version.
**Dad's role when daughter is at work:** Pick foods from the "green list" on the 2 g sodium handout (fresh fruit, plain rice, oatmeal, unsalted crackers, eggs, chicken without gravy).

**Teach-back prompt:**
"Can you walk me through what you would pick for lunch tomorrow, and tell me one food you will leave behind because of the salt in it?"

### Topic 3 — Fluid Intake

**Key message (plain language):**
Your team did **not** set a strict fluid limit. But too much liquid can be a problem with heart failure. A safe rule of thumb is **about 2 liters a day — that is about 8 cups (eight 8-ounce glasses).** This includes coffee, soup, ice, and popsicles, not just water.

If the HF clinic gives you a different number on 2026-04-24, use their number instead.

**Teach-back prompt:**
"Tell me in your own words how much fluid is okay for you in a day, and what counts as fluid besides water."

### Topic 5 — Symptom Red Flags

**Key message (plain language):**
Heart failure gives you warning signs before it gets bad. Know the signs. Know who to call.

| If you notice this… | What to do | Who to call |
|---|---|---|
| **Severe shortness of breath even sitting still** | Sit up, stay calm, do not drive yourself | **911** |
| **Chest pain or pressure** | Stop, sit down | **911** |
| **Fainting / passing out** | Stay lying down | **911** |
| **Coughing pink foamy spit** | Sit up | **911** |
| **Gained more than 5 lb in 2 days and feel short of breath** | Sit up | **911** |
| **Gained 2 lb in 1 day or 5 lb in 1 week** | Write it down on log | **HF clinic 555-0133** (same business day) — after hours **555-0145** |
| **More ankle swelling than your usual** | Elevate legs, note on log | **HF clinic 555-0133** |
| **Need an extra pillow to sleep / wake up short of breath** | Prop up | **HF clinic 555-0133** |
| **Dizzy when you stand up once** | Sit back down, sip water | **HF clinic 555-0133** |
| **Dizzy every time you stand up** | Stay seated, get help | **HF clinic 555-0133** (same day) |
| **New bleeding — gums, nose you can't stop, dark stools (like coffee grounds or tar), blood in urine, big bruises (apixaban)** | Press on any visible bleeding | **HF clinic 555-0133** (after hours **555-0145**); **911** if you cannot stop bleeding or feel faint |
| **Muscle cramps, weakness, irregular heartbeat (possible potassium change)** | Stop heavy activity | **HF clinic 555-0133** |
| **Shaky, sweaty, confused (low blood sugar on metformin / empagliflozin)** | Eat or drink something sweet (juice, glucose tab) | **HF clinic 555-0133** if it keeps happening |
| **Missed a dose and not sure what to do** | Do not double up on your own | **Pharmacy 555-0200** or **clinic 555-0133** |

**Teach-back prompt:**
"If you woke up tomorrow 3 pounds heavier than yesterday and a little more short of breath, tell me in your own words what you would do, and who you would call."

### Topic 6 — Fall Prevention

**Key message (plain language):**
You are at higher risk for a fall because:
1. You had a fall in 2025.
2. You use a cane to walk.
3. Your new water pill and blood pressure medicine can make you dizzy when you stand up.

**What to do:**
- **Use your cane every time** you walk, even from the bed to the bathroom.
- **Stand up slowly.** Sit at the edge of the bed for a count of 10, then stand.
- **Use the grab bars** in the bathroom.
- Take your water pill in the **morning** so you do not have to rush to the bathroom at night.
- Keep a night light on between the bedroom and bathroom.
- If you feel dizzy, **sit down right where you are** before you fall.

**Daughter's role:** Clear throw rugs and cords. Keep a cordless phone or cell phone within reach of Dad's chair and bedside.

**Teach-back prompt:**

### Topic 8 — When to Call HF Clinic vs. 911

**Key message (plain language):**

**Call HF Clinic 555-0133 (Monday–Friday, 8 AM–5 PM):**
- Weight up 2–4 lb in 1 day, or 5 lb in 1 week.
- More ankle swelling.
- Need an extra pillow to sleep.
- New dry cough.
- Felt dizzy once standing up.
- Missed a dose and not sure what to do.

**Call After-Hours Nurse Line 555-0145 (evenings, weekends, holidays):**
- Same concerns as above, but after clinic is closed.

**Call 911 (right now):**
- Cannot catch your breath sitting still.
- Chest pain or pressure.
- Fainted.
- Coughing pink foam.
- Weight up more than 5 lb in 2 days and feeling worse.
- Bleeding you cannot stop.

**Do not drive yourself to the ER.**

**Teach-back prompt:**
"If you were sitting in your chair on a Saturday night and you could not catch your breath, who would you call? And if it was a weekday afternoon and you just felt a little more swollen, who would you call?"
