**Key message (plain language):**

Your weight tells us early if your body is holding extra water. Weigh yourself the same way each day.

### Topic 2 — Low-Sodium Diet  (plate icon)

**Key message (plain language):**

Salt makes your body hold water. Extra water makes your heart work too hard. Too much salt is one of the top reasons people with heart failure end up back in the hospital.

**Your goal: less than 2,000 mg of sodium a day.** That is about **one teaspoon** of salt in all the food you eat in a whole day.

**Simple steps:**
- **Take the salt shaker off the table.**
- **Canned soup is the big one.** Most canned soup has **800 mg or more** in one bowl. That is almost half your whole day.
  - Look for cans that say **"low sodium"** or **"no salt added."**
  - Or make soup at home with herbs (pepper, garlic, onion, lemon).
- Read the label. Look at the **sodium** line. **Less than 140 mg per serving = low sodium.** That's the target number on packages.
- Watch out for: deli meat, cheese, frozen dinners, pickles, soy sauce, fast food, canned vegetables.
- Instead use: fresh fruit, fresh or frozen vegetables (no sauce), plain rice, oatmeal, unsalted crackers, eggs, chicken without gravy.

**Daughter's role:**
- Help read labels at the store.
- Swap the usual soup brand for a low-sodium version.

**Dad's role when daughter is at work:**
- Pick lunch from the "green list" on the 2 g sodium handout (fresh fruit, plain rice, unsalted crackers, eggs, chicken without gravy).
- If in doubt, skip it.

**Teach-back prompt:**
*"Can you tell me what you would pick for lunch tomorrow? And name one food you will leave behind because of the salt in it?"*

### Topic 3 — Fluid Intake  (cup icon)

**Key message (plain language):**

Your team did **not** set a strict fluid limit. But drinking too much liquid can hurt with heart failure.

A safe plan: **about 2 liters a day.** That is about **8 cups** — picture eight 8-ounce glasses of water in a row.

Count these as fluid, not just water:
- Coffee and tea
- Soup broth
- Juice, soda, and milk
- Ice (it melts)
- Jello and popsicles

If the HF clinic gives you a different number on 2026-04-24, use their number instead.

**Teach-back prompt:**
*"Tell me in your own words how much fluid is okay for you in one day, and name three things besides water that count."*

### Topic 5 — Symptom Red Flags  (exclamation icon)

**Key message (plain language):**

Your body gives you warning signs before heart failure gets bad. Watch for them. You have three choices: keep going, call the clinic, or call 911.

| Warning sign | What it means | What to do | Who to call |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shortness of breath when you are sitting still | Heart is stressed | Sit up, do not drive | **911** |
| Chest pain or pressure | Possible heart attack | Stop, sit down | **911** |
| Fainting / passed out | Serious | Stay down, get help | **911** |
| Coughing pink foamy spit | Fluid in the lungs | Sit up | **911** |
| Weight up **more than 5 lb in 2 days** and short of breath | Heart is failing | Sit up | **911** |
| Weight up **2 lb in 1 day** OR **5 lb in 1 week** | Holding water | Write on log, call | **HF clinic 555-0133**; after hours **555-0145** |
| More ankle swelling than your usual | Holding water | Put feet up, call | **HF clinic 555-0133** |
| Need an extra pillow at night, or wake up breathless | Fluid building up | Prop up, call | **HF clinic 555-0133** |
| Dizzy **one time** when standing | Possible low BP | Sit back down, sip water, call | **HF clinic 555-0133** |
| Dizzy **every time** you stand | Blood pressure too low | Stay seated, get help, call | **HF clinic 555-0133** (same business day) |
| Bleeding on apixaban — gums, nose that won't stop, dark black stools like tar, blood in urine, big bruises without a bump | Blood thinner side effect | Press on any bleeding | **HF clinic 555-0133**; after hours **555-0145**. **Call 911** if bleeding does not stop or you feel faint |
| Muscle cramps, weakness, or your heart feels like it is skipping | Possible potassium change | Stop heavy activity | **HF clinic 555-0133** |

### Topic 6 — Fall Prevention  (cane icon)

**Key message (plain language):**

You have a higher chance of falling. Here is why:
1. You had a fall last year (2025).
2. You use a cane to walk.
3. Your **new water pill** and your blood pressure pill can make you dizzy when you stand up too fast.

**Simple rules to stay safe:**
- **Use your cane every time** — even walking from the bed to the bathroom.
- **Stand up slowly.** Sit on the edge of the bed. Count to 10. Then stand.
- **Use the grab bars** in the bathroom every time.
- Take your water pill in the **morning** (sun icon). This keeps you from rushing to the bathroom at night.
- Keep a **night light** between the bed and the bathroom.
- Wear **non-slip shoes or slippers** — not socks on wood floors.
- If you feel dizzy, **sit down right where you are.** The floor is better than a fall.

**Daughter's role:**
- Pick up throw rugs and loose cords.
- Keep a phone within arm's reach of Dad's chair and bedside.
- Make sure the night light works.

**Dad's role when daughter is at work:**
- Cane by the bed at night.
- Non-slip footwear always.
- If you feel dizzy, sit down. Call **555-0133** from wherever you sit.

**Teach-back prompt:**
*"Can you show me — step by step — how you are going to get out of bed tomorrow morning in a way that keeps you from getting dizzy?"*
