## Part B — Teach-Back Education Plan

Framing for the bedside nurse: Use the "I want to make sure I explained this well" framing. Never ask "Do you understand?" Never test — invite the patient to explain back in his own words. Include the daughter when possible.

### 1. Daily Weight Monitoring

**Key message:** Every morning, after you go to the bathroom and before you eat or drink, step on the scale in the same clothes. Write the number down. A sudden jump in weight means your body is holding extra water.

**What to watch:**
- Up **2 pounds in 1 day** OR **5 pounds in 1 week** = call the HF clinic that same business day at **555-0133**.
- Up **more than 5 pounds in 2 days** with shortness of breath = call **911**.

**Teach-back prompt:** "I want to make sure I explained this well. Can you walk me through what you will do tomorrow morning with the scale, and what number would make you pick up the phone to call us?"

### 4. Medication Changes

**Key message:** Three medicines are different today:
- **Torsemide** is your new water pill. It replaces furosemide. **Do not take the old furosemide anymore** — return those bottles to the mail-order pharmacy.
- **Empagliflozin** is a new pill that helps your heart and kidneys.
- **Potassium** is new — take it while you are on torsemide. It replaces what the water pill takes out.

Take the water pill (torsemide) in the **morning** so you are not getting up all night to use the bathroom.

**Teach-back prompt:** "Can you show me on the pill bottles which one is your new water pill, and tell me when in the day you will take it?"

**Red flag:** If you take two doses by accident or miss a dose and don't know what to do → call **555-0133** during the day or the after-hours line at **555-0145**.

### 5. Symptom Red Flags

**Key message:** Some symptoms mean your heart needs help now.

| If you notice... | Do this... | Call... |
|---|---|---|
| Worse shortness of breath at rest | Sit up, rest | **911** |
| Chest pain or pressure | Stop activity, sit down | **911** |
| Fainting / passing out | Do not drive | **911** |
| Pink frothy sputum (coughing up pink foam) | Sit up | **911** |
| Weight up 2-4 lb in 1 day or 5 lb in 1 week | Write it down, call clinic | **555-0133** (same day) |
| More ankle swelling, need extra pillow at night | Note it, call clinic | **555-0133** (same day) |
| Dizzy on standing once | Sit back down, sip water, call | **555-0133** |
| Unusual bruising or bleeding (apixaban) | Press on any bleeding | **555-0133** (after-hours: 555-0145) |
| Feeling weak, muscle cramps, irregular heartbeat (potassium changes) | Note it | **555-0133** |

**Teach-back prompt:** "If you woke up tomorrow two pounds heavier and a little more short of breath, tell me in your own words what you would do and who you would call."

### 8. When to Call the HF Clinic vs. 911

**Key message:**
- **HF Clinic (555-0133, M-F 8:00-17:00):** Weight up, mild swelling, missed dose, new cough, felt dizzy once. Call the same business day.
- **After-hours nurse line (555-0145):** Evenings or weekends for the same types of concerns.
- **911:** Severe shortness of breath at rest, chest pain, fainting, coughing pink foam, weight up more than 5 lb in 2 days with symptoms.

**Teach-back prompt:** "If you felt a little more short of breath on a Saturday afternoon, who would you call? What if you suddenly could not catch your breath at all?"
