## Part B — Teach-Back Education Plan

> **Note to bedside RN:** Framing for every prompt — "I want to make sure *I* explained this well." Never "Do you understand?" Daughter should be present when possible; at least the weight-log and evening-dose teaching must be witnessed by daughter.

### 1. Daily Weight Monitoring

**Key message:** Weigh yourself every morning after you use the bathroom and before breakfast. Wear the same kind of clothes. Write the number on the log right away. Call the HF clinic the same business day (555-0133) if you gain more than 2 pounds in one day or more than 5 pounds in one week. That extra weight is water, not food.

**Teach-back prompt:** "Can you show me how and when you will weigh yourself tomorrow morning, and tell me what number would make you call the clinic?"

**Red flag / escalation:**
- Gain >2 lb (about 1 kg) in 1 day OR >5 lb (about 2.3 kg) in 1 week → call HF clinic 555-0133 the same business day.
- Gain >5 lb in 2 days that is not coming down → call 911.

### 4. Medication Changes

**Key message:** Your old water pill, furosemide, is stopped. Do not take it anymore. Return the old bottles to the mail-order pharmacy drop box. You will now take one new water pill called torsemide in the morning. You also have a new diabetes/heart pill called empagliflozin once a day, and a new potassium pill once a day to replace potassium the water pill removes.

**Teach-back prompt:** "Tell me in your own words which old pill you will stop, which new pill takes its place, and when in the day you will take it."

**Red flag / escalation:**
- If you accidentally take both furosemide and torsemide → call nurse triage 555-0145 right away.
- If you miss a dose → take it as soon as you remember that day; do not double up the next day; call the clinic if unsure (555-0133).

### 5. Symptom Red Flags

**Key message:** Some symptoms mean your heart failure is getting worse. Know which ones to watch for and who to call.

**Teach-back prompt:** "Tell me two symptoms that would make you call the HF clinic today, and two symptoms that would make you call 911."

**Red flag / escalation:**
- New or worse shortness of breath at rest → call 911.
- Chest pain or pressure, or coughing pink frothy sputum → call 911.
- Fainting or near-fainting → call 911.
- Unusual bleeding, blood in stool/urine, large bruises (you are on apixaban, a blood thinner) → call HF clinic 555-0133 same day; call 911 if heavy bleeding.
- Mild increase in ankle swelling, need for an extra pillow to sleep, feeling dizzy once standing up → call HF clinic 555-0133 the same business day.

### 8. When to Call HF Clinic vs. 911

**Key message:** Call the HF clinic at 555-0133 the same business day for warning signs (yellow zone). Call 911 right away for emergencies (red zone). After 5 pm or on weekends, the nurse triage line at 555-0145 is available.

**Teach-back prompt:** "Walk me through which number you would call if you woke up tonight gasping for air, and which number you would call if your ankles were a little more swollen but you felt okay otherwise."

**Red flag / escalation:** See HF Zones sheet below.

## Part C — HF Zones Quick Reference

Post this on the refrigerator.

### Green Zone — All Clear
- No shortness of breath at rest.
- Weight stable within 2 lb of your dry weight (about 181 lb / 82 kg).
- No new swelling.
- Usual energy.
- Taking your medicines as scheduled.

**Action:** Keep doing what you are doing.

### Yellow Zone — Warning (Call HF Clinic 555-0133, same business day)
- Weight up 2-4 lb in 1 day OR 5 lb in 1 week.
- Mild increase in ankle swelling.
- Needing an extra pillow to sleep.
- New dry cough.
- Felt dizzy once when standing.
- Missed a dose.

**Action:** Call HF Clinic 555-0133 the same business day (M-F 8:00-17:00). After hours: nurse triage 555-0145.

### Red Zone — Emergency (Call 911)
- Severe shortness of breath at rest.
- Chest pain or pressure.
- Fainting.
- Coughing pink frothy sputum.
- Weight up more than 5 lb in 2 days that is not coming down.

**Action:** Call 911.
