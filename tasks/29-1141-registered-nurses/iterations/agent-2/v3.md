### Topic 2 — Low-Salt Eating

**Key message (for you):**
Salt pulls water into your body. Water makes your heart work harder. Your target is **less than 2,000 mg of salt a day** — about one small teaspoon total from all your food combined.

Three simple rules:

1. **No salt at the table.** Put the shaker in the cupboard.
2. **Read the label on soup cans.** Look for the word "Sodium" (it means salt). Pick cans that say "140 mg or less" per serving, or that say "low sodium" or "no salt added."
3. **Watch out for these high-salt foods:** lunch meat, hot dogs, bacon, pickles, soy sauce, frozen dinners, regular canned soup, pizza, and fast food.

Fresh or plain frozen vegetables, plain chicken or fish, fruit, rice, and unsalted nuts are good choices.

**Teach-back prompt:**
"Tell me three changes you will make to your meals this week so you eat less salt. Then show me where on a soup can you would look to find the salt amount, and what number you want that to be."

**Caregiver role:**
Your daughter helps read labels at the store. Together, pick **3 soups you like that are low-salt** and keep them in the pantry. She can also make a big pot of low-salt soup on weekends for you to eat on her work days.

**Red flag and what to do:**
- You ate a salty meal (takeout, restaurant, regular soup) and the next morning your weight is up more than 2 lb → **Call HF clinic 555-0133** today.

### Topic 6 — Fall Prevention

**Key message (for you):**
Your fall risk is moderate. You fell once last year and you use a cane. The new water pill can make you feel light-headed when you first stand up, especially this first week.

Six rules to follow at home:

1. **Use your cane every time you stand and walk.** Keep it right next to the bed.
2. **Stand up slow.** Sit on the edge of the bed and count to 10. Stand for a count of 10 before you start walking.
3. **In the bathroom:** Use the grab bars (they are already installed). Keep the nightlight on. Do not rush.
4. **Floor check:** No loose rugs. Tape down any cords. Keep a clear path from bed to bathroom.
5. **Shoes:** Wear shoes or slippers that have backs and non-slip soles. No bare feet on tile.
6. **Timing:** Morning water pill means you sleep through the night without bathroom trips. Do NOT take the water pill late in the day.

**Teach-back prompt:**
"Show me right now how you will get out of bed tomorrow morning — lying down, to sitting, to standing, to picking up your cane, to walking to the bathroom — and tell me how long you will pause at each step."

**Caregiver role:**

**Red flags and what to do:**
