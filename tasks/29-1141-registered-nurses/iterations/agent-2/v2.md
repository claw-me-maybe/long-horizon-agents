### Topic 2 — Low-Sodium Diet

**Key message (for patient):**
Salt pulls water into your body. Too much salt makes your heart work harder. Your target is **less than 2,000 mg of sodium a day** — about one small teaspoon of salt total from all foods.

Easy wins:
- Do not add salt at the table. Put the shaker away.
- Canned soup is a big source of salt. Pick "low sodium" or "no salt added" cans. The label should say 140 mg or less per serving.
- Fresh or frozen plain vegetables are low in salt. Lunch meat, bacon, hot dogs, pickles, soy sauce, and frozen dinners are high in salt.
- When you eat out, ask for no added salt and get sauces on the side.

**Teach-back prompt:**
"Tell me two changes you will make at meals this week to cut down on salt, and show me where you would look on a soup can label to see how much sodium is in it."

**Caregiver role:**
Daughter helps read labels on grocery day. They agree on 3 brands of low-sodium soup that the patient likes.

**Red flag / action:**
- If you eat a high-salt meal (takeout, restaurant, big soup) and you gain >2 lb the next morning → **Call HF clinic 555-0133** same business day.

### Topic 8 — When to Call HF Clinic vs. 911

**Key message (for patient):**
Two phone numbers. Learn them. Put both on the refrigerator and in your phone.

- **HF Clinic — 555-0133** (Monday through Friday, 8 am to 5 pm): Call for warning signs (yellow zone) — extra weight, more swelling, missed doses, questions.
- **After-hours nurse triage — 555-0145** (evenings, weekends): For warning signs that come up when the clinic is closed.
- **911** (any time, day or night): Call right away for emergency signs (red zone) — severe shortness of breath, chest pain, fainting, pink frothy spit, a fall with head injury, or very fast weight gain that is not coming down.

**When in doubt, call the clinic. It is always okay to call.**
