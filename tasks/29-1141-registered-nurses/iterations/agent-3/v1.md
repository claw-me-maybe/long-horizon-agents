## Part B — Teach-Back Education Plan

Each topic includes a key message (patient-facing), a teach-back prompt, and red-flag triggers where relevant.

### 1. Daily weight monitoring

**Key message:** Weigh yourself every morning after using the bathroom and before breakfast. Wear the same kind of clothes each time. Write the number down on the log sheet right away. Too much weight gain means water is building up in your body.

**Teach-back prompt:** "I want to make sure I explained this clearly — can you walk me through how you will weigh yourself tomorrow morning and where you will write it down?"

**Red-flag triggers:**
- Weight up 2 lb (about 1 kg) in 1 day OR 5 lb (about 2 kg) in 1 week → call HF clinic same day at 555-0133
- Weight up more than 5 lb in 2 days and not coming down → call 911

### 4. Medication changes

**Key message:** Your old water pill (furosemide) is stopped. Your new water pill is called torsemide — take one pill in the morning only. Taking both the old and new pills together would be dangerous. You also have a new pill called empagliflozin for your heart and diabetes, and a new potassium pill to replace what the water pill washes out.

**Teach-back prompt:** "Tell me in your own words which of your old pills you are stopping, and which new ones you are adding. If you're not sure, that tells me I need to explain it better."

**Red-flag triggers:**
- Accidentally taking both furosemide and torsemide → call HF clinic 555-0133; if feeling dizzy or very weak, call 911
- Missed dose → take when you remember unless it's close to the next dose; if unsure, call the after-hours nurse line 555-0145

### 5. Symptom red flags

**Key message:** Some symptoms mean your heart failure is getting worse. Others mean you need to go to the hospital now. Knowing the difference helps you stay safe.

**Teach-back prompt:** "Can you tell me two symptoms that would make you pick up the phone and call the clinic, and two that would make you call 911?"

**Red-flag triggers:**
- Worsening shortness of breath, needing an extra pillow, new swelling → call HF clinic 555-0133 same day
- Chest pain or pressure, fainting, severe shortness of breath at rest, coughing pink frothy spit → call 911
- Unusual bruising or blood in stool/urine (you are on apixaban, a blood thinner) → call HF clinic 555-0133
- Feeling shaky, sweaty, or very confused (possible low blood sugar) → check sugar, eat something, call PCP 555-0178 if it happens often

### 8. When to call the HF clinic vs. 911

**Key message:** Call the HF clinic (555-0133, Mon–Fri 8:00–17:00) for warning signs that are new but not emergencies — more swelling, more weight, dry cough, one episode of dizziness, or a missed dose you're not sure about. Call 911 for emergencies — severe trouble breathing, chest pain, fainting, or pink frothy cough.

**Teach-back prompt:** "If you stepped on the scale tomorrow and you were up 3 pounds from today, what would you do and who would you call?"

**Red-flag triggers:**
- HF Clinic: 555-0133 (M–F 8:00–17:00)
- After-hours nurse line: 555-0145
- PCP: 555-0178
- Pharmacy: 555-0200
- Emergency: 911
