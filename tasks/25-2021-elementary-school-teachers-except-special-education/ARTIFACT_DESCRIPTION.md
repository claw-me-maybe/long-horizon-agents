# 2nd Grade Place Value Unit: Three-Digit Numbers with Base-Ten Manipulatives (12 Days)

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 25-2021
- **Title:** Elementary School Teachers, Except Special Education

## Scenario
Mr. Chen teaches a self-contained 2nd grade class of 22 students at Maple Creek Elementary, a Title I school in a mid-sized district. His roster includes 3 students receiving Tier 2 math intervention (Bridges Intervention, pulled for 30 min 3x/week by the interventionist), 4 multilingual learners (2 at WIDA 2, 2 at WIDA 3, all with Spanish as home language), one student on a 504 plan for ADHD (movement breaks every 15 min, preferential seating), and 2 identified as advanced (accelerated in number sense and demonstrating addition fluency into the hundreds). The class meets for a 75-minute math block daily; the unit precedes the winter benchmark (iReady Diagnostic) administered in early January. The reading specialist has flagged two of the MLLs as also working on oral-language production; math-talk routines are a shared growth target.

## Inputs
- **Standards (CCSS-Math, Grade 2):**
  - **2.NBT.A.1** — Understand that the three digits of a three-digit number represent amounts of hundreds, tens, and ones.
  - **2.NBT.A.1.a** — "100 can be thought of as a bundle of ten tens — called a 'hundred'."
  - **2.NBT.A.1.b** — The numbers 100, 200, 300, 400, 500, 600, 700, 800, 900 refer to one, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, or nine hundreds (and 0 tens and 0 ones).
  - **2.NBT.A.2** — Count within 1000; skip-count by 5s, 10s, and 100s.
  - **2.NBT.A.3** — Read and write numbers to 1000 using base-ten numerals, number names, and expanded form.
  - **2.NBT.A.4** — Compare two three-digit numbers based on meanings of the hundreds, tens, and ones digits, using >, =, and < symbols.
  - Standards for Mathematical Practice (SMP) 2, 4, 7 — Reason abstractly and quantitatively; Model with mathematics; Look for and make use of structure.
- **Prior unit data (October):**
  - Students finished two-digit place value unit; cool-down data: 18/22 fluent with tens/ones at end.
  - 4 students (including 2 Tier 2 learners) still miscount bundles of ten (e.g., count-all instead of count-on-by-tens).
  - The 2 advanced students independently wrote 4-digit numbers on a challenge task.
- **Curricular program:** District adopted Eureka Math / EngageNY Grade 2 Module 3. Teacher may use/adapt and must supplement with the district's Number Talk routines.
- **Manipulatives available:**
  - Base-ten blocks (ones cubes, tens rods, hundreds flats) — class set sufficient for 500 ones, 50 rods, 10 flats per student pair.
  - Place-value disks (1, 10, 100) in student bags.
  - Hundred charts (laminated, dry-erase).
  - Digit cards 0-9 (one set per student).
  - Number lines 0-1000 (wall-mounted and student-sized).
  - Optional manipulative: "money model" using dimes and pennies (dimes = tens, pennies = ones) — introduce mid-unit if helpful.
- **Vocabulary targets (primary):** hundreds, tens, ones, digit, value, place, expanded form, standard form, word form, compare, greater than, less than, equal to, bundle.
- **Schedule (75-min block):**
  - 10-min Number Talk / fluency routine
  - 50-min core lesson (Launch / Explore / Discuss / Summarize)
  - 15-min small-group rotations (teacher table + 2 independent stations)
- **Literacy block note:** Math block is 10:15-11:30 daily, immediately after morning meeting; recess is directly before, so students arrive energized.

## Artifact specification
12-day unit plan using **Concrete-Representational-Abstract (CRA) progression** and a **launch / explore / discuss / summarize** lesson structure. Must reflect **Universal Design for Learning (UDL)** principles.

Required sections:
1. **Unit overview**: big ideas, essential questions in kid-friendly language ("How can grouping help us count big numbers?"), end-of-unit assessment plan.
2. **Standards map**: each lesson tagged to specific CCSS sub-standard.
3. **Daily lesson sequence (12 days)** with per-day:
   - Learning target (student-facing "I can..." statement)
   - Number Talk prompt (retrieval / reasoning routine)
   - Launch (hook / connection to prior learning)
   - Explore (hands-on task with specified manipulatives)
   - Discuss (share strategies, named participation structure — turn-and-talk, number strings)
   - Summarize (anchor-chart addition, exit ticket)
   - Small-group rotation plan (Tier 2, core, extension)
4. **Center/rotation tasks** (at least 4 reusable stations): hands-on building, game, independent practice, teacher table.
5. **Family communication letter** (short) explaining place value and one home activity.
6. **Differentiation and accommodations**:
   - Tier 2 intervention plan (pre-teach vocabulary, use disks longer, daily fluency check)
   - MLL supports (sentence frames: "The number ___ has ___ hundreds, ___ tens, ___ ones."; bilingual anchor chart; partner pairings)
   - 504 ADHD supports (movement breaks, choice boards, reduced problem count)
   - Enrichment for 2 advanced students (four-digit exploration, riddles)
7. **End-of-unit assessment** (10-15 items) with scoring guide.

Pedagogical conventions: CRA progression; Number Talks (Parrish); gradual release; anchor charts; turn-and-talk; exit tickets; UDL multiple means of representation/expression; Math Workshop / 3-Act tasks (Meyer).

**Suggested 12-day arc (starting frame for the downstream model):**
- Day 1: Review 2-digit place value; bundling-by-ten investigation.
- Day 2: Introduce "a hundred is 10 tens" with base-ten flats; build numbers with ones only, then rods, then flats.
- Day 3: Build three-digit numbers with manipulatives; name them; connect to digit cards.
- Day 4: Zero as placeholder (the tricky "305" case; common misconception).
- Day 5: Standard form, word form, expanded form (3-way connection routine).
- Day 6: Count within 1000; skip-count by 10s and 100s.
- Day 7: Compare two-digit numbers review -> transition to three-digit.
- Day 8: Compare three-digit numbers; greatest-place-first rule.
- Day 9: Compare with manipulatives and symbolic; "Alligator" vs. "More balloons" anchor.
- Day 10: Mixed review / rotations; apply to real-world contexts (crowd sizes, book counts).
- Day 11: Review day with games (Greatest Number Wins, Build-a-Number).
- Day 12: End-of-unit assessment + celebration.

## Output format

## Iteration targets
1. **Manipulative progression** — v1 may jump from concrete to abstract too quickly; explicitly plan the representational (drawings, disks) bridge and when to remove manipulatives.
2. **Number Talk variety** — ensure Number Talks aren't all counting-by-tens; include comparing, decomposing, and estimating prompts.
3. **Exit ticket -> rotation assignment loop** — each day's exit ticket should feed into tomorrow's small-group assignments with a decision rule.
4. **Vocabulary scaffolds for MLLs** — move beyond translations; add visual supports, total physical response (TPR), and structured partner talk.
5. **Common misconceptions** — name and address specific misconceptions (e.g., "305" read as "thirty-five"; confusing digit and value).
6. **Joy / engagement** — add at least 2 games or movement-based tasks; primary-grade plans lose engagement if they are only worksheets.

## Common misconceptions to anticipate (for the downstream model)
- Reading "305" as "thirty-five" (skipping zero-tens).
- Writing "two hundred and forty-three" as "200403" (concatenation).
- Miscounting flats as "10s" (confusing hundred with ten).
- Comparing by total-digit-count rather than place value (e.g., saying 99 > 100 because "9 is bigger than 1").
- Thinking "bigger number of digits" in a single place means bigger (e.g., 245 vs. 183 — comparing the "8" and "4").
- Expanded form confusion: writing 245 as "2 + 4 + 5" rather than "200 + 40 + 5".

## Rotation station starter menu (for the downstream model)
These are suggested starter stations; the artifact should include at least 4 reusable stations:
- **Build-It Station**: Digit cards and base-ten blocks; build numbers to match cards, record on recording sheet.
- **Greatest Number Wins** (partner game): Partners draw 3 digit cards each, arrange into largest number, compare; use <, >, = symbols.
- **Number of the Day**: Student-chosen number; complete graphic organizer (standard, word, expanded, draw with blocks, compare to 500, +10, -10).
- **Hundred-Chart Patterns**: Color patterns when skip-counting by 10s and 100s; extend to a thousand chart.
- **Riddle Cards** (for advanced): "I am greater than 500 and less than 600. My tens digit is 3 more than my ones digit. Who am I?" (multiple answers possible, justify).

## Pedagogical vocabulary glossary
- **CRA**: Concrete-Representational-Abstract progression — learners move from manipulating physical objects to drawings/diagrams to symbolic notation.
- **Number Talk**: A short (5-10 min) routine where students solve a problem mentally and share strategies; builds number sense and discourse.
- **Launch / Explore / Discuss / Summarize**: A four-phase lesson structure common in elementary math (Van de Walle).
- **UDL**: Universal Design for Learning; planning for multiple means of engagement, representation, and action/expression.
- **Tier 2**: MTSS intervention layer for students needing targeted small-group support beyond Tier 1 core instruction.
- **Exit ticket**: A brief end-of-lesson check used to inform next-day grouping and instruction.
- **WIDA levels**: English-language proficiency levels (1 Entering to 6 Reaching) that calibrate linguistic supports for MLLs.
- **MTSS**: Multi-Tiered System of Supports; a school-wide framework for delivering academic and behavioral supports at escalating intensity.
- **Anchor chart**: A classroom-posted visual reference, typically co-created with students, that captures key ideas/routines.
- **I-can statement**: A student-facing learning target phrased as "I can..." to make objectives accessible to learners.
- **iReady Diagnostic**: A computer-adaptive K-8 assessment used for universal screening and progress monitoring (Curriculum Associates).

## Iteration loop expectation
This artifact will be drafted, evaluated, and iterated. Expect 3-5 rounds of feedback focused on CRA progression, differentiation specificity, Number Talk variety, exit-ticket utility, misconception-handling, and joy/engagement in the primary classroom.

## Accessibility and equity checks
- All three MLL anchor chart versions (English, Spanish, visual-only) should be planned from Day 1.
- 504 ADHD supports should appear as routine structures (timer visible to all, movement break built into lesson flow), not as exclusive "special" accommodations.
- Tier 2 students should not be segregated to the "low rotation" every day — plan mixed-ability grouping for specific tasks where productive struggle is welcomed.
- Advanced learners should not simply get "more problems" — plan open-middle tasks (Dan Meyer) and structured extensions.
- Family letter should be translated into Spanish; assume the school's family-engagement liaison can review.
