# Executive Pre-Brief + Action Checklist — Board Dinner with Northwind Capital Managing Partners, May 6, 2026

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 43-6011
- **Title:** Executive Secretaries and Executive Administrative Assistants

## Scenario
You are Vanessa Okoye, executive assistant to Ingrid Halvorsen, CEO of Sentinel Grid Technologies (a Series C cleantech firm, Boulder, CO, ~220 employees, $48M ARR). On May 6, 2026, Ingrid will host a closed, 6-person dinner at The Flagstaff House in Boulder with two managing partners from Northwind Capital — a growth-equity firm considering leading Sentinel's Series D. The dinner begins at 6:30 PM and is expected to run until 10:00 PM. Northwind requested this dinner after an initial pitch meeting three weeks ago. Ingrid has asked you to produce a pre-brief memo that she will review the afternoon of May 6 on her flight back from a conference in Phoenix.

## Artifact specification
Produce an executive pre-brief memo with a practical action checklist. Keep it in an executive-assistant voice, not a chief-of-staff voice.

1. **Front matter** — TO: Ingrid. FROM: Vanessa. DATE: 2026-05-06. RE: Northwind Dinner, May 6 6:30 PM, Flagstaff House (Aspen Room). CLASSIFICATION: Confidential - CEO Eyes + Amit/Niamh brief only.

2. **At-a-glance (read in 60 seconds)** — 6 facts only: venue, time, attendees, valuation floor, one sentence on the desired outcome, and one sentence on the biggest risk.

3. **Attendees** — a short dossier per Northwind person: role, tenure, relevant board seats, one recent public position, and one tasteful rapport hook if already volunteered or publicly available. Keep the note strictly informational.

4. **Briefing**
   - Deal context: ask, current range, walk-away, and the one decision Ingrid needs from herself before the dinner
   - Our three headline proof-points, each with one supporting fact and the document or contract to reference if pressed
   - Their three known concerns, with a concise response tied to a recent proof point
   - Any strategic guidance must be phrased as approved talking points, questions to escalate, or CEO decisions needed. Do not draft deep investment strategy for Ingrid.

5. **Anticipated questions and prepared answers** — 5-7 likely questions with bullet-point answers. Include at least two hard ones that require a real answer, not a deflection, such as FERC 2222 timing or the customer concentration trajectory.

6. **Rapport notes** — handled with taste and restraint. Use only rapport notes that are either publicly available or already volunteered. No family or health detail unless directly volunteered in an appropriate context. If Curtis mentions the knee, respond briefly and move on. Yuki's Tesla Energy + Berkeley background is the main technical bridge to Niamh.

7. **Logistics block** — Ingrid's travel from airport, timing, suggested seating, the menu-allergen open item, car service for Northwind, and who handles the check (Sentinel does). Convert any open issue into an owned action with a deadline.

8. **Open items and asks of Ingrid** — keep to items Vanessa cannot resolve alone. If the second term sheet should be mentioned, provide a recommendation and a fallback phrasing, not a long analysis.

9. **Post-dinner follow-up plan** — who sends the thank-you, who schedules the next call (target within 48 hrs), and what gets captured as a follow-up action item.

10. **Action checklist** — a short, practical list of what Vanessa will do before the dinner, after the dinner, and by when. Include the scallop/shellfish issue as an owned action with a named deadline.

Tone: crisp, executive-level. No padding. No adjectives for their own sake. Short paragraphs and dense bullets. Confidential and discreet — rapport notes are strictly informational, never gossip.

## Output format
Single Markdown file, ~2 pages rendered (160-260 lines source). Dense. Use a clear split between the pre-brief and the action checklist so the output feels like a real EA handoff.

## Iteration targets
1. **Compress the at-a-glance** — v1 may sprawl; v2 is genuinely 60-second readable and keeps to the six facts that matter.
2. **Separate facts from guidance** — v1 may blur confirmed details with inference; v2 labels what is known, what is a recommendation, and what still needs Ingrid's judgment.
3. **Make the Q&A materially harder** — include at least one question that forces a substantive reasoning jump, such as what changes if FERC 2222 slips 18 months, and answer it without hiding behind slogans.
4. **Turn logistics into owned actions** — especially the scallop/shellfish issue, which must be assigned to Vanessa with a deadline and a completion path.
5. **Handle the second-term-sheet question explicitly** — v2 should say whether to mention it, how to mention it, and what not to say.
6. **Keep rapport notes bounded** — remove anything that would feel surveilled or over-collected if read aloud.

## Success criteria
- At-a-glance can be scanned in under one minute and contains no more than six facts.
- Confirmed facts are clearly separated from working guidance or inference.
- All six attendees are named with correct titles.
- Each of the three Northwind concerns has a specific response tied to a recent proof point.
- At least one Q&A item requires actual reasoning, not a canned reassurance.
- The shellfish/scallop issue appears as a completed owner-action with a deadline, not an unresolved note.
- Rapport notes would still read as appropriate if the memo were forwarded.
- Logistics are internally consistent (Ingrid: DEN 4:50 -> home 5:45 -> restaurant 6:15, dinner 6:30).
- Tone matches executive-assistant-to-CEO register: no filler, no hedging, no over-explaining.

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: front matter, at-a-glance, attendee dossiers for all three Northwind people, briefing with the three proof-points and three concerns, a first pass at Q&A (4+ items), logistics block, and open items. Tightening Q&A difficulty, separating facts from guidance, and resolving the shellfish/scallop item belong to iteration.

## Example of v1 failure modes
- Inflating the at-a-glance into a full page of prose; Ingrid should be able to absorb it during taxi from Jetway to the car.
- Adding speculative personal detail to rapport notes (e.g., inferring Curtis's views from blog posts rather than citing them).
- Forgetting Yuki entirely because she was added late; she will be the day-to-day on the deal and is the most important long-term relationship.
- Softball Q&A: "What makes Sentinel different?" is not a stress question. "If FERC 2222 enforcement slips by 18 months, what does your revenue model look like?" is.
- Losing the valuation guardrail ($340M floor) in prose rather than bolding it in the at-a-glance.
- Implying that Sentinel should mention the competing term sheets offensively when the better move may be to hold that information as leverage for a second conversation.
- Leaving the scallop-amuse-bouche allergen question as "to follow up" without a time-boxed commitment from Vanessa before the dinner.
- Drafting deep investment strategy for Ingrid; that is CEO/CFO territory. The EA briefing provides context and questions-to-escalate, not answers-to-memorize.

## Common pitfalls to avoid
- Do not confuse Sentinel's 2019 Series A lead with Northwind; Northwind is the Series D prospect.
- Do not describe Curtis's knee injury as a Q&A topic; if he raises it, Ingrid offers a warm one-liner and moves on.
- Do not speculate about Beatrice's view of Sentinel; the only public data point is her critical remarks about GridGuardian (competitor), which may or may not transfer.
- Do not omit the Amit/Niamh preparation note — they need to know Ingrid is carrying the first 10 minutes; they jump in on CFO/CTO questions.
- Do not put the walk-away price in the logistics block; it belongs in the briefing with deliberate framing.
- Do not use bullet points for rapport notes (they read clinical); short prose sentences warm the tone while keeping the register executive.
