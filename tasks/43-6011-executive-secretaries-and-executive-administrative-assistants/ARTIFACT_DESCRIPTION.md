# CEO Briefing Memo — Board Dinner with Northwind Capital Managing Partners, May 6, 2026

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 43-6011
- **Title:** Executive Secretaries and Executive Administrative Assistants

## Scenario
You are Vanessa Okoye, executive assistant to Ingrid Halvorsen, CEO of Sentinel Grid Technologies (a Series C cleantech firm, Boulder, CO, ~220 employees, $48M ARR). On May 6, 2026, Ingrid will host a closed, 6-person dinner at The Flagstaff House in Boulder with two managing partners from Northwind Capital — a growth-equity firm considering leading Sentinel's Series D. The dinner begins at 6:30 PM and is expected to run until 10:00 PM. Northwind requested this dinner after an initial pitch meeting three weeks ago. Ingrid has asked you to produce a pre-brief memo that she will review the afternoon of May 6 on her flight back from a conference in Phoenix.

## Artifact specification
Produce a CEO pre-brief memo with:

1. **Front matter** — TO: Ingrid. FROM: Vanessa. DATE: 2026-05-06. RE: Northwind Dinner, May 6 6:30 PM, Flagstaff House (Aspen Room). CLASSIFICATION: Confidential — CEO Eyes + Amit/Niamh brief only.

2. **At-a-glance (read in 60 seconds)** — venue, time, 6 attendees, 3 Sentinel talking points, 3 known concerns, valuation range to defend.

3. **Attendees** — a short dossier per Northwind person: role, tenure, relevant board seats, recent public positions, personal-rapport hooks (handled tastefully), dietary.

4. **Strategic briefing**
   - Deal context (ask, current range, walk-away)
   - Our three headline proof-points, each with a one-line supporting fact and the primary document/contract to reference if pressed
   - Their three known concerns, with our best current response to each

5. **Anticipated questions and prepared answers** — 5-7 likely questions with bullet-point answers. Include at least one genuinely hard one (e.g., on FERC 2222 exposure or on the customer concentration trajectory).

6. **Rapport notes** — handled with taste: Beatrice's daughter at CU Boulder; Curtis's Stanford-ski connection and current knee recovery (do not ask about the knee directly unless he mentions it; offer warm-but-not-specific well-wishes if he does). Yuki's Tesla Energy + Berkeley background as a technical bridge to Niamh.

7. **Logistics block** — Ingrid's travel from airport, timing, who sits where (suggested seating), the menu-allergen open item (shellfish vs scallop), car service for Northwind, who handles the check (Sentinel does).

8. **Open items and asks of Ingrid** — anything Vanessa cannot resolve alone (e.g., whether to mention the second term sheet, and how).

9. **Post-dinner follow-up plan** — who sends the thank-you, who schedules the next call (target within 48 hrs), how deliverables from dinner are captured.

Tone: crisp, executive-level. No padding. No adjectives for their own sake. Short paragraphs and dense bullets. Confidential and discreet — rapport notes are strictly informational, never gossip.

## Output format
Single Markdown file, ~2 pages rendered (160-260 lines source). Dense.

## Iteration targets
1. **Compress the "at-a-glance"** — v1 may sprawl; v2 is genuinely 60-second readable: 6 facts, 3 points, 3 concerns, 1 number (valuation floor).
2. **Make anticipated Q&A genuinely hard** — v1 may softball; v2 includes a question Ingrid might actually fumble (e.g., "What happens to your business if FERC 2222 enforcement slips 18 months?") with a real answer, not a deflection.
3. **Tune the rapport notes for taste** — remove anything that would feel intrusive if read aloud to the subject; keep only what creates warmth without surveillance feel.
4. **Resolve the scallop/shellfish risk in the logistics block** — specify that you will confirm the menu substitution with the restaurant by a named time today, not leave it floating.
5. **Handle the second-term-sheet question explicitly** — v2 includes a recommendation on whether/how to reference competing term sheets, not a vague note.
6. **Flag what's uncertain honestly** — e.g., Beatrice's historical GridGuardian remarks: mention, but do not assume she'll bring it up.

## Success criteria
- At-a-glance is readable in under one minute.
- All six attendees (not just Ingrid) are named with correct titles.
- All three Northwind concerns have a specific response tied to a recent proof point.
- Dietary conflict (Yuki + scallops) is addressed, not left ambiguous.
- Rapport notes contain nothing that would embarrass the executive if the memo leaked.
- Logistics timeline is internally consistent (Ingrid: DEN 4:50 → home 5:45 → restaurant 6:15, dinner 6:30).
- Tone matches executive-assistant-to-CEO register: no filler, no hedging, no over-explaining.

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: front matter, at-a-glance, attendee dossiers for all three Northwind people, strategic briefing with the three proof-points and three concerns, a first pass at Q&A (4+ items), logistics block, and open items. Tightening Q&A difficulty, refining rapport notes for taste, and resolving the shellfish/scallop item belong to iteration.
