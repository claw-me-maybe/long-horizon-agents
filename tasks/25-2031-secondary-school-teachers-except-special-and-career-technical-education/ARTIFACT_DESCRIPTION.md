# Algebra 2 Unit Plan: Exponential & Logarithmic Functions with CER Modeling Tasks

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 25-2031
- **Title:** Secondary School Teachers, Except Special and Career/Technical Education

## Scenario
Ms. Rivera teaches three sections of Algebra 2 (Grade 10-11) at Jefferson High School, a comprehensive public school of 1,850 students in a working-class suburb. Her target section has 28 learners: 4 with IEPs (three with processing speed accommodations, one with a math-specific SLD goal), 6 multilingual learners at WIDA 3-4 (four Spanish home language, two Vietnamese), and 9 who struggled with the prior quadratics unit (pre-assessment mean 62%, with the lowest cluster on factoring non-monic trinomials). The department is piloting Claim-Evidence-Reasoning (CER) tasks in math as part of a schoolwide disciplinary-literacy initiative led by the instructional coach. State summative assessments are 7 weeks out, and the department is under pressure to raise the percentage of students scoring proficient on exponential-modeling items, which have historically been the lowest cluster on district assessments.

## Inputs
- **Standards (CCSS-Math, High School):**
  - **HSF-IF.C.7e** — Graph exponential and logarithmic functions, showing intercepts and end behavior.
  - **HSF-IF.C.8b** — Use properties of exponents to interpret expressions for exponential functions; classify functions as growth or decay.
  - **HSF-BF.B.5** — Understand the inverse relationship between exponents and logarithms.
  - **HSF-LE.A.4** — Solve `a * b^(ct) = d` where `a`, `c`, and `d` are numbers and the base `b` is 2, 10, or `e`.
  - **HSA-SSE.B.3c** — Use the properties of exponents to transform expressions for exponential functions.
  - Standards for Mathematical Practice (SMP) 1, 3, 4 — Make sense of problems and persevere; Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others; Model with mathematics.
- **Prior unit artifacts:**
  - Quadratics Exit Ticket data (CSV referenced, n=28, mean 62%, lowest cluster on factoring non-monic trinomials and vertex-form transformations).
  - Two students flagged on the IEP Goal Tracker for symbolic-manipulation fluency (goals reference error rate <25% on 2-step algebraic manipulation over 4 consecutive assessments).
  - Prior unit's CER attempt (polynomial end-behavior argument) showed the class struggles to distinguish "evidence" from "restating the problem."
- **Textbook anchor:** Pearson Algebra 2 Common Core (2015), Chapter 7 — teacher must supplement because the textbook lacks contextual modeling tasks and has only one CER-style prompt.
- **Context data:**
  - District pacing guide allocates 13 instructional days (55-min periods).
  - One day already reserved for PSAT administration.
  - Block-schedule Wednesdays give 85-min periods; plan must accommodate both lengths.
  - State summative assessment is 7 weeks after unit end; released items include 2 exponential-modeling tasks per form.
- **Tech available:** Desmos (classroom codes), TI-84 cart (class set), Google Classroom, 1:1 Chromebooks, document camera, interactive whiteboard.
- **CER rubric:** 4-point department rubric already adopted (Claim / Evidence / Reasoning / Mathematical Communication); department-normed exemplars available.
- **Colleague input:** The other Algebra 2 teacher is willing to co-plan one modeling task; the math coach can cover class for one co-observation.

## Artifact specification
Unit plan using **Understanding by Design (UbD) / backwards design** Stages 1-3, plus a **daily lesson sequence**.

Required sections:
1. **Stage 1 — Desired Results**
   - Transfer goals (e.g., "Students will be able to model real-world growth and decay phenomena using exponential and logarithmic functions and argue for the appropriateness of the model.")
   - Enduring understandings (2-3, e.g., "Logarithms and exponents are inverse operations that let us solve problems neither can solve alone.")
   - Essential questions (2-4, open-ended, revisited across the unit).
   - Knowledge and skill targets explicitly mapped to each CCSS standard.
2. **Stage 2 — Assessment Evidence**
   - Summative performance task (choice: carbon-14 dating OR compound-interest investment modeling OR epidemiological early-spread modeling).
   - CER rubric alignment with department rubric, including department-normed exemplars referenced.
   - Formative check per lesson with a named artifact (exit ticket, whiteboard check, Desmos snapshot).
3. **Stage 3 — Learning Plan (WHERETO)**
   - 13-day daily sequence. Each day includes: Learning Target (I-can), Do-Now (5 min, retrieval practice from prior unit or spaced), Mini-Lesson (10-15 min), Guided Practice, Independent or Collaborative Task, Exit Ticket.
   - Indicate which SMPs are foregrounded each day.
   - Plan includes a contingency for the block-schedule Wednesday (what to do with the extra 30 min — Desmos task, mixed practice, or extended CER).
4. **Differentiation plan**
   - Tiered tasks for the 9 struggling learners (anchored tasks, scaffolded tasks, extension).
   - MLL supports (sentence frames for CER, bilingual glossary of `base`, `exponent`, `logarithm`, `decay factor`, `half-life`; partner structures).
   - IEP accommodations (extended time, reduced problem sets, graphic organizers, chunked directions).
   - Clear distinction between accommodation (access) and modification (changed expectation).
5. **Modeling performance task**
   - Full task prompt with authentic data set.
   - Student-facing rubric aligned to CER.
   - Exemplar claim statement and a sample "approaching" response to calibrate peer feedback.
6. **Materials list** and **Desmos / TI-84 activity links (placeholders okay)**.
7. **Home-school bridge**: one short (2-sentence) family-friendly summary per week describing what students are learning and one optional at-home conversation prompt.
8. **Intervention plan for the 9 struggling learners**:
   - Pre-teach vocabulary Monday morning in a 10-minute advisory block.
   - Pair with a thought partner (not just "higher-achieving student"; roles explicit).
   - Targeted re-attempt window on Day 9 with faded scaffolds.
   - Progress-monitoring indicator that connects to IEP goals for the 4 students on IEPs.
9. **Plan for the PSAT-day gap**:
   - Which Day is displaced.
   - What students do independently (asynchronous Desmos activity or recorded mini-lesson) if not displaced.
   - How the teacher re-enters instruction without losing momentum.
10. **Connection to state-assessment prep**:
    - Name at least 3 released items in exponential/log domain and where in the unit they surface as practice.
    - Describe spaced-review plan through the 7-week runway after unit end.

Pedagogical conventions: UbD backwards design; explicit CER scaffolding; retrieval-practice warmups; gradual release (I do / we do / you do); spaced practice across the 7-week runway to state assessments.

**Suggested 13-day arc (for the downstream model's use as a starting frame, not prescriptive):**
- Day 1: Pre-assessment; review properties of exponents; "growth vs. decay" contexts.
- Day 2: Graphing `y = a * b^x` on Desmos; domain/range/asymptote.
- Day 3: Contextual growth problems (population, viral sharing); CER lite.
- Day 4: Decay contexts (medication, radioactive); half-life intuition.
- Day 5: Introduce inverse functions; graph `y = x` reflection.
- Day 6: Logarithm as inverse; evaluate simple logs by inspection.
- Day 7: Common log, natural log, change of base.
- Day 8: Properties of logs (product, quotient, power); error analysis.
- Day 9: Solving exponential equations using logarithms; CER on whether a scenario is exponential.
- Day 10: Compound interest and continuous compounding (e).
- Day 11: Summative modeling task (launch, data, initial model).
- Day 12: Summative modeling task (refinement, written argument).
- Day 13: Gallery walk / peer critique / feedback and reteach loop.

(Note: Day 1 of unit falls on a Wednesday block; one day later is the PSAT gap. Teacher must collapse or adjust.)

## Iteration targets
1. **Sharpen the modeling task** — the v1 modeling task is likely to be either too open (students flounder on "what model is this?") or too closed (fill-in-the-blank). Calibrate scaffolding so it is accessible to the lowest quartile yet non-trivial for top performers. Add an authentic data source and a constraint that forces a modeling decision.
2. **Deepen CER integration** — v1 may bolt CER onto one task; revise so CER reasoning appears in at least 4 of 13 days with varying scaffolding (fully scaffolded on Day 2, partial scaffolds on Days 6 and 9, independent on Day 12). Student-facing stems should show a clear fade.
3. **Align formative assessment to standards** — each exit ticket should clearly target a specific CCSS sub-standard with a decision rule for reteach (e.g., "if <70% on Item 2 (HSF-LE.A.4), spend Day 8 Do-Now on solving exponential equations with like bases").
4. **Differentiation specificity** — replace generic "provide extra support" with named strategies: partial-solutions with error analysis, anchor charts with worked examples, think-alouds modeling metacognition, co-constructed concept maps, and strategic pairings with explicit role cards.
5. **Coherence across days** — ensure concept progression follows: properties of exponents review -> exponential functions and graphs -> exponential growth/decay contexts -> inverse functions -> logarithms as inverses -> log properties -> solving equations with logarithms -> modeling and argument.
6. **Time realism** — verify each 55-min block accounts for transitions, closure, and buffer; 85-min block days need a distinct plan, not just "extended practice".
7. **Retrieval and spacing** — Do-Nows should intentionally recycle content from units 1-3 to support state-test readiness, not only preview the day's content.

## Success criteria
- Every CCSS standard listed is addressed on a named day with a named task.
- Daily plans fit realistic pacing (sum of segment minutes <= period length, including a transition/closure buffer).
- Performance task has a scorable rubric with distinguishable 1/2/3/4 descriptors for each CER dimension, calibrated against the department exemplars.
- Differentiation addresses IEP accommodations, MLL scaffolds, and the struggling quadratics cluster with **distinct** named strategies (not one blanket accommodation repeated).
- At least one task requires students to critique reasoning or construct an argument (SMP 3) — e.g., evaluate a faulty claim about whether a scenario is exponential or linear.
- Exit tickets include a decision rule tying student response to a next-day action.
- The 85-min Wednesday block has a plan that does something pedagogically distinct (not just "double period of the same").
- Summative performance task uses authentic data (not textbook-invented numbers).

## Pedagogical vocabulary glossary (for reference)
- **UbD / backwards design**: Wiggins & McTighe framework where curriculum is planned from desired results back through assessments to learning activities.
- **CER**: Claim-Evidence-Reasoning; a disciplinary writing structure used in math and science to support argumentation.
- **SMPs**: Standards for Mathematical Practice (CCSS) — eight habits of mathematical thinking students should develop.
- **Gradual release**: "I do / We do / You do together / You do alone" sequencing.
- **Retrieval practice**: Low-stakes memory practice at the start of class; builds durable memory (Roediger & Karpicke).
- **Spaced practice**: Spreading practice of a skill across days/weeks rather than massing it.
- **Anchor chart**: A visual, classroom-posted reference co-constructed with students that captures key ideas/rules.
- **WIDA levels**: English-language proficiency levels (1 Entering to 6 Reaching) used to calibrate linguistic scaffolds.
- **Exit ticket**: A brief, targeted formative check completed in the last 3-5 minutes of class used to inform next-day instruction.
- **IEP accommodation vs. modification**: Accommodation changes *how* a student accesses content (extended time, read-aloud); modification changes *what* is expected (reduced scope, different standards).
- **Modeling task**: A problem where the mathematics is not pre-identified; students must decide what to simplify, what to assume, and which tools/functions to apply.
- **Error analysis**: A task where students are shown a worked (incorrect) solution and must identify the error and correct it; supports SMP 3.

## Evaluator notes (for fixture use)
- A strong v1 will name the summative task context (carbon-14, investment, viral spread) and at least sketch the data students will work with. Weak v1 leaves the summative vague.
- A strong v1 will show CER in more than one day, with fading scaffolds. Weak v1 has CER only in the summative.
- Watch for **modeling vs. mimicking**: does the task require students to choose a model, or does it tell them "use `y = a(1.05)^t`"? Iteration should move toward the former.
- Watch for **MLL supports beyond translation**: sentence frames, structured talk, multimodal representations — not only a glossary.
- Watch that **differentiation is per-sub-population** (IEP vs. MLL vs. struggling quadratics cluster), not a single generic list.
- If v1 omits the PSAT day or block-schedule Wednesday, that is a natural iteration target.
- Look for evidence that the teacher has thought about the 7-week runway to state assessments (spacing, review), not just the 13-day unit in isolation.
