# Algebra 2 Unit Plan: Exponential & Logarithmic Functions with CER Modeling Tasks

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 25-2031
- **Title:** Secondary School Teachers, Except Special and Career/Technical Education

## Scenario
Ms. Rivera teaches three sections of Algebra 2 (Grade 10-11) at Jefferson High School, a comprehensive public school of 1,850 students. Her target section has 28 learners: 4 with IEPs (processing speed accommodations), 6 multilingual learners at WIDA 3-4, and 9 who struggled with the prior quadratics unit (pre-assessment mean 62%). The department is piloting Claim-Evidence-Reasoning (CER) tasks in math as part of a schoolwide disciplinary-literacy initiative, and state assessments are 7 weeks out.

## Inputs
- **Standards (CCSS-Math):**
  - HSF-IF.C.7e (graph exponential and logarithmic functions, showing intercepts and end behavior)
  - HSF-BF.B.5 (inverse relationship between exponents and logarithms)
  - HSF-LE.A.4 (solve `a * b^(ct) = d` using logarithms)
  - HSA-SSE.B.3c (rewrite exponential expressions to reveal rate of growth/decay)
  - Standards for Mathematical Practice 1, 3, 4 (perseverance, argumentation, modeling)
- **Prior unit artifacts:** Quadratics Exit Ticket data (attached CSV, mean 62%, lowest cluster on factoring non-monic trinomials); two students flagged on IEP Goal Tracker for symbolic-manipulation fluency.
- **Textbook anchor:** Pearson Algebra 2 Common Core (2015), Chapter 7 — but teacher must supplement because textbook lacks modeling tasks.
- **Context data:** District pacing guide allocates 13 instructional days (55-min periods); one day already reserved for PSAT; block-schedule Wednesdays (85 min).
- **Tech available:** Desmos (classroom codes), TI-84 cart, Google Classroom, a Chromebook per student.
- **CER rubric:** 4-point department rubric already adopted (Claim / Evidence / Reasoning / Mathematical Communication).

## Artifact specification
Unit plan using **Understanding by Design (UbD) / backwards design** Stages 1-3, plus a **daily lesson sequence**.

Required sections:
1. **Stage 1 — Desired Results**: transfer goals, enduring understandings, essential questions, knowledge/skill targets mapped to each CCSS standard.
2. **Stage 2 — Assessment Evidence**: summative performance task (carbon-14 dating OR compound-interest investment modeling), CER rubric alignment, formative checks per lesson.
3. **Stage 3 — Learning Plan (WHERETO)**: 13-day daily sequence. Each day includes Learning Target (I-can), Do-Now (5 min, retrieval practice), Mini-Lesson (10-15 min), Guided Practice, Independent/Collaborative Task, Exit Ticket.
4. **Differentiation plan**: tiered tasks for the 9 struggling learners, MLL supports (sentence frames for CER, bilingual glossary of `base`, `exponent`, `logarithm`, `decay factor`), IEP accommodations (extended time, reduced problem sets, graphic organizers).
5. **Modeling performance task**: full task prompt, student-facing rubric, exemplar claim statement.
6. **Materials list** and **Desmos activity links (placeholders okay)**.

Pedagogical conventions: UbD backwards design; explicit CER scaffolding; retrieval-practice warmups; gradual release (I do / we do / you do).

## Output format
Single Markdown file, ~1,800-2,500 words. Tables for daily sequence and assessment map. No graphics required.

## Iteration targets
1. **Sharpen the modeling task** — current draft likely too open or too closed; calibrate scaffolding so it is accessible to the lowest quartile yet non-trivial for top performers.
2. **Deepen CER integration** — v1 may bolt CER onto one task; revise so CER reasoning appears in at least 4 of 13 days with varying scaffolding.
3. **Align formative assessment to standards** — each exit ticket should clearly target a specific CCSS sub-standard with a decision rule for reteach.
4. **Differentiation specificity** — replace generic "provide extra support" with named strategies (e.g., anchor charts, think-alouds, partial-solutions with error analysis).
5. **Coherence across days** — ensure concept progression: properties of exponents -> exponential functions -> inverses -> logarithms -> log properties -> solving equations -> modeling.
6. **Time realism** — verify each 55-min block accounts for transitions, closure, and buffer.

## Success criteria
- Every CCSS standard listed is addressed on a named day with a named task.
- Daily plans fit realistic pacing (sum of segment minutes <= period length).
- Performance task has a scorable rubric with distinguishable 1/2/3/4 descriptors for each CER dimension.
- Differentiation addresses IEP accommodations, MLL scaffolds, and the struggling quadratics cluster with **different** named strategies (not just one blanket accommodation).
- At least one task requires students to critique reasoning or construct an argument (MP3).
- Exit tickets include a decision rule ("if <70% correct on item 2, reteach X tomorrow").

## Scope target
In 15 minutes, v1 should include: complete Stage 1 (transfer/understandings/EQs), draft summative task prompt, a 13-day daily skeleton with learning targets named, and a first pass at differentiation listed per-day. CER integration, exit-ticket decision rules, and fully articulated rubric descriptors are expected to be rough and become primary iteration targets.
