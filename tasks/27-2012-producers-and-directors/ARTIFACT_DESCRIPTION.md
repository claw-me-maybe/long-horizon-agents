# Shot List + Director's Notes: "Ridgeline" Pilot, Episode 101, Scene 14

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 27-2012
- **Title:** Producers and Directors

## Scenario
You are the director of the pilot episode of **"Ridgeline,"** an 8-episode limited drama series greenlit by FX on Hulu, shooting in and around Missoula, Montana. Showrunner is Leigh Harada (her follow-up after "The Glass Orchard"); cinematographer is Bradford Oyelaran, ASC; the pilot shoots July 6–August 3, 2026. You are prepping Scene 14 of Episode 101: a late-dusk confrontation at a remote cattle-dip vat between Sheriff Eleanor "Nora" Chilcott (played by Regina King) and her estranged brother Ray (played by Jesse Plemons), who she suspects of arson. The scene is six pages, emotionally the pilot's hinge, and shoots on Day 6 (July 13), a single-day location with magic-hour window anxiety. Production designer needs your shot list by April 28, 2026 — tomorrow — to finalize the vat-set build and the prop cattle-dip rig; 1st AD Keoni Takahashi needs it for schedule locks.

## Inputs
Materials you're working from:
- Script Scene 14 (6 pp), blocked: scene opens with Ray fixing the vat pump, Nora arrives on horseback, dialogue across the dip vat, beat where Ray descends into the empty vat to retrieve a dropped glove, the accusation/denial, Nora rides off; beat ends on Ray alone at the vat as night falls.
- Location: "Kicking Horse Ranch," 47 miles NE of Missoula; single approach road; magic hour in early July is ~8:55-9:40 PM local; sunset 9:14 PM.
- Stunt/safety note: the vat is a functional-looking but empty concrete trough (dressed), 8 ft x 24 ft x 4 ft deep; Plemons does his own descent via a welded steel ladder — no stunt double for that action; coverage must protect the reveal that the vat is empty, not full.
- Cast schedule: Regina King available until 10:15 PM (hard out, nursing infant back at Missoula basecamp).
- Equipment: one A-cam (ARRI Alexa 35), one B-cam (Alexa Mini LF), one Ronin stabilizer, one 30ft jib, one Easyrig, three lenses locked in (Cooke S7/i: 32mm, 50mm, 75mm). No drone this day (wind/FAA window).
- Showrunner note from last week's tone meeting: "I want this scene to feel like a John Huston western that happens to be shot in a grief drama. Wide. Patient. The vat should scare me before anyone says anything scary."
- Horse wrangler needs 4 hours for Regina's horse acclimation; Regina is a confident rider but not stunt-trained.
- Coverage constraint: the scene must play at 6 pages in 14 shooting hours max, with a 30-minute meal break, a 90-minute magic-hour window you want to hit for 3 specific shots, and a hard cast out.

## Artifact specification
Produce a **shot list + director's notes document** with the following structure:
1. **Scene header** — scene number, script pages, location, day/night, interior/exterior, time of day in story.
2. **Scene intention** (2-3 paragraphs) — what this scene does for the pilot, emotional arc beats, the one image that must exist in the cut.
3. **Blocking sketch** — a text + ASCII top-down of the vat and the actors' positions through the scene's beats. Label positions A/B/C/D with actions.
4. **Shot list table** — ordered shots with: Shot #, Size (WS/MS/CU etc.), Subject, Movement, Lens, Camera, Beat Covered (script line reference), Notes, Expected setup time. Include at least one oner/developing shot, at least one insert, and the magic-hour-dependent shots clearly marked.
5. **Magic hour priority list** — which 3 shots must happen in the window; fallback if weather/schedule fails; what can be stolen later.
6. **Director's notes by department** — short, specific notes to DP, production designer, costume, sound, stunts, cast. These are not script-notes; they are execution-specific.
7. **Shooting order rationale** — a short paragraph defending the order (lighting continuity, actor energy, chase-the-light pressure, etc.).
8. **Risk register** — 4-6 risks with mitigations (horse behavior, light loss, Regina's hard out, vat emptiness revealed too early in coverage, etc.).
9. **Scene cut-for-time plan** — if you're running behind at 7 PM, which 2 shots do you sacrifice, and what does the cut survive with?

Voice: declarative, operational. Directors' notes should be specific enough that the right decision is made in your absence ("the 50mm, Plemons-side, at vat-lip height, 2 inches below his jawline" — not "something intimate here"). Acknowledge Leigh Harada's tone note in at least one concrete shot choice, by name or by allusion to the Huston reference.

## Output format
Single markdown file. Shot list in GFM table format (may split into Act I / Act II if long). Blocking sketch in fenced code block with ASCII. Rough length: 250-450 lines.

## Iteration targets
1. The scene intention must commit to one image that has to exist in the cut — cut hedging, pick.
2. Every shot on the list should be justifiable by beat — if a shot doesn't cover a specific line or action, defend it or drop it.
3. Magic-hour priority list needs a fallback that doesn't rely on a second day — this location pickup is expensive.
4. The empty-vat reveal must be managed in coverage — the shot list should mark the shots that must not over-show the vat's interior before the descent.
5. Director's notes by department should be uneven on purpose — DP gets the most, costume gets the least.
6. Risk register must include at least one risk you'd rather not mention (e.g., Regina's hard out vs. Plemons's habit of extending takes).

## Success criteria
- The 1st AD can build a day-out-of-days schedule and a shooting order from this document without another conversation.
- Scene intention is specific enough that the editor, reading only this page, understands the emotional hinge.
- Shot count is realistic for 14 hours (experienced heuristic: 22-32 setups for a 6-page dialogue-heavy scene is defensible; above 40 is ambitious).
- At least one developing/oner shot is specified — not just coverage.
- Tone note from the showrunner is visibly honored in at least one shot choice with explicit rationale.
- Document is unafraid of specificity: lens lengths named, camera positions named, beat references attached.

## Scope target
A 15-minute v1 should include: scene header, scene intention (possibly rough), the blocking sketch (ASCII), a shot list of 14-20 shots (not yet the full 25-30), a magic hour priority paragraph, and 2-3 department notes. Missing: risk register, cut-for-time plan, the full shot complement, and polish on shooting-order rationale.
