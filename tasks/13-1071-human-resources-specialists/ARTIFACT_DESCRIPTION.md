# Senior Clinical Research Coordinator Job Description — Marrowbrook Health Sciences

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 13-1071
- **Title:** Human Resources Specialists

## Scenario
Marrowbrook Health Sciences, a 1,100-person clinical research organization in Research Triangle Park, NC, is converting its 12 contract Clinical Research Coordinators (CRCs) into full-time "Senior CRC" roles after losing a multi-site Phase III oncology study in March 2026 due to coordinator turnover. HR Business Partner Dari Okafor has been asked by the Head of Clinical Operations, Dr. Wren Alcántara, to produce a full job description package that HR, Legal, Talent Acquisition, Compensation, and the hiring managers can all sign off on by May 2, 2026, so recruiting can open Requisition MBH-2026-CLIN-042 on May 5.

## Inputs

**Intake meeting notes (Dr. Alcántara, 4/10/26)**
- Current CRCs burn out at ~14 months; goal tenure is 3+ years.
- Wants people who can run 2 oncology trials simultaneously, including ones with CAR-T protocols.
- "Not negotiable: SoCRA or ACRP certification, or sitting for it within 12 months."
- Salary band from Comp team: $86,000–$112,000, 10% target bonus, 0.05% equity.
- Role reports to Clinical Ops Manager; dotted line to Medical Monitor on assigned studies.
- Location: Hybrid, 3 days on-site in RTP campus.

**Inputs from other functions**
- Legal: must meet NC wage-transparency posting rules (salary range on posting); EEO language from corporate template v3.2; no non-compete language for NC-based roles.
- Compensation: the role must map to Career Framework Level P3 ("Senior Individual Contributor, Specialized").
- Talent Acquisition: the last req got 412 applicants but only 6 phone-screen-qualified; the JD had 38 bullet points and was described as "a wall of text."
- DEI: requires skills-first language; no "rockstar," "ninja," "native speaker of X."
- Clinical Ops: must include 21 CFR Part 11 and ICH-GCP E6(R3) familiarity.

**Competitor postings scanned** (sent by TA for tone-calibration; excerpts)
- IQVIA: 14 bullets, 340 words, opens with trial-type specifics
- Parexel: 22 bullets, 510 words, very compliance-heavy
- Labcorp Drug Development: 18 bullets, 380 words, strong mission statement

## Artifact specification
Complete job description package. Required sections in order:
1. **Posting header** — title, req ID, location and hybrid designation, pay range per NC law, FLSA status
2. **About Marrowbrook** (3–4 sentences; not boilerplate — reference the oncology-study franchise)
3. **What you'll do** — max 8 outcome-oriented bullets, verb-led
4. **What you'll bring** — separated into "Required" (≤6) and "Preferred" (≤5), skills-first phrasing
5. **Certifications and regulatory** — SoCRA/ACRP; Part 11; ICH-GCP E6(R3)
6. **How we work** — hybrid expectations, on-call coverage for active trials, travel (up to 15%)
7. **Compensation and benefits** — NC-compliant pay range disclosure, bonus, equity, medical day-one
8. **EEO / accommodations statement** (use corporate template v3.2, do not rewrite)
9. **Internal-only addendum** (not posted): success profile, interview loop plan, competency weightings for scorecard

Tone: professional, candidate-magnetizing, but technically precise for a regulated clinical role. Inclusive language per DEI guidance.

## Output format
Markdown file containing both the external posting and the internal addendum, clearly delimited. Target 950–1,400 words total.

## Iteration targets
1. v1 "What you'll do" likely exceeds 8 bullets or is task-listy rather than outcome-oriented; tighten to outcomes like "Serve as site lead on 2 concurrent oncology protocols."
2. Required qualifications usually have 3–4 "nice-to-haves" smuggled in; move them to Preferred.
3. Pay-range disclosure must be visible on the posting itself (not in a footer) to meet NC law — v1 often buries it.
4. The About section tends to be generic; add a concrete reference to Marrowbrook's oncology franchise or the specific pain point that motivated this role.
5. The internal addendum's competency weightings often sum to 100% unevenly or duplicate competencies — tighten.
6. Scan for DEI-flagged terms and bias ("proven track record," "fast-paced," "10+ years experience") and replace with skill-based phrasing.

## Success criteria
- A qualified CRC reading the posting in 60 seconds can decide whether to apply.
- Every external bullet would survive Legal, DEI, and hiring-manager review without edit.
- The internal addendum gives a recruiter enough to build a structured interview loop without re-interviewing the hiring manager.
- Pay range, location, and certifications are unambiguous on the first screen.
- The role is clearly distinguishable from a standard CRC (not a retitle) — seniority signals are concrete (scope, autonomy, mentoring).
- Posting avoids "wall of text" — visual hierarchy is usable on mobile.

## Scope target
v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: complete posting header with pay range, drafted About section, 6–8 outcome bullets for "What you'll do," a split Required/Preferred list, certifications paragraph, compensation/benefits block, and at least the skeleton of the internal addendum (success profile and interview loop). Roughly 700–900 words; EEO template and full competency weighting tuning are iteration work.
