# Go-To-Market Plan: Verdica Pro Launch into the US Landscape Contractor Segment

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 11-2021
- **Title:** Marketing Managers

## Scenario
Verdica, a $72M consumer lawn-care brand historically sold DTC and in big-box retail, is launching Verdica Pro — a concentrated, commercial-grade SKU line — into the US independent landscape contractor (ILC) segment for Spring 2027 (retail ship March 1, 2027). Marketing Director Sofia Marchetti must deliver the GTM plan to the CMO, Raj Bhattacharya, by November 21, 2026, in advance of the December 10 launch-readiness review. The segment is currently dominated by SiteOne Professional and Ewing Outdoor; Verdica has strong consumer brand equity but no pro-channel credibility. Launch budget is $2.8M across Feb-Sep 2027.

## Inputs
The model starts with:

- **Product:** Three SKUs — Verdica Pro Turf Builder (2.5-gal concentrate), Verdica Pro Weed Control Plus (32oz concentrate), Verdica Pro Grub Shield (granular, 40-lb bag). MSRP: $84.99, $46.99, $62.99 respectively. Pro dealer margin: 28%. Consumer-brand equivalents are roughly 60% of these prices.
- **Segment sizing:**
  - US independent landscape contractors: ~38,000 firms, median crew size 4, median annual material spend $48K.
  - Buying pattern: 71% purchase through pro dealers (Ewing, SiteOne, Horizon); 19% direct from manufacturer; 10% big-box.
  - Key purchase drivers (per qualitative research, n=34 contractors): 1) product performance at scale, 2) dealer availability in their region, 3) consistent pricing, 4) tech rep/agronomist support, 5) brand the customer recognizes.
- **Channel landscape:**
  - Target dealer partners (Tier 1, 60 locations total): Horizon Distributors, Winfield United Professional, Regional co-op Greenleaf Northwest.
  - SiteOne (dominant; 700+ branches) has declined Verdica Pro carry citing overlap with existing private-label.
  - Ewing Outdoor (460+ branches) is in negotiation; likely 120 branches agreed to stock by Spring.
- **Competitive positioning:**
  - SiteOne Professional: breadth + convenience; commodity + private-label pricing.
  - Ewing Outdoor: relationship-driven; agronomic expertise.
  - Helena Pro / Harrell's: agronomy-first; premium positioning.
- **Verdica's credibility problem:** Consumer brand perceived as "homeowner quality." 62% of surveyed contractors said they "would not stock a homeowner brand in front of a commercial customer."
- **Budget:** $2.8M = dealer co-op/MDF $800K, field marketing/events $650K, content & digital $520K, trial programs $480K, PR & influencer $180K, brand campaign $170K.
- **Team:** 1 segment marketing manager (hiring), 2 field marketing reps (existing, consumer-oriented), 1 digital marketer, 1 PR specialist. Sales org has no dedicated pro reps yet.

## Artifact specification
A launch GTM plan with the following sections:
1. **Executive summary** — segment opportunity, 12-month launch goals (revenue, distribution, awareness), $2.8M budget allocation at a glance, top 3 risks.
2. **Segment & buyer** — ILC market sizing, segmentation into 3-4 sub-segments (e.g., turf-focused, full-service, mosquito/lawn-health specialists) with prioritization, buyer journey map and unit economics.
3. **Positioning** — a one-paragraph positioning statement (For X, who Y, Verdica Pro is Z that unlike competitors, offers W). Must address the "homeowner-brand" credibility objection directly. Plus 3-5 proof points.
4. **Product & pricing** — MSRP vs. dealer-laid-in, competitive price ladder (Verdica Pro vs. SiteOne private label vs. Helena), pricing rationale.
5. **Channel strategy** — Tier 1 dealer plan (Horizon, Winfield, Greenleaf NW), the Ewing negotiation, the SiteOne workaround (e.g., direct-to-contractor program for SiteOne-dominant regions), and dealer enablement (POS, training, co-op).
6. **Marketing mix by objective** — for three objectives (Awareness, Trial, Repeat), show the tactics, spend, and success metrics. Include: content plan (5-8 asset types), field events (trade shows: GIE+EXPO, Equip Expo; regional clinics), digital (search + paid social + channel partner co-marketing), PR, influencer (named pro-landscaper creators).
7. **Trial & conversion program** — sample/discount structure to get first-use, follow-up mechanics to drive second purchase. Include unit economics (cost per trial, trial-to-buy conversion assumption, LTV).
8. **Launch calendar (Feb-Sep 2027)** — month-by-month major beats: dealer training, trade shows, PR embargo, brand campaign flight, first-order incentive windows.
9. **KPIs & dashboard** — leading (dealer branch count, training completions, sample orders) and lagging (sell-through by dealer, # active buying contractors, revenue, repeat rate). Include 90-day, 180-day, 365-day targets.
10. **Risks & dependencies** — including the SiteOne dependency, the pro-rep hiring, and the credibility risk.

Tone: commercial, specific, sales-friendly. Written as if it will be used by sales to align partners, not just by marketing internally.

## Output format
Markdown document with embedded tables, 7-10 pages. Positioning statement, price ladder, marketing-mix-by-objective table, and launch calendar are the key visual anchors.

## Iteration targets
1. **Get the positioning statement right** — v1 tends to be generic ("premium performance for pros"). Iterate to a statement that explicitly addresses the homeowner-brand objection. (E.g., "For landscape contractors running 3-10 person crews, who need consistent performance across 50+ properties per week, Verdica Pro delivers [specific performance claim backed by spec]; unlike SiteOne private label, every SKU is formulated for commercial application rates...")
2. **Quantify the trial program unit economics** — v1 often lists a sampling program without math. Iterate to: cost per sample-kit, targeted contractor count, expected trial-to-buy conversion (and basis for assumption), CAC, payback period.
3. **Make the SiteOne workaround specific** — v1 may say "focus on alternative dealers." Iterate to a named direct-to-contractor program with an e-commerce or field-rep mechanic, geographic prioritization (regions where SiteOne has < 25% share), and incremental spend.
4. **Tighten the KPI dashboard** — v1 lists 15 metrics. Iterate to 5-7, clearly split leading vs. lagging, with numeric 90/180/365-day targets.
5. **Fix the spend allocation logic** — v1 spreads budget evenly. Iterate to over-weight trial and dealer enablement in first 120 days, then rotate to brand campaign as dealer distribution builds.
6. **Connect the pro-rep hiring dependency into the plan** — v1 may not address it. A stronger version acknowledges no dedicated pro sales reps and proposes either a dealer-led model or an interim channel-marketing specialist.

## Success criteria
(For evaluator use only.)
1. The positioning statement is a specific, testable claim — not a tagline — and directly counters the homeowner-brand objection.
2. Trial program has stated unit economics with at least three numbers: sample cost, target contractor count, trial-to-buy %.
3. Channel strategy explicitly addresses the SiteOne declination (named workaround), not just "focus on Horizon and Ewing."
4. Budget allocation across $2.8M reconciles to the stated categories (co-op $800K, etc.) and demonstrates a time-phased shift (e.g., trial-heavy Q1, brand-heavy Q3).
5. Launch calendar names at least two industry events (GIE+EXPO, Equip Expo, or similar) with specific dates.
6. KPI dashboard has clearly separated leading vs. lagging metrics, at least 5 items total with 12-month numeric targets.

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: executive summary with 12-month revenue target, segment sizing, a draft positioning statement (even if weak on objection-handling), channel strategy naming Tier 1 dealers and the SiteOne situation, a spend-allocation breakdown summing to $2.8M, and a KPI list with 90/180/365-day targets. Calendar month-by-month detail and trial-program unit economics are iteration targets.
