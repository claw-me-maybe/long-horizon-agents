# Go-To-Market Plan: Verdica Pro Launch into the US Landscape Contractor Segment

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 11-2021
- **Title:** Marketing Managers

## Scenario
Verdica, a $72M consumer lawn-care brand historically sold DTC and in big-box retail (Home
Depot, Lowe's, Ace Hardware), is launching Verdica Pro — a concentrated, commercial-grade
SKU line — into the US independent landscape contractor (ILC) segment for Spring 2027
(retail ship March 1, 2027). Marketing Director Sofia Marchetti must deliver the GTM plan
to the CMO, Raj Bhattacharya, by November 21, 2026, in advance of the December 10
launch-readiness review. The segment is currently dominated by SiteOne Professional and
Ewing Outdoor; Verdica has strong consumer brand equity but no pro-channel credibility.
Launch budget is $2.8M across Feb-Sep 2027. The plan will be reviewed jointly by Sales
(VP Sales: Carlos Rincon) and Supply Chain (VP: Nina Akande) because channel commitments
drive forecasting.

## Inputs
The model starts with:

### Product
Three SKUs at launch:
- Verdica Pro Turf Builder: 2.5-gal concentrate; MSRP $84.99; dealer laid-in $47.79 (44%
  off-list margin); 28% dealer margin at MSRP
- Verdica Pro Weed Control Plus: 32oz concentrate; MSRP $46.99; dealer laid-in $26.41;
  28% dealer margin
- Verdica Pro Grub Shield: 40-lb granular bag; MSRP $62.99; dealer laid-in $35.41; 28%
  dealer margin

Consumer-brand equivalents are roughly 60% of these prices at MSRP (Verdica Lawn Food at
$49.99, etc.).

### Segment sizing
- US independent landscape contractors: ~38,000 firms; median crew size 4; median annual
  material spend $48K
- Total segment TAM: ~$3.2B in chemicals/fertilizers alone
- Buying pattern: 71% purchase through pro dealers (Ewing, SiteOne, Horizon, independent
  co-ops); 19% direct from manufacturer; 10% big-box
- Key purchase drivers (per qualitative research, n=34 contractors, conducted August 2026):
  1. Product performance at scale (rated 4.7/5 in importance)
  2. Dealer availability in their region (4.5/5)
  3. Consistent pricing and availability (4.3/5)
  4. Tech rep / agronomist support (4.1/5)
  5. Brand the commercial customer recognizes (3.8/5)
- Contractor size distribution: 48% are 1-3 crew, 31% are 4-8 crew, 21% are 9+ crew

### Channel landscape
- **Target Tier 1 dealer partners (60 locations total):**
  - Horizon Distributors: 27 locations; already engaged; verbal agreement to carry all 3 SKUs
  - Winfield United Professional: 14 locations; negotiations in progress
  - Regional co-op Greenleaf Northwest: 19 locations; MOU signed Oct 2026
- **SiteOne (dominant, 700+ branches):** Has declined Verdica Pro carry citing overlap
  with their existing LESCO private-label. Dominant in: Southeast (62% share), Texas (54%),
  Florida (71%). Where Verdica must find a workaround.
- **Ewing Outdoor (460+ branches):** In active negotiation; likely 120 branches agreed
  to stock by Spring. Strong in Midwest and California.
- **Direct-to-contractor (e-commerce + field rep):** Currently no infrastructure; opportunity
  in SiteOne-dominant regions.

### Competitive positioning
- **SiteOne Professional:** Breadth + convenience; commodity + private-label pricing;
  relationship-driven but transactional in dense metros.
- **Ewing Outdoor:** Relationship-driven; agronomic expertise; smaller than SiteOne but
  more premium positioning.
- **Helena Pro / Harrell's:** Agronomy-first; premium positioning; ~20% market share
  combined among mid-to-large contractors.
- **Verdica's credibility problem:** Consumer brand perceived as "homeowner quality."
  62% of surveyed contractors said they "would not stock a homeowner brand in front of a
  commercial customer." The brand name itself is a liability to overcome.

### Budget allocation (total $2.8M Feb-Sep 2027)
- Dealer co-op / MDF: $800K
- Field marketing / events / trade shows: $650K
- Content & digital: $520K
- Trial programs (sample kits, first-order incentives): $480K
- PR & influencer: $180K
- Brand campaign (late-launch, May-Sep): $170K

### Team
- 1 segment marketing manager (currently open rec; targeted hire Jan 2027)
- 2 field marketing reps (existing, consumer-oriented; need retraining)
- 1 digital marketer (shared with consumer business)
- 1 PR specialist
- Sales org has no dedicated pro reps yet — a key constraint

### Risks flagged by Sales
- No pro reps means dealer conversations fall to regional consumer-channel AMs who don't
  have pro credibility
- The Helix Motion... wait, wrong scenario. The actual risk: 45-day dealer stocking
  window starting Dec 15; any delay in POS materials or dealer training blows through it

## Artifact specification
A launch GTM plan with the following sections:

1. **Executive summary** — segment opportunity, 12-month launch goals (revenue, # dealers,
   # active buying contractors, awareness), $2.8M budget allocation at a glance, top 3
   risks.
2. **Segment & buyer** — ILC market sizing, segmentation into 3-4 sub-segments (e.g.,
   turf-focused firms, full-service landscape, mosquito/lawn-health specialists, design-
   build with maintenance) with prioritization, buyer journey map, and unit economics
   (contractor LTV, payback on trial).
3. **Positioning** — a one-paragraph positioning statement in the form "For X, who Y,
   Verdica Pro is Z that unlike competitors, offers W." Must address the "homeowner-brand"
   credibility objection directly. Plus 3-5 proof points (performance spec, agronomist
   support, case study, packaging differentiation, formulation concentration vs. consumer
   SKU).
4. **Product & pricing** — MSRP vs. dealer laid-in, competitive price ladder (Verdica Pro
   vs. SiteOne LESCO private label vs. Helena Pro vs. Ewing specialty), pricing rationale
   and guardrails.
5. **Channel strategy** — Tier 1 dealer plan (Horizon, Winfield, Greenleaf NW), the Ewing
   negotiation sequence, the SiteOne workaround (e.g., direct-to-contractor program for
   SiteOne-dominant regions), and dealer enablement (POS, training, co-op spend allocation,
   rep-commission terms).
6. **Marketing mix by objective** — for three objectives (Awareness, Trial, Repeat), show
   the tactics, spend, and success metrics. Include: content plan (5-8 asset types: how-to
   videos, spec sheets, agronomy comparison, case studies, calculator tools), field events
   (trade shows: GIE+EXPO October, Equip Expo October, Landscapes Live conference January),
   digital (search + paid social + channel partner co-marketing), PR, and influencer (named
   pro-landscaper creators like Stanley "Dirt Monkey" Genadek or regional equivalents).
7. **Trial & conversion program** — sample/discount structure to get first-use, follow-up
   mechanics to drive second purchase. Include unit economics: cost per trial kit (~$45),
   target contractor count (5,000), trial-to-buy conversion assumption (18-22%), CAC, LTV
   at 2-year horizon, payback period.
8. **Launch calendar (Feb-Sep 2027)** — month-by-month major beats: dealer training (Feb),
   trade shows (GIE+EXPO Oct, though technically outside window — pre-launch press prep
   needed), PR embargo lift (March 1), brand campaign flight (May-Sep), first-order
   incentive window (Mar-Apr).
9. **KPIs & dashboard** — leading (dealer branch count, training completions, sample
   orders, sample-to-order conversion) and lagging (sell-through by dealer, # active
   buying contractors, revenue, repeat rate, channel share). Include 90-day, 180-day,
   365-day targets.
10. **Risks & dependencies** — including the SiteOne dependency, the pro-rep hiring, the
    45-day dealer-stocking window, and the credibility risk.

Tone: commercial, specific, sales-friendly. Written as if it will be used by sales to
align partners, not just by marketing internally. Avoid marketing jargon ("drive awareness"
without a specific tactic, "optimize conversion" without a number).

## Output format
Markdown document with embedded tables, 7-10 pages. Positioning statement, price ladder,
marketing-mix-by-objective table, and launch calendar are the key visual anchors.

## Iteration targets
1. **Get the positioning statement right** — v1 tends to be generic ("premium performance
   for pros"). Iterate to a statement that explicitly addresses the homeowner-brand
   objection. (E.g., "For landscape contractors running 3-10 person crews, who need
   consistent performance across 50+ properties per week, Verdica Pro delivers
   commercial-grade concentration (2x actives vs. consumer line) in a dealer-only
   trade-dress that signals to your customer this is pro-only product; unlike SiteOne's
   LESCO private label, every Verdica Pro SKU has 24/7 agronomist support and a
   performance-guarantee program.")
2. **Quantify the trial program unit economics** — v1 often lists a sampling program
   without math. Iterate to: cost per sample kit ($45), targeted contractor count (5,000
   top-tier ILCs), expected trial-to-buy conversion (~20% based on category norms and
   Verdica consumer-to-pro conversion in limited test), CAC (~$225), LTV at 2-year horizon
   (~$8,400 for median 4-crew ILC), payback period, and cost to achieve.
3. **Make the SiteOne workaround specific** — v1 may say "focus on alternative dealers."
   Iterate to a named direct-to-contractor program with (a) an e-commerce or field-rep
   mechanic, (b) geographic prioritization (regions where SiteOne has < 40% share:
   Pacific Northwest, Upper Midwest, Northeast), and (c) incremental budget ($220K of the
   $800K co-op, reallocated toward DTC promotional spend in SiteOne-dominant regions).
4. **Tighten the KPI dashboard** — v1 lists 15 metrics. Iterate to 5-7, clearly split
   leading vs. lagging, with numeric 90/180/365-day targets. Leading examples: "#
   contractor accounts activated in dealer POS by Apr 30: 1,200." Lagging: "Retail
   sell-through revenue Aug 31, 2027: $4.2M."
5. **Fix the spend allocation logic** — v1 spreads budget evenly across months. Iterate
   to over-weight trial and dealer enablement in first 120 days (Feb-May), then rotate
   to brand campaign as dealer distribution builds. A visual spend curve table by month
   makes the logic obvious.
6. **Connect the pro-rep hiring dependency into the plan** — v1 may not address it. A
   stronger version acknowledges no dedicated pro sales reps and proposes either (a) a
   dealer-led model with tight marketing support, or (b) an interim channel-marketing
   specialist hire who does both, or (c) a flag to Sales that 3 named pro reps need to
   be hired by Feb 15 or launch slips.
7. **Address the 45-day dealer stocking window** — v1 may not. Iterate to a named
   critical path with POS materials delivered by Dec 20, training by Jan 15, first-order
   inventory by Feb 10.

## Success criteria
(For evaluator use only.)
1. The positioning statement is a specific, testable claim — not a tagline — and directly
   counters the homeowner-brand objection (e.g., explicit reference to pro-only packaging,
   agronomy support, or concentration difference).
2. Trial program has stated unit economics with at least three numbers: sample cost,
   target contractor count, trial-to-buy %.
3. Channel strategy explicitly addresses the SiteOne declination (named workaround), not
   just "focus on Horizon and Ewing." Includes geographic prioritization.
4. Budget allocation across $2.8M reconciles to the stated categories (co-op $800K, etc.)
   and demonstrates a time-phased shift (e.g., trial-heavy Q1, brand-heavy Q3).
5. Launch calendar names at least two industry events (GIE+EXPO, Equip Expo, Landscapes
   Live) with specific dates/months.
6. KPI dashboard has clearly separated leading vs. lagging metrics, at least 5 items
   total with 12-month numeric targets.
7. Pro-rep hiring dependency is named and addressed (not ignored).

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: executive summary with 12-month revenue target,
segment sizing, a draft positioning statement (even if weak on objection-handling),
channel strategy naming Tier 1 dealers and the SiteOne situation, a spend-allocation
breakdown summing to $2.8M, and a KPI list with 90/180/365-day targets. Calendar
month-by-month detail, trial-program unit economics, and pro-rep dependency flag are
iteration targets for v2/v3. By v3, the positioning statement should be testable and the
SiteOne workaround should include named regions and a specific DTC mechanic.
