# 2026-2027 School Improvement Plan — Ridgeway Middle School, Targeting Math Proficiency Gap

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 11-9032
- **Title:** Education Administrators, Kindergarten through Secondary

## Scenario
Ridgeway Middle School (grades 6-8, enrollment 642) in the Millcrest Valley Unified School
District has been designated as a "Targeted Support and Improvement (TSI)" school under
ESSA after three consecutive years of Black and Hispanic student math proficiency gaps
exceeding 25 percentage points vs. white peers. Principal Dr. Kenneth Osei must submit a
2026-2027 School Improvement Plan (SIP) to the Superintendent, Dr. Lydia Brockman, and the
School Board by July 15, 2026, to be implemented starting with the August 19, 2026 teacher
prep week. The state DOE (Department of Education) will review the plan against the
evidence-based intervention requirements of ESSA Section 1111(d). The plan will also be
shared with the Ridgeway Parent Advisory Council and the local Equity & Access Coalition.

## Inputs
The model starts with:

### 2025-2026 Smarter Balanced math proficiency rates (% Meets/Exceeds)
- Grade 6 schoolwide: 51% (state avg 54%)
- Grade 7 schoolwide: 47% (state avg 51%)
- Grade 8 schoolwide: 44% (state avg 49%)

### By subgroup (grades 6-8 combined)
- White students (n=234): 62%
- Black students (n=98): 31% (-31 pp gap)
- Hispanic/Latino students (n=187): 34% (-28 pp gap)
- Asian students (n=61): 71%
- Students with disabilities (n=89): 18%
- English Learners (n=72): 22%
- Economically disadvantaged (n=341): 35%

### Additional context
- 3-year trend: Black/white math gap has widened from 27 pp (2022-23) to 31 pp (2025-26)
- 8th grade Algebra 1 enrollment: 38% of white students, 12% of Black students, 16% of
  Hispanic students — a significant opportunity-to-learn gap

### Attendance
- Chronic absenteeism (missing 10%+ of school days): 24% (district avg 18%)
- Higher among Black (32%) and economically disadvantaged (29%) subgroups
- Top absence reasons reported: illness, family obligations, transportation

### Teacher data
- 32 FTE teachers; 6 math teachers (4 certified in math content area, 2 teaching
  out-of-subject certification with math endorsements via emergency permit)
- First/second-year teachers: 9 of 32 (28%) — disproportionately assigned to Title I
  classes
- Mean teacher-reported PD hours on culturally responsive instruction (last 2 years): 6
- 3 teachers have 15+ years experience; 11 have 5-10 years

### Curriculum & instruction
- Math curriculum: Big Ideas Math (adopted 2022)
- Fidelity observations show 40-50% implementation of recommended small-group
  differentiation components
- MTSS (Multi-Tiered System of Support): Tier 2 and 3 interventions inconsistent; 18% of
  Tier 2 students receive ≥90 min/week of intervention (target: 100%)
- Universal screener: NWEA MAP, fall/winter/spring

### Family engagement
- Parent-teacher conference attendance: 41% overall
- 23% for economically disadvantaged families
- 19% for families with Spanish as primary home language (72 families)
- PTSA meetings avg 14 attendees

### Resources available
- Title I allocation 2026-27: $418,000
- ESSA school improvement funds (1003): $215,000
- Total available: $633,000
- Staffing flexibility: 1 new FTE can be added; current instructional coach is 0.5 FTE
- District-provided bilingual family liaison: 0.4 FTE

### Historical initiatives that did not move the gap
- After-school tutoring 2023-24: attendance averaged 12/wk of 120 invited; no measurable
  gain on math proficiency or NWEA MAP scores
- Universal screener + benchmarking only, no reteach protocol in place
- "Growth mindset" assembly programming 2024-25 — three schoolwide assemblies
- Extended-learning summer bridge 2024: 34 students served; outcomes not tracked

### Discipline data (equity audit inputs)
- Office referrals, 2025-26: Black students 2.8x the rate of white students
- Suspensions: Black students 3.1x the rate of white students
- These discipline disparities are also a factor in loss of instructional time

## Artifact specification
A formal School Improvement Plan matching ESSA TSI requirements. Structure:

1. **Needs Assessment summary** — student outcome data, disaggregated by subgroup, with a
   narrative that identifies the 2-3 priority problem statements (e.g., "Grade 7 Black
   student math proficiency is 28 pp below white peers, driven by..."). Include a
   root-cause analysis.
2. **Priority areas (exactly 3)** with SMART goals for each. Each goal with: baseline,
   2026-27 target, intermediate milestone at mid-year, measurement instrument.
3. **Evidence-based interventions** (ESSA Tiers 1, 2, or 3 evidence). For each, cite the
   What Works Clearinghouse (WWC) or comparable source, the effect size, the target
   student group, the dosage (minutes/week, weeks/year), the staff assigned, and the
   fidelity measurement approach.
4. **Implementation roadmap by month, Aug 2026 - June 2027** — key actions, responsible
   party, resource, success indicator. Monthly cadence.
5. **Professional learning plan** — PD hours, topics, providers, linked to each
   intervention. Must address the observed gaps in culturally responsive instruction
   (6 mean hours in 2 years is inadequate) and small-group differentiation fidelity.
6. **Family & community engagement plan** — specific to closing the parent-conference gap
   in economically disadvantaged and Black families. Named strategies (e.g., home visits,
   bilingual community liaison scheduling, evening/weekend options, food and childcare at
   events, parent math-night workshops).
7. **Budget** — crosswalk of the $633K total between Title I and 1003 funds, by
   intervention. Every line item tied to a priority area. Must reconcile.
8. **Equity audit addendum** — discipline data by subgroup, course-enrollment data for
   advanced math (Algebra 1 in 8th), teacher assignment patterns (first-year teacher
   concentration in high-needs sections). Required by state equity requirements.
9. **Monitoring & evaluation** — who reviews data when (weekly PLC, monthly leadership
   team, quarterly board report), what triggers mid-year course correction, how NWEA MAP
   data feeds re-teach decisions.
10. **Stakeholder voice** — how student, family, and staff input was/will be incorporated.
11. **Plan risks** — honest list of 4-6 risks (teacher turnover, chronic absenteeism as a
    ceiling on intervention dosage, family engagement capacity, budget continuity into
    2027-28).

Tone: professional, student-centered, evidence-cited. Avoid deficit framing of students or
families; avoid blame framing of teachers. Specific, not aspirational. Uses person-first
language for students with disabilities. Does not treat subgroup data as a proxy for
individual students (i.e., "Black students" as a data category, not as a predictive
characteristic).

## Output format
Markdown document with tables, 10-14 pages. Must conform to state SIP template (the model
should assume a state template similar to what ESSA TSI plans typically require: needs
assessment, goals, strategies, budget, monitoring). Goals table and budget table are the
centerpiece.

## Iteration targets
1. **Replace generic goals with specific, SMART goals** — v1 often writes "improve math
   achievement." Iterate to "Increase Grade 7 Black student math proficiency from 31% to
   42% (+11 pp) on 2027 Smarter Balanced, with mid-year NWEA MAP growth of 1.3x typical
   growth rate, measured at winter benchmark Jan 15, 2027."
2. **Cite actual evidence-based interventions** — v1 often says "small-group instruction."
   Iterate to: "Saga Education-style high-dosage tutoring (WWC Strong evidence, effect
   size +0.37 in middle-school math, Guryan et al. 2023), 3x/week 50 min during school
   day, group ratio 2:1, targeting 85 students (Tier 2/3 based on NWEA MAP), $212K for 4
   tutoring fellows and coordinator."
3. **Make the root-cause analysis actually causal** — v1 lists symptoms (low attendance,
   low PD). Iterate to a multi-layer analysis that distinguishes student-level (lost
   instructional time due to absences and discipline), classroom-level (teacher content
   knowledge gap for 2 out-of-subject teachers, inconsistent curriculum fidelity), and
   school-level (MTSS inconsistency, opportunity-to-learn gap in Algebra 1 enrollment)
   causes, and specifies which causes each intervention addresses.
4. **Confront what didn't work** — v1 ignores the failed 2023-24 tutoring and "growth
   mindset" programming. Iterate to explicitly diagnose why those didn't work (after-school
   timing misaligned with transportation; voluntary participation so students most in need
   didn't come; no curriculum alignment with day-school math; "growth mindset" assemblies
   were not paired with pedagogy shifts) and how the new plan avoids the same failure
   mode.
5. **Tighten the budget-to-intervention tie** — v1 may list a lump budget. Iterate to a
   table where every dollar is tagged to an intervention and, in turn, to a priority
   area and goal. Must sum to $633K (±$5K).
6. **Address the out-of-subject math teacher issue honestly** — v1 may avoid it. A better
   version proposes concrete action (content coaching, co-teaching with a math-certified
   mentor, schedule change for next year, emergency content-area PD) and cites research
   on teacher content knowledge and student math outcomes (Hill, Rowan, Ball 2005).
7. **Address discipline disparity explicitly** — v1 may treat discipline as separate from
   math. Iterate to connect: Black students lose disproportionate instructional time to
   suspensions; Tier 1 PBIS or restorative-practice work is part of the math plan, not
   a separate initiative.

## Success criteria
(For evaluator use only.)
1. Exactly 3 priority areas, each with a SMART goal that includes baseline, target, and
   a mid-year intermediate milestone on a specific measurement instrument.
2. At least 3 evidence-based interventions cite a specific WWC tier or a named research
   source with an effect size.
3. Budget sums to $633K (±$5K) with every line item linked to a priority area.
4. The 2023-24 after-school tutoring failure is explicitly addressed — why it didn't work
   (dosage, voluntary nature, timing) and how the new plan differs.
5. Root-cause analysis distinguishes student-level from system-level causes (not just
   "students need more support").
6. Family engagement plan has at least one strategy targeted specifically at the 23%
   Black/econ-disadvantaged and 19% Spanish-speaking conference attendance — not just
   schoolwide generic engagement.
7. The plan names the out-of-subject math teacher issue and proposes a specific action.
8. Discipline disparity is connected to the math plan (not siloed).
9. 8th grade Algebra 1 opportunity-gap (12% Black, 16% Hispanic vs. 38% white) is
   addressed as an intervention or policy change.

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: needs assessment summary with disaggregated data
table, 3 priority areas with draft SMART goals, 4-5 interventions (at least 2 with cited
evidence), a rough budget breakdown summing to approximately $633K, a professional
learning sketch, and a list of risks. The month-by-month roadmap, detailed PD plan,
equity audit addendum, and stakeholder voice section can be outline-level. By v2/v3, the
intervention list should be comprehensive with WWC citations, budget should reconcile
exactly, and the equity-audit addendum should connect discipline and opportunity-to-learn
gaps to the math plan.
