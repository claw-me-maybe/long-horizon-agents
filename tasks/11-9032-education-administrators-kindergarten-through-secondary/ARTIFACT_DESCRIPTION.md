# 2026-2027 School Improvement Plan — Ridgeway Middle School, Targeting Math Proficiency Gap

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 11-9032
- **Title:** Education Administrators, Kindergarten through Secondary

## Scenario
Ridgeway Middle School (grades 6-8, enrollment 642) in the Millcrest Valley Unified School District has been designated as a "Targeted Support and Improvement (TSI)" school under ESSA after three consecutive years of Black and Hispanic student math proficiency gaps exceeding 25 percentage points vs. white peers. Principal Dr. Kenneth Osei must submit a 2026-2027 School Improvement Plan (SIP) to the Superintendent, Dr. Lydia Brockman, and the School Board by July 15, 2026, to be implemented starting with the August 19, 2026 teacher prep week. The state DOE (Department of Education) will review the plan against the evidence-based intervention requirements of ESSA Section 1111(d).

## Inputs
The model starts with:

- **2025-2026 Smarter Balanced math proficiency rates (% Meets/Exceeds):**
  - Grade 6 schoolwide: 51% (state avg 54%)
  - Grade 7 schoolwide: 47% (state avg 51%)
  - Grade 8 schoolwide: 44% (state avg 49%)
  - By subgroup (grades 6-8 combined):
    - White students (n=234): 62%
    - Black students (n=98): 31% (-31 pp gap)
    - Hispanic/Latino students (n=187): 34% (-28 pp gap)
    - Asian students (n=61): 71%
    - Students with disabilities (n=89): 18%
    - English Learners (n=72): 22%
    - Economically disadvantaged (n=341): 35%
- **Attendance:** Chronic absenteeism 24% (district avg 18%). Higher among Black (32%) and economically disadvantaged (29%) subgroups.
- **Teacher data:**
  - 32 FTE teachers; 6 math teachers (4 certified in math, 2 teaching out-of-subject).
  - First/second-year teachers: 9 of 32 (28%).
  - Mean teacher-reported PD hours on culturally responsive instruction (last 2 years): 6.
- **Curriculum & instruction:**
  - Math curriculum: Big Ideas Math (adopted 2022). Fidelity observations show 40-50% implementation of recommended small-group differentiation.
  - MTSS (Multi-Tiered System of Support): Tier 2 and 3 interventions inconsistent; 18% of Tier 2 students receive ≥90 min/week of intervention, target is 100%.
- **Family engagement:** Parent-teacher conference attendance 41% overall; 23% for economically disadvantaged families.
- **Resources available:**
  - Title I allocation 2026-27: $418,000
  - ESSA school improvement funds (1003): $215,000
  - Staffing flexibility: 1 new FTE can be added; current instructional coach is 0.5 FTE
- **Historical initiatives that did not move the gap:**
  - After-school tutoring 2023-24 (attendance avg 12/wk of 120 invited; no measurable gain).
  - Universal screener + benchmarking only, no reteach protocol.
  - "Growth mindset" assembly programming 2024-25.

## Artifact specification
A formal School Improvement Plan matching ESSA TSI requirements. Structure:
1. **Needs Assessment summary** — student outcome data, disaggregated by subgroup, with a narrative that identifies the 2-3 priority problem statements (e.g., "Grade 7 Black student math proficiency is 28 pp below white peers, driven by..."). Include a root-cause analysis.
2. **Priority areas (exactly 3)** with SMART goals for each. Each goal with: baseline, 2026-27 target, intermediate milestone at mid-year, measurement instrument.
3. **Evidence-based interventions** (ESSA Tiers 1, 2, or 3 evidence). For each, cite the What Works Clearinghouse or comparable source, the effect size, the target student group, the dosage (minutes/week, weeks/year), the staff assigned, and the fidelity measurement.
4. **Implementation roadmap by month, Aug 2026 - June 2027** — key actions, responsible party, resource, success indicator.
5. **Professional learning plan** — PD hours, topics, providers, linked to each intervention. Must address the observed gaps in culturally responsive instruction and small-group differentiation.
6. **Family & community engagement plan** — specific to closing the parent-conference gap in economically disadvantaged and Black families. Named strategies (e.g., home visits, bilingual community liaison).
7. **Budget** — crosswalk of the $633K total between Title I and 1003 funds, by intervention. Every line item tied to a priority area.
8. **Equity audit addendum** — discipline data by subgroup, course-enrollment data for advanced math (Algebra 1 in 8th), teacher assignment patterns. Required by state equity requirements.
9. **Monitoring & evaluation** — who reviews data when, what triggers mid-year course correction.
10. **Plan risks** — honest list of 4-6 risks (teacher turnover, chronic absenteeism as a limiter, family engagement capacity).

Tone: professional, student-centered, evidence-cited. Avoid deficit framing of students or families; avoid blame framing of teachers. Specific, not aspirational.

## Output format
Markdown document with tables, 10-14 pages. Must conform to state SIP template (the model should assume a state template similar to what ESSA TSI plans typically require: needs assessment, goals, strategies, budget, monitoring). Goals and budget tables are the centerpiece.

## Iteration targets
1. **Replace generic goals with specific, SMART goals** — v1 often writes "improve math achievement." Iterate to "Increase Grade 7 Black student math proficiency from 31% to 42% (+11 pp) on 2027 Smarter Balanced, with mid-year NWEA MAP growth of 1.3x typical."
2. **Cite actual evidence-based interventions** — v1 often says "small-group instruction." Iterate to: "Saga Education-style high-dosage tutoring (WWC Strong evidence, effect size +0.37 in middle-school math), 3x/week 50 min, group ratio 2:1, targeting 85 students (Tier 2/3), $212K."
3. **Make the root-cause analysis actually causal** — v1 lists symptoms (low attendance, low PD). Iterate to a multi-layer analysis that distinguishes student-level, classroom-level, and school-level causes, and specifies which causes each intervention addresses.
4. **Confront what didn't work** — v1 ignores the failed 2023-24 tutoring and "growth mindset" programming. Iterate to explicitly diagnose why those didn't work (dosage, targeting, fidelity) and how the new plan avoids the same failure mode.
5. **Tighten the budget-to-intervention tie** — v1 may list a lump budget. Iterate to a table where every dollar is tagged to an intervention and, in turn, to a priority area and goal.
6. **Address the out-of-subject math teacher issue honestly** — v1 may avoid it. A better version proposes concrete action (coaching, partnering, schedule change for next year) and cites research on teacher content knowledge.

## Success criteria
(For evaluator use only.)
1. Exactly 3 priority areas, each with a SMART goal that includes baseline, target, and a mid-year intermediate milestone.
2. At least 3 evidence-based interventions cite a specific WWC tier or a named research source with an effect size.
3. Budget sums to $633K (±$5K) with every line item linked to a priority area.
4. The 2023-24 after-school tutoring failure is explicitly addressed — why it didn't work and how the new plan differs.
5. Root-cause analysis distinguishes student-level from system-level causes (not just "students need more support").
6. Family engagement plan has at least one strategy targeted specifically at the 23% Black/econ-disadvantaged conference attendance — not just schoolwide generic engagement.
7. The plan names the out-of-subject math teacher issue and proposes a specific action.

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: needs assessment summary with disaggregated data table, 3 priority areas with draft SMART goals, 4-5 interventions (at least 2 with cited evidence), a rough budget breakdown summing to approximately $633K, and a list of risks. The month-by-month roadmap, detailed PD plan, and equity audit can be outline-level.
