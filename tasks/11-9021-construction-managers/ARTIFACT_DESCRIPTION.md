# Change Order Package #CO-017 — Elevator Shaft Rework, Cedarhurst Medical Office Building

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 11-9021
- **Title:** Construction Managers

## Scenario
Merrick Construction is the CM-at-Risk on the Cedarhurst Medical Office Building, a $48M
four-story, 94,000 sq-ft MOB outside Charlotte, NC, scheduled for substantial completion
on May 28, 2027. During elevator shaft inspection on September 11, 2026, a 3-inch
out-of-plumb deviation was identified in the north elevator shaft (Shaft N-1) from the 2nd
floor slab through the roof, which prevents KONE (elevator vendor) from installing the
already-fabricated jack and car rails per shop drawings. Project Manager Luis Caballero
must prepare Change Order Package #CO-017 for submission to the Owner (Piedmont Health
Properties, LLC, represented by Director of Development Olivia Reyes) and the Architect
(Kessler+Nomura Architects, Principal-in-Charge Rhonda Tan) by September 25, 2026. The
package drives a request for $187,400 and 18 calendar days of schedule extension. The
project is mid-construction (63% complete); the Owner has been cost-sensitive throughout
and has escalated prior smaller change orders.

## Inputs
The model starts with:

### Contract basis
- CM-at-Risk with GMP of $47,950,000 (executed February 4, 2025)
- AIA A133-2019 with A201-2017 General Conditions
- Current Contract Sum: $48,423,910 (GMP + 4 prior approved change orders, #CO-013,
  CO-014, CO-015, CO-016)
- Current Substantial Completion date: May 28, 2027
- Liquidated damages: $3,500/day after SC
- Elevator procurement agreement: KONE, subcontract value $1,840,000

### Non-conformance facts
- Root cause under investigation: survey control used by concrete subcontractor (Piedmont
  Structural Inc., "PSI") on the 2nd floor slab pour (March 11, 2026) referenced a
  benchmark that was 0.5 inch off plumb relative to the original control established by
  the site surveyor. This propagated through successive pours at Levels 3, 4, and Roof.
- ATC Surveyors (engaged as 3rd-party independent surveyor for quality assurance) confirmed
  the deviation: 3.0 inches total out-of-plumb at the roof, with a gradient of approximately
  0.5 inch per floor starting from Level 2.
- Discovery: Shop-drawing clash identified during KONE pre-installation inspection on
  September 11, 2026 (5 months after the Level 2 pour).
- Effect: Elevator rails cannot be installed without remediation; KONE requires rework of
  shaft walls (CMU) and slab edges between Level 2 and Level 4. Below Level 2 is
  compliant; verified by ATC resurvey on September 13.

### Proposed remediation (scope)
1. Demolition / saw-cutting of existing CMU shaft walls between Levels 2 and 4 on two
   affected faces (east and west faces of Shaft N-1), approximately 380 SF per floor per
   face (1,520 SF total)
2. Reset plumb line using ATC-verified new control points
3. New CMU blockwork to rebuild shaft walls
4. Grind and repour slab edges at shaft penetrations (6 locations: 3 floors × 2 faces)
5. Re-survey post-rework and produce as-built shaft geometry acceptance document
6. Re-inspect by KONE field engineer before re-mobilizing installation
7. Remobilize CMU subcontractor (Skylark Masonry), concrete subcontractor (Piedmont
   Structural), and KONE

### Cost build-up (draft)
- **Demo / rework CMU (Skylark Masonry):** $52,300
  - Direct labor: 320 hrs @ $74 loaded = $23,680
  - Materials (CMU, mortar, reinforcing): $18,900
  - Equipment (saw-cutting, hoisting): $6,200
  - Skylark overhead/profit at 12%: $3,520
- **Concrete grind/repour (Piedmont Structural):** $41,200
  - Labor, materials, pump, finishing
  - Includes curing time waste
- **KONE remobilization + engineering:** $38,500
  - Shop drawing update: $8,500
  - Remobilization (crew travel, equipment): $22,000
  - Pre-install re-inspection: $8,000
- **General conditions extension:** $51,300
  - 18 calendar days × $2,850/day (field office, supervision, insurance, rental)
- **CM fee (stated in contract at 3.75% of direct cost):** $6,911
  - 3.75% × ($52,300 + $41,200 + $38,500 + $51,300) = 3.75% × $183,300 = $6,873
  - Rounded for negotiation: $6,911
- **Subtotal:** $190,211
- **Proposed CO amount (rounded after Skylark negotiation reduced labor hrs):** $187,400

### Schedule impact analysis
- Shaft rework critical path: 14 working days
  - Demo and saw-cutting: 4 days
  - CMU reset and new blockwork: 6 days
  - Concrete grind/repour: 3 days
  - Cure + survey + KONE inspection: 1 day
- KONE installation delay: original mobilization was October 1, 2026; revised
  October 23, 2026 (3 weeks later due to KONE crew scheduling)
- Net critical-path impact: 18 calendar days
- Current schedule float on critical path: 6 days (reduced from 20 days due to Q2 2026
  weather delays already absorbed)

### Cost responsibility (open question)
- Piedmont Structural's benchmark error is plausibly a subcontractor defect, but the
  original surveyor's QC sign-off is in dispute (Piedmont Structural claims they used the
  surveyor's provided benchmark; the surveyor claims their benchmark was correct at the
  time and PSI transferred it incorrectly).
- Owner may assert CM is responsible under the contract's quality control provisions
  (Article 3.3.6).
- The change order will be filed as a Change Directive (under A201 Section 7.3) with cost
  responsibility to be resolved in subsequent negotiation/arbitration.

### Current project status
- 63% complete by schedule, 65% by cost
- Weather-related float burned in Q2 2026 due to heavy rain
- Current remaining critical-path float: 6 days
- 5 prior change orders totaling $473,910 (net), all approved

## Artifact specification
A Change Order Package following AIA-style conventions. Sections required:

1. **Change Order Summary (Cover Sheet)** — CO #017 identification, brief description
   (one sentence), cost delta ($187,400), time delta (18 calendar days), new Contract Sum
   ($48,611,310), new Substantial Completion date (June 15, 2027), signature block layout
   (Owner / Architect / CM).
2. **Narrative description** — 1-2 page narrative covering:
   (a) the non-conformance (what, where, when discovered);
   (b) discovery (ATC inspection triggered by KONE pre-install);
   (c) required remediation (scope summary);
   (d) reason for the change (why rework is necessary);
   (e) position on cost responsibility (careful language preserving CM's rights without
   prejudicing Owner's ability to backcharge Piedmont Structural or surveyor).
3. **Cost breakdown** — detailed schedule of values by subcontractor + CM fee + GCs.
   Conforms to AIA G701/G702-style detail with line items reconciling to $187,400.
4. **Backup documentation list** — itemized list of attached backup (subcontractor
   proposals with numbers/dates, labor certified payroll, material invoices, equipment
   rate sheets, ATC survey report, KONE field deficiency report). Even if not physically
   attached, list what's referenced.
5. **Time Impact Analysis (TIA)** — baseline schedule fragnet excerpt showing critical
   path before and after. 18-day extension justified with activity-level logic, not just
   a claim.
6. **Schedule fragnet narrative** — activity-by-activity sequence for the 14 working days
   of rework, with predecessor/successor logic, resource constraints (KONE availability
   is a 21-day re-mobilization wait), and critical-path callout.
7. **Quality & re-acceptance plan** — re-survey criteria (plumb tolerance, survey
   methodology), re-inspection by KONE before reinstatement (named hold point), hold
   points, warranty on the rework scope.
8. **Reservation of rights language** — standard AIA reservation to preserve Owner's and
   CM's positions on root cause, indemnification, subcontractor backcharge, and warranty.
9. **Risk notes** — latent condition discovery risk (what if 3-inch deviation isn't the
   full extent? what if Level 1 also has issues not yet surveyed?), weather risk during
   the rework window (exterior wall exposure through October), trade stacking risk for
   other MEP work adjacent to the shaft.

Tone: formal, contract-aware, precise. Written to be defensible in potential dispute
while preserving working relationship with Owner and Architect. Active voice in narrative;
contract voice in reservation language.

## Output format
Markdown document formatted as a Change Order Package, 6-9 pages rendered. Must include:
Cover sheet, cost breakdown table, schedule fragnet table (or structured text
representation), backup list, and contract-language snippets for reservation of rights.
Structured so that it could be transposed directly into AIA G701 + G702 official forms.

## Iteration targets
1. **Fix the narrative cost-responsibility position** — v1 often either admits CM fault
   or blames the subcontractor outright. Iterate to the careful CM-at-Risk position:
   describe the facts without attribution, note the open root-cause investigation,
   proceed under Change Directive with cost to be resolved through backcharge negotiation
   with PSI and/or the surveyor, and explicitly reserve all rights of the CM under the
   contract.
2. **Make the TIA defensible** — v1 often claims 18 days with a sentence. Iterate to a
   fragnet with named activities, durations, predecessors, and a critical-path
   demonstration. Must explain why the 3-week KONE remobilization doesn't just add as
   pure float but affects the CP (because the rework plus remobilization sequence exceeds
   the remaining 6 days of CP float).
3. **Tighten the cost buildup** — v1 may round or bundle. Iterate to line-item granularity
   with labor hours × rate, material quantities × unit cost, equipment rates, and CM fee
   correctly calculated at 3.75% of direct cost (not total). Reconcile any rounding.
4. **Strengthen the backup documentation list** — v1 may list "subcontractor quotes."
   Iterate to: "Skylark Masonry Proposal #SM-6621 dated 9/18/26; Piedmont Structural
   Change Proposal CP-14 dated 9/19/26; ATC Surveyors Report ATC-2026-0911 dated
   9/14/26; KONE Field Deficiency Report KDR-N1-0911 dated 9/11/26; OSHA 300 log review
   9/15/26; weather data from Charlotte-Douglas airport Sep 11-20, 2026."
5. **Add risk language about latent extent** — v1 may claim 3-inch is fully bounded. A
   better version notes the possibility that survey of Level 1 (below the assumed point
   of origin) could reveal additional out-of-plumb, and includes a contingency/hold with
   proposed process if additional scope is discovered during demo (e.g., "Any additional
   out-of-plumb condition discovered during demolition will be addressed through a
   supplemental Change Order, with work continuing pending direction").
6. **Clarify new Substantial Completion date math** — v1 may say "+18 days." Iterate to
   name the new SC date (May 28, 2027 + 18 calendar days = June 15, 2027), note LD
   implication ($3,500/day × 18 days = $63,000 exposure if disputed as CM-caused, which
   is why reservation of rights matters), and reference what this does to the remaining
   6 days of CP float (reduces from 6 to effectively 0, meaning any future delay is
   direct SC impact).

## Success criteria
(For evaluator use only.)
1. Cost breakdown lines sum to the CO total ($187,400 ± $2,000 with rounding note) with
   correct CM fee calculation at 3.75% of direct cost.
2. Schedule extension is supported by an activity-level fragnet with named durations and
   predecessors totaling the claimed critical-path impact of 18 calendar days.
3. Cost-responsibility language explicitly reserves rights and avoids pre-admitting fault;
   proceeds as a Change Directive pending root-cause resolution between PSI and surveyor.
4. New Substantial Completion date is stated as a specific calendar date (June 15, 2027),
   and the LD exposure ($3,500/day) is acknowledged in the time-extension justification.
5. Backup documentation list names at least 5 specific documents (with numbers/dates) not
   generic "subcontractor quotes."
6. Latent-extent risk (further investigation of Level 1 or below, or additional
   out-of-plumb during demo) is addressed explicitly with a contingency or supplemental-CO
   process.
7. At least one AIA article (e.g., A201-2017 Section 7.3 for Change Directive) is
   specifically cited.

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: cover summary with cost and time delta; cost breakdown
table with the 5 major cost categories and CM fee; a narrative paragraph describing the
non-conformance and remediation; a time-impact statement with the 18-day claim (even if
supported by only a paragraph, not a full fragnet); a backup document list (even if
generic); and reservation-of-rights boilerplate. Full fragnet detail, specific
subcontractor proposal references with numbers/dates, and latent-risk language polish
are iteration targets for v2/v3. By v3, the cost should reconcile exactly with the CM
fee math shown, the TIA should have activity-level logic, and the risk language should
distinguish CM-liable scenarios from Owner-responsible ones.
