# FY2027 Territory & Compensation Plan — Northbrook Industrial Supply Mid-Market Segment

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 11-2022
- **Title:** Sales Managers

## Scenario
Northbrook Industrial Supply, a $180M B2B distributor of MRO and safety supplies, is redesigning its 18-rep Mid-Market sales team for FY2027 (Feb 2027-Jan 2028). Current ARR coverage is uneven: three reps carry $12M+ books while six carry under $4M, and the Top 20 accounts are concentrated with two reps. Sales Director Marcus Tremaine must deliver a territory + compensation plan to the VP of Sales, Janet Coluccio, by November 14, 2026, in time for rep communication December 1 and year-start February 1. The plan must hit a segment quota of $42M new + retention with OTE budget capped at $3.1M.

- **FY27 segment targets:** $42.0M total = $27.5M retention + $14.5M new/expansion. Expected churn 7.5%. OTE budget $3.1M (avg $172K per rep; current avg $165K).
- **Geographic footprint:** 14 states across US Midwest + Great Lakes. Current territories are a patchwork of legacy state assignments.
- **Customer concentration:** Top 20 accounts = 31% of segment ARR; currently managed by Alvaro (11 accounts) and Petersen (6 accounts). Churn in Top 20 would be catastrophic.
- **Product mix trend:** Safety & PPE category growing 14% YoY; legacy Bearings & Power Transmission declining 3% YoY. Only 4 of 18 reps have Safety product certification.
- **Known rep changes:** Huang being promoted to Enterprise Feb 1 (leaving book of $3.8M / 29 accounts). Hiring 2 new reps starting Jan 15, 2027 (ramp: 25% productive Q1, 75% Q2, 100% Q3).
- **Comp plan constraints from Finance:** Base:Variable ratio 60:40 for reps, 70:30 for strategic-account roles. Max accelerator 2.0x above 120% attainment. Must include SPIF budget line of $120K total.

## Artifact specification
A multi-part sales plan deliverable with:
1. **Plan summary** — one page explaining structural choices: how territories are organized (geo? industry? book-size?), how the Top 20 are handled, the comp plan philosophy, and key trade-offs.
2. **Role taxonomy** — proposed roles (e.g., Strategic Account Executive, Mid-Market AE, New Business Hunter, Named-Account Specialist). Count in each, OTE per role, quota per role.
3. **Territory / book assignment table** — every named rep (or role slot) assigned a territory/book with: projected book ARR, quota, account count, geography/segment rationale. Must reconcile to $42M total.
4. **Top 20 coverage strategy** — explicit plan: who owns what, overlay support, executive sponsorship, QBR cadence.
5. **Compensation plan specification** — base/variable split, quota credit rules, accelerator/decelerator schedule, SPIF design, clawback rules. Include a pay-curve table for at least one role (e.g., 50%, 75%, 100%, 120%, 150% attainment).
6. **Safety-category growth plan** — how the plan drives the 14% growth product category: certification, named accounts, SPIF design.
7. **Transition & risk** — Huang's book redistribution, new-hire ramp assumptions, rep morale risks, and rep-retention actions.
8. **Quota-setting methodology** — how quotas were built up (bottom-up from accounts? top-down from segment target?), and the at-plan attainment expectation (what % of reps hit 100%+).

Tone: operational and rep-facing. Assume this will be socialized with reps, so avoid jargon that implies reps are interchangeable.

## Output format
Markdown document with embedded tables, 5-8 pages. The territory-assignment table and comp-curve table are the centerpiece. Should be exportable to a 12-slide internal deck and a one-page rep summary.

## Iteration targets
1. **Reconcile the math to $42M and $3.1M** — v1 often has rep quotas summing to ~$40M or OTE summing to ~$3.3M. Iterate to exact reconciliation with explicit roll-up table.
2. **Make Top 20 coverage more defensible** — v1 tends to leave the Top 20 with Alvaro and Petersen unchanged (lazy). A stronger version considers a Named-Accounts overlay or Strategic-AE role with explicit rationale and a transition plan that protects renewals.
3. **Quantify the Huang-book redistribution** — v1 usually says "redistribute across adjacent reps." Iterate to a specific $-per-receiving-rep breakdown with implications for their quota and OTE.
4. **Design an at-plan attainment realistic target** — v1 may implicitly assume 90-100% of reps will hit quota. A better version states an attainment distribution (e.g., "50-60% of reps at 100%+ plan") and ties comp budget to that expectation.
5. **Make the Safety-category play specific** — v1 often says "focus on safety." Iterate to: which 4 reps are certified, which 4 will be certified by Q2, named target accounts, SPIF structure ($ per new Safety dollar).
6. **Address quota fairness perceptions** — v1 may ignore that moving an account off Alvaro feels like a pay cut. Iterate to a named retention mechanic (quota relief, guarantee period, or transition bonus).

## Success criteria
(For evaluator use only.)
1. The sum of rep quotas = $42M (±$0.5M explicitly reconciled) and sum of OTE = $3.1M (±$50K).
2. Top 20 account coverage is explicitly addressed with named owners or an overlay model — not just "Alvaro keeps them."
3. Pay curve table shows correct accelerator math at 120% (first dollar at 2x OR up-to-cap defined) and has at least 5 attainment tiers.
4. Huang's $3.8M book is redistributed with a stated $-destination and the resulting quota changes for receiving reps.
5. New-hire quotas reflect the stated ramp (e.g., Q1 new-hire quota = 25% of full quota, not 100%).
6. At least one trade-off is honestly acknowledged (e.g., "This plan reduces Alvaro's book by $3.2M, which risks his departure; mitigation = 6-month earnings guarantee").

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: plan summary with stated territory philosophy, a rep-assignment table covering at least 12 of the 18 reps, a role taxonomy, at least one pay-curve table, and a Top 20 strategy stub. Quota roll-up should be approximately to $42M. Safety-category play and transition risk sections can be outlined but not fleshed out.
