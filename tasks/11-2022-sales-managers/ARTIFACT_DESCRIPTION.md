# FY2027 Territory & Compensation Plan — Northbrook Industrial Supply Mid-Market Segment

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 11-2022
- **Title:** Sales Managers

## Scenario
Northbrook Industrial Supply, a $180M B2B distributor of MRO and safety supplies serving
14 states across the US Midwest and Great Lakes, is redesigning its 18-rep Mid-Market sales
team for FY2027 (Feb 2027-Jan 2028). Current ARR coverage is uneven: three reps carry $12M+
books while six carry under $4M, and the Top 20 accounts are concentrated with two reps.
Sales Director Marcus Tremaine must deliver a territory + compensation plan to the VP of
Sales, Janet Coluccio, by November 14, 2026, in time for rep communication December 1 and
year-start February 1. The plan must hit a segment quota of $42M new + retention with OTE
budget capped at $3.1M. Failure modes include: triggering voluntary attrition of top
performers, creating Top 20 renewal risk, or under-resourcing the Safety & PPE category
where 14% growth is available.

## Artifact specification
A multi-part sales plan deliverable with the following structure:

1. **Plan summary** — one page explaining structural choices: how territories are organized
   (geo? industry? book-size?), how the Top 20 are handled, the comp plan philosophy, and
   the top three trade-offs this plan makes.
2. **Role taxonomy** — proposed roles (e.g., Strategic Account Executive, Mid-Market AE,
   New Business Hunter, Named-Account Specialist). Count in each, OTE per role, quota
   per role. Must sum to 18 current + 2 new = 20 FTE minus Huang promotion = 19 FTE.
3. **Territory / book assignment table** — every named rep (or role slot) assigned a
   territory/book with: projected book ARR, quota, account count, geography/segment
   rationale. Must reconcile to $42M segment quota.
4. **Top 20 coverage strategy** — explicit plan: who owns what, overlay support, executive
   sponsorship, QBR cadence. The Evercore RFP situation specifically addressed.
5. **Compensation plan specification** — base/variable split, quota credit rules,
   accelerator/decelerator schedule, SPIF design, clawback rules. Include a pay-curve table
   for at least one role (e.g., 50%, 75%, 100%, 120%, 150% attainment) showing total W-2.
6. **Safety-category growth plan** — how the plan drives the 14% growth product category:
   certification roadmap for 4 additional reps, named target accounts for Safety cross-sell,
   SPIF design for first-order Safety revenue.
7. **Transition & risk** — Huang's book redistribution with $-per-receiving-rep breakdown,
   new-hire ramp assumptions, rep morale risks, and rep-retention actions (especially for
   Wozniak and Brinkley).
8. **Quota-setting methodology** — how quotas were built up (bottom-up from accounts?
   top-down from segment target?), and the at-plan attainment expectation (what % of reps
   will hit 100%+).
9. **Communication plan** — Dec 1 rep announcement sequence, 1:1 meeting schedule, FAQ
   document outline, escalation path for rep objections.

Tone: operational and rep-facing. Assume this will be socialized with reps, so avoid jargon
that implies reps are interchangeable. Treat reps as adults; don't soften hard changes.

## Output format
Markdown document with embedded tables, 5-8 pages. The territory-assignment table and
comp-curve table are the centerpiece. Should be exportable to a 12-slide internal deck and
a one-page rep summary handout.

## Iteration targets
1. **Reconcile the math to $42M and $3.1M** — v1 often has rep quotas summing to ~$40M or
   OTE summing to ~$3.3M. Iterate to exact reconciliation (±$0.5M quota, ±$50K OTE) with
   explicit roll-up table. This is a forcing function for discipline.
2. **Make Top 20 coverage more defensible** — v1 tends to leave the Top 20 with Alvaro and
   Petersen unchanged (lazy). A stronger version considers a Named-Accounts overlay or
   Strategic-AE role with explicit rationale and a transition plan that protects renewals.
   The Evercore RFP requires an explicit response — pull Alvaro off 3 smaller accounts to
   focus? Add a specialist overlay?
3. **Quantify the Huang-book redistribution** — v1 usually says "redistribute across
   adjacent reps." Iterate to a specific $-per-receiving-rep breakdown with implications
   for their quota and OTE. 29 accounts totaling $3.8M need to go somewhere.
4. **Design a realistic at-plan attainment target** — v1 may implicitly assume 90-100% of
   reps will hit quota. A better version states an attainment distribution (e.g., "50-60%
   of reps at 100%+ plan; 20% at 120%+; 20% below 90%") and ties the comp budget to that
   expectation. Finance's $3.1M assumption is tied to attainment.
5. **Make the Safety-category play specific** — v1 often says "focus on safety." Iterate
   to: which 4 reps are certified today, which 4 will be certified by end of Q2 (named
   training program, cost, time-off), named target accounts, SPIF structure ($200 per new
   Safety-line dollar net-new, capped at $2,500/rep/quarter, etc.).
6. **Address quota fairness perceptions** — v1 may ignore that moving an account off
   Alvaro feels like a pay cut. Iterate to a named retention mechanic (quota relief for
   transferred accounts, earnings guarantee period, or transition bonus of X% for Y months).

## Success criteria
(For evaluator use only.)
1. The sum of rep quotas = $42M (±$0.5M explicitly reconciled) and sum of OTE = $3.1M
   (±$50K). The roll-up is shown.
2. Top 20 account coverage is explicitly addressed with named owners or an overlay model
   — not just "Alvaro keeps them." The Evercore RFP is named and addressed.
3. Pay curve table shows correct accelerator math at 120% (first dollar at 2x OR up-to-cap
   defined) and has at least 5 attainment tiers.
4. Huang's $3.8M book is redistributed with a stated $-destination and the resulting quota
   changes for receiving reps.
5. New-hire quotas reflect the stated ramp (e.g., Q1 new-hire quota = 25% of full annual
   quota, not full quota).
6. At least one trade-off is honestly acknowledged (e.g., "This plan reduces Alvaro's book
   by $3.2M, which risks his departure; mitigation = 6-month earnings guarantee").
7. Safety-category plan identifies at least 4 reps by name for certification by Q2.

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: plan summary with stated territory philosophy; a
rep-assignment table covering at least 12 of the 18 reps with quota and book; a role
taxonomy (even if broadly defined); at least one pay-curve table; a Top 20 strategy stub;
and a one-paragraph Huang-book redistribution approach. Quota roll-up should be
approximately $42M even if not exact. Safety-category detail, transition-risk actions, and
communication plan can be outlined but not fleshed out. By v2/v3, the math should
reconcile exactly, the Top 20 strategy should name specific overlay or specialist roles,
and the retention mechanics for Alvaro, Wozniak, and Brinkley should be concrete.
