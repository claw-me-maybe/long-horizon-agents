# K-5 ELA Curriculum Adoption Framework: Structured Literacy Transition Plan (Year 1)

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 25-9031
- **Title:** Instructional Coordinators

## Scenario
Dr. Ndiaye is the K-5 ELA Instructional Coordinator for Riverside Unified School District, an 11-school district of 6,400 students serving a mixed suburban/rural community. The state passed legislation requiring evidence-based structured-literacy instruction aligned to the science of reading (effective fall 2025), and the board approved a new core program (CKLA Amplify for K-2; HMH Into Reading with structured-literacy supplements for 3-5) in March 2025. She must produce a Year-1 adoption framework that covers rollout sequencing, teacher PD, coaching, fidelity monitoring, and assessment transitions across 11 schools. The framework will be presented to the board at the June 2025 study session and formally adopted in July. This is NOT a lesson plan — it is a strategic district-level curriculum and implementation document. Dr. Ndiaye reports to the Assistant Superintendent of Teaching & Learning and collaborates with the Special Education Director (for dyslexia/MTSS alignment) and the HR Director (for PD scheduling within collective-bargaining constraints).

## Inputs
- **Standards and frameworks:**
  - State ELA standards (aligned to CCSS-ELA-Literacy K-5: Reading Foundational Skills RF.K.1-4 through RF.5.4)
  - National Reading Panel (2000) five pillars
  - Simple View of Reading (Gough & Tunmer); Scarborough's Reading Rope
  - State dyslexia screening mandate (universal K-2 screening, 3x/year)
- **Current state (baseline):**
  - 2023-24 MAP Reading data: 41% K-2 and 53% 3-5 at/above benchmark; Tier 3 MTSS caseload of 287 students
  - Teacher survey (n=214): 22% report "highly prepared" to teach phonics
  - Prior curriculum: balanced-literacy program (Fountas & Pinnell); Reading Recovery in 4 schools
  - Current screener: DIBELS 8th Edition (being retained); new: Acadience Reading
- **Resources:**
  - $1.2M in state structured-literacy grant funds (2-year award)
  - 2 reading coaches currently on staff; 3 new coaching positions approved
  - PD calendar: 4 full days (pre-service August, November, February, June); 2 early-release Wednesdays/month
- **Constraints:** Collective bargaining limits PD to 18 hours outside contract; Title I compliance; board reports quarterly.

## Artifact specification
Year-1 adoption framework document. This is a **strategic curriculum / PD planning artifact**, not classroom lesson plans.

Required sections:
1. **Executive summary** (1 page): purpose, rationale (science of reading), key metrics, board-reportable outcomes.
2. **Theory of action / logic model**: if-then chain from PD inputs -> classroom practice change -> student outcomes. Name assumptions.
3. **Scope & sequence of rollout** (by quarter, Aug 2025 - Jun 2026): which schools, grade bands, and components launch when. Include Year-0 pilot schools if any.
4. **Professional learning plan**:
   - PD series outline (topic, hours, facilitator, audience, success measures)
   - Coaching model (cycle length, FTE allocation, observation tool alignment)
   - Differentiated tracks for veteran vs. novice teachers
   - Administrator learning (principals need to recognize structured literacy in walkthroughs)
5. **Implementation fidelity plan**:
   - Walkthrough tool (look-fors tied to Tier 1 structured-literacy components: phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocab, comprehension)
   - Observation cadence and data flow
   - Fidelity-to-program metrics (e.g., minutes/day of phonics in K-2, scope-and-sequence pacing)
6. **Assessment transition plan**: screener, diagnostic, progress-monitoring, summative — named tools, windows, and data review cycles.
7. **Equity and MTSS alignment**: how Tier 1 shift reduces Tier 3 overreferral; ELL and dyslexia specifics.
8. **Budget narrative** (high level): allocation of $1.2M across materials, PD, coaches, assessments.
9. **Risks and mitigations** (table): at least 5 named risks (e.g., teacher resistance, material shortage, early data dip).
10. **Board-reportable KPIs and reporting cadence**.

Pedagogical conventions: backwards design from student outcomes; logic-model framing; MTSS tiers (1-2-3); science of reading alignment; adult learning theory (Knowles) for PD design.

## Output format
Single Markdown file with tables and a theory-of-action diagram rendered in ASCII or described. ~2,500-3,500 words.

## Iteration targets
1. **Sharpen the theory of action** — v1 often lists activities without causal chains; force explicit links from PD hours -> observable teacher moves -> student metrics.
2. **Calibrate rollout pacing** — 11 schools in Year 1 is aggressive; decide and defend whether to phase (e.g., 4 pilot -> 7 in spring) or go all-in.
3. **Make fidelity look-fors observable** — move from "teachers use evidence-based practices" to countable actions on the walkthrough tool.
4. **Address the F&P legacy** — explicit plan for what happens to leveled-reader libraries, running records, and Reading Recovery staff; this is politically hot.
5. **Budget realism** — check that PD hours, coach FTE, and materials can actually be purchased within $1.2M.
6. **Tighten KPIs** — each KPI must be measurable with a named data source and cadence.

## Success criteria
- Theory of action / logic model is present and traces a complete inputs -> activities -> outputs -> outcomes chain.
- Rollout sequence is quarter-by-quarter with named schools or cohorts.
- PD plan specifies facilitator, audience, duration, and success measure for each session.
- Fidelity walkthrough tool lists observable look-fors tied to the five pillars.
- Budget lines sum to ~$1.2M with stated tradeoffs.
- At least 5 risks are named with mitigations and owners.
- Document avoids prescribing lesson-level content — it frames teacher enactment, not scripts.

## Scope target
At 15 minutes, v1 should include: executive summary, draft theory of action, a quarterly rollout table (even if rough), a PD series outline with topics and hours, a preliminary walkthrough look-fors list, and a skeletal risk register. Budget narrative, equity/MTSS section, and board KPIs are expected to be placeholder-level, to be iterated.
