# K-5 ELA Curriculum Adoption Framework: Structured Literacy Transition Plan (Year 1)

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 25-9031
- **Title:** Instructional Coordinators

## Scenario
Dr. Ndiaye is the K-5 ELA Instructional Coordinator for Riverside Unified School District, an 11-school district of 6,400 students serving a mixed suburban/rural community. The state passed legislation requiring evidence-based structured-literacy instruction aligned to the science of reading (effective fall 2025), and the board approved a new core program (CKLA Amplify for K-2; HMH Into Reading with structured-literacy supplements for 3-5) in March 2025. She must produce a Year-1 adoption framework that covers rollout sequencing, teacher PD, coaching, fidelity monitoring, and assessment transitions across 11 schools, but for v1 the artifact should read like a coordinator-level recommendation packet rather than a full district framework. The framework will be presented to the board at the June 2025 study session and formally adopted in July. Dr. Ndiaye reports to the Assistant Superintendent of Teaching & Learning and collaborates with the Special Education Director (for dyslexia/MTSS alignment), the Assessment Director (for DIBELS/Acadience transition), and the HR Director (for PD scheduling within collective-bargaining constraints).

  - National Reading Panel (2000) five pillars

  - State dyslexia screening mandate (universal K-2 screening, 3x/year)

  - Teacher survey (n=214): 22% report "highly prepared" to teach phonics
  - Prior curriculum: balanced-literacy program (Fountas & Pinnell); Reading Recovery in 4 schools
  - Current screener: DIBELS 8th Edition through spring 2025; Acadience Reading will replace DIBELS beginning fall 2025 so the district has one universal screener platform
  - Assessment windows: BOY (first 20 school days), MOY (January), EOY (last 20 school days); diagnostic reviews occur within 10 school days of each screener window
- **Resources:**
  - $1.2M in state structured-literacy grant funds (2-year award)
  - 2 reading coaches currently on staff; 3 new coaching positions approved, but only 3.0 total coach FTE can be funded in Year 1
  - PD calendar: 4 full days (pre-service August, November, February, June); 2 early-release Wednesdays/month; outside-contract PD capped at 18 hours annually
- **Constraints:**
  - Collective bargaining limits PD to 18 hours outside contract time annually and prefers no more than 2 after-school sessions per month.
  - Title I compliance required at 6 of 11 schools (parent engagement plan, set-aside allocations).
  - Board reports quarterly (September, December, March, June).
  - State ESSA reporting deadline is October 30 for fall data.
  - Materials orders must be placed by May 15 to arrive before August PD.

  - K-2 schools: 6 schools, grouped into 3 rollout cohorts of 2 schools each
  - 3-5 schools: 5 schools, grouped into 2 rollout cohorts of 2 and 3 schools

  - Teachers' union leadership (REA) cautiously supportive; president sits on curriculum council.

  - School Board is unanimous on adoption direction but split on pace.

## Artifact specification
Year-1 adoption framework document. This is a **strategic curriculum / PD planning artifact**, not classroom lesson plans.

Required sections:
1. **Executive summary** (1 page): purpose, rationale (science of reading), key metrics, board-reportable outcomes.
2. **Theory of action / logic model**: if-then chain from PD inputs -> classroom practice change -> student outcomes. Name assumptions.
3. **Scope & sequence of rollout** (by quarter, Aug 2025 - Jun 2026): which schools, grade bands, and components launch when. Include Year-0 pilot schools if any, but v1 should make one concrete pacing choice and defend it.
4. **Professional learning plan**:
   - PD series outline (topic, hours, facilitator, audience, success measures)
   - Coaching model (cycle length, FTE allocation, observation tool alignment)
   - Differentiated tracks for veteran vs. novice teachers
   - Administrator learning (principals need to recognize structured literacy in walkthroughs)
5. **Implementation fidelity plan**:
   - Walkthrough tool (look-fors tied to Tier 1 structured-literacy components: phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocab, comprehension)
   - Observation cadence and data flow
   - Fidelity-to-program metrics (e.g., minutes/day of phonics in K-2, scope-and-sequence pacing)
6. **Assessment transition plan**: screener, diagnostic, progress-monitoring, summative — named tools, windows, and data review cycles. Resolve the DIBELS-to-Acadience transition explicitly.
7. **Equity and MTSS alignment**: how Tier 1 shift reduces Tier 3 overreferral; ELL and dyslexia specifics.
8. **Budget narrative** (high level): allocation of $1.2M across materials, PD, coaches, assessments.
9. **Risks and mitigations** (table): at least 5 named risks (e.g., teacher resistance, material shortage, early data dip).
10. **Board-reportable KPIs and reporting cadence**.
11. **Legacy materials transition**: what happens to leveled readers, running records, and Reading Recovery staffing.

Pedagogical conventions: backwards design from student outcomes; logic-model framing (Kellogg Foundation); MTSS tiers (1-2-3); science of reading alignment; adult learning theory (Knowles) for PD design; implementation science (NIRN frameworks — Fixsen & Blase).

**Reference frames the model should consider:**
- **Scarborough's Reading Rope**: strands of word recognition (phonological awareness, decoding, sight recognition) and language comprehension (background knowledge, vocabulary, language structures, verbal reasoning, literacy knowledge).
- **Simple View of Reading**: Reading Comprehension = Decoding x Language Comprehension (both required).
- **National Reading Panel pillars**: phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, comprehension.
- **NIRN implementation stages**: Exploration -> Installation -> Initial Implementation -> Full Implementation.
- **Adult learning principles (Knowles)**: relevance, autonomy, experience-based, problem-centered.

## Output format
Single Markdown file with tables and a theory-of-action diagram rendered in ASCII or described. ~2,500-3,500 words.

## Iteration targets
1. **Sharpen the theory of action** — v1 often lists activities without causal chains; force explicit links from PD hours -> observable teacher moves -> student metrics.
2. **Lock the rollout choice** — v1 should choose one paced rollout model (for example, cohort rollout or full-district launch) and tie it to coach capacity and PD hours.
3. **Make fidelity look-fors observable** — move from "teachers use evidence-based practices" to countable actions on the walkthrough tool.
4. **Resolve the assessment transition** — v1 should name the district’s universal screener, the phase-out date for the old tool, and the first year data-review cadence.
5. **Address the F&P legacy** — explicit plan for what happens to leveled-reader libraries, running records, and Reading Recovery staff; this is politically hot.
6. **Budget realism** — check that PD hours, coach FTE, and materials can actually be purchased within $1.2M.
7. **Tighten KPIs** — each KPI must be measurable with a named data source, cadence, and threshold.

## Success criteria
- Theory of action / logic model is present and traces a complete inputs -> activities -> outputs -> outcomes chain.
- Rollout sequence is quarter-by-quarter with named schools or cohorts and one defended pacing decision.
- PD plan specifies facilitator, audience, duration, and success measure for each session, with no more than 18 hours outside contract time.
- Fidelity walkthrough tool lists observable look-fors tied to the five pillars and distinguishes program fidelity from practice fidelity.
- Assessment transition names DIBELS and Acadience, with a single implementation decision and windows.
- Budget lines sum to ~$1.2M with stated tradeoffs.
- At least 5 risks are named with mitigations and owners.
- Board-facing success criteria are thresholded and rubric-like, with explicit green/yellow/red bands or comparable cut scores, not generic ("on track" language alone is insufficient).
- Document avoids prescribing lesson-level content — it frames teacher enactment, not scripts.

## Scope target
At 15 minutes, v1 should include:
- Executive summary sized to a board study-session handout.
- Draft theory of action (logic model boxes labeled; arrows drawn; assumptions listed even if brief).
- A quarterly rollout table with one concrete cohort sequence.
- PD series outline with topics and hours, using the available full days and early-release time without exceeding the contract-time cap.
- Preliminary walkthrough look-fors list (at least 8 items tied to the five pillars).
- Skeletal risk register (5 risks named with mitigation seeds and owner roles).
- A brief assessment-transition note naming the screener decision and the first year review cycle.
- A short legacy-materials transition subsection with named owners.

Budget narrative, equity/MTSS section, board KPIs, and family communication plan can stay concise in v1, but they should no longer be placeholders.

## Evaluator notes (for fixture use)
- Strong v1 reads as a **district strategy document**, not classroom lesson plans. The artifact should read like something the superintendent and board review.
- Strong v1 uses the phrase "theory of action" or "logic model" explicitly and draws a causal chain. Weak v1 has a list of activities with no causal framing.
- Strong v1 sequences 11 schools realistically — either a phased cohort rollout or a full adoption with explicit risk mitigations. Weak v1 handwaves "all schools will implement".
- Strong v1 addresses the F&P legacy (running records, leveled libraries, Reading Recovery) with a named transition path. Weak v1 ignores sunk costs and political resistance.
- Strong v1 walkthrough look-fors are observable/countable (e.g., "teacher uses Elkonin boxes during phonemic awareness segment") not evaluative abstractions ("teacher is effective").
- Strong v1 budget has named line items that plausibly sum to $1.2M; tradeoffs stated (e.g., "coach FTE chosen over additional tutoring").
- Strong v1 explicitly treats implementation dip (student data may dip before rising during Year 1) as an expected signal, not a failure.
- Strong v1 distinguishes **fidelity to program** (following scope and sequence) from **fidelity to instructional practice** (quality of delivery).

## Pedagogical vocabulary glossary
- **Science of reading**: Interdisciplinary body of research on how skilled reading develops; emphasizes systematic, explicit phonics in early grades.
- **Structured literacy**: Operationalization of science of reading — systematic, cumulative, explicit, diagnostic.
- **Balanced literacy**: Prior framework emphasizing independent reading with leveled texts and cueing strategies (three-cueing); now widely critiqued.
- **MTSS**: Multi-Tiered System of Supports; Tier 1 (core), Tier 2 (targeted small group), Tier 3 (intensive individual).
- **Screener vs. diagnostic vs. progress-monitoring**: Screener identifies risk quickly; diagnostic pinpoints gaps; progress-monitoring tracks response to intervention.
- **Walkthrough**: Brief classroom observation (5-15 min) collecting look-fors, not evaluative.
- **Coaching cycle**: Pre-conference -> observation -> post-conference; typically 2-6 weeks per goal.
- **Implementation dip**: Temporary decline when adults are still learning a new practice (Fullan).
- **Three-cueing (MSV)**: Discredited strategy (Meaning, Structure, Visual) where students guess from context/pictures.
- **Orthographic mapping**: Cognitive process forming connections between letter sequences and pronunciations/meanings (Ehri).
- **Decodable text**: Text using phonics patterns students have been explicitly taught.

## Theory-of-action seed
**If** the district provides 32 hours of content-rich PD, coaching cycles averaging 6 weeks, and fidelity walkthroughs 2x/month, **then** K-2 teachers will deliver 45+ minutes of explicit, systematic phonics daily with 80% fidelity, **which will** lead to a 10-15 percentage-point gain in K-2 universal screener benchmark status by spring, **and reduce** Tier 3 MTSS caseload by 15% within 18 months. Named assumptions: teachers engage PD in good faith, materials arrive on time, coaches have bandwidth, admin walkthroughs align with coach feedback.

## Board communication expectations
The artifact should anticipate that the board will ask:
- "How will we know this is working?" (KPIs, data review cadence)
- "What's the cost?" (budget lines, two-year projection)
- "What about our current staff?" (PD pathway, redeployment plan)
- "Will this help our struggling readers?" (MTSS integration, Tier 3 projection)
- "Are we moving too fast or too slow?" (rollout rationale)
- "How will we communicate with families?" (engagement plan, translated materials)

- Multilingual-program specifics beyond ELA.
- Year 2 full roadmap (appears only as sketch).
