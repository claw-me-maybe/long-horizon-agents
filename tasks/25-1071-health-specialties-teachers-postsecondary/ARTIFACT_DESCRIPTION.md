# BSN Year 3 OSCE Station Design: Acute Heart Failure Assessment & SBAR Handoff

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 25-1071
- **Title:** Health Specialties Teachers, Postsecondary

## Scenario
Dr. Okafor, PhD, RN, CNE, is a tenure-track Associate Professor of Nursing at St. Catherine University's BSN program. She coordinates NURS 3410 (Adult Health II) for a cohort of 64 third-year BSN students split across 4 clinical sections. She must design one station for the spring high-stakes summative Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE), specifically assessing acute decompensated heart failure (ADHF) focused assessment and SBAR handoff to a charge nurse. The OSCE follows a 10-station circuit (8 min encounter + 2 min transition). This station will be standardized-patient based with a trained SP.

## Inputs
- **Accreditation / program framing:**
  - AACN Essentials (2021): Domain 2 (Person-Centered Care), Domain 6 (Interprofessional Partnerships), Domain 9 (Professionalism), Domain 10 (Personal, Professional, & Leadership Development)
  - NCSBN NCLEX-RN Test Plan (2023): Physiological Adaptation; Safety & Infection Control; Management of Care
  - QSEN competencies: Patient-Centered Care, Teamwork & Collaboration, Safety, Informatics
  - Program-level BSN outcomes: "Apply clinical judgment using the NCSBN Clinical Judgment Measurement Model (CJMM)"
- **Course context:**
  - Prior week: cardiac A&P review, HF pathophysiology lecture (left- vs right-sided), pharmacology (loop diuretics, ACE-I, beta-blockers, SGLT2 inhibitors)
  - Cohort clinical performance data: median students weak on (a) lung-sound interpretation, (b) structured handoff
  - Simulation lab: Laerdal SimMan 3G available; Lippincott DocuCare for simulated charting; 2 SPs trained
- **Case seed:** Mr. R, 68, h/o HFrEF (EF 30%), presenting with 3-day worsening dyspnea, 2-pillow orthopnea, 4-lb weight gain, bilateral leg edema, crackles to mid-lungs, SpO2 88% RA, BP 158/94, HR 108, RR 26, JVD present.
- **Station logistics:** 8-minute encounter; student enters with hand-off from ED nurse; must perform focused cardiopulmonary assessment, identify ADHF indicators, prioritize nursing interventions, and deliver SBAR to charge nurse (faculty or second SP) within time.
- **Evaluation:** Dual scoring — faculty rater with analytic rubric + SP global rating scale.

## Artifact specification
Complete OSCE station packet using **Miller's Pyramid (Shows How level)** and **standardized-patient simulation best practices (ASPE Standards of Best Practice)**.

Required sections:
1. **Station metadata**: station number, title, length, setting (ED bay vs. med-surg), rater type.
2. **Learning objectives** mapped to AACN Essentials, QSEN, and NCLEX-RN test plan categories. Use measurable verbs (Bloom's analyze/apply/evaluate).
3. **Candidate instructions** (what student reads at door, <150 words, realistic stem).
4. **SP role/script**:
   - Demographics, back-story, affect
   - Symptoms and vital signs to verbalize / permit palpation
   - Scripted responses to likely student questions
   - Progression rules (what changes if student forgets X)
   - Safety/boundary notes for the SP
5. **Setup checklist**: room, equipment (stethoscope, BP cuff, pulse-ox, SCDs, IV pole), moulage (pitting edema pads, JVD markings), documentation access.
6. **Analytic scoring rubric** (minimum 15 items across domains):
   - Focused history (HPI, PMH, meds, allergies)
   - Focused physical (cardiac, pulmonary, peripheral)
   - Clinical judgment (recognizes ADHF, prioritizes oxygen + diuretic anticipation)
   - Communication (therapeutic, closed-loop)
   - SBAR structure (S/B/A/R each scored)
   - Safety (hand hygiene, two identifiers)
7. **Global rating scale** (SP/Faculty): professionalism, empathy, overall competency (5-pt anchored scale).
8. **Cut-score / pass-fail rationale**: borderline regression method or compensatory model stated.
9. **Debrief prompts** (Debriefing with Good Judgment / PEARLS framework): 4-6 questions, post-circuit.
10. **Accommodations plan**: testing accommodations for students with documented disabilities (extended time, reader).
11. **Remediation pathway** for failing candidates.

Pedagogical conventions: Miller's Pyramid; CJMM (Recognize Cues -> Analyze Cues -> Prioritize -> Generate Solutions -> Take Action -> Evaluate); ASPE SOBP for SP methodology; PEARLS for debrief.

## Output format
Single Markdown file, ~1,800-2,500 words. Must include a rubric table. SBAR handoff exemplar included as an appendix.

## Iteration targets
1. **Discriminate competent from borderline** — v1 rubrics often only separate novice vs. expert; refine items so the borderline candidate (safe but slow) can be distinguished from unsafe.
2. **Right-size the 8-minute scope** — cut history items if the station cannot realistically be completed; don't design an impossible station.
3. **SP standardization** — add explicit triggers for SP behavior change so scoring variance drops across 64 candidates over a day.
4. **CJMM alignment** — ensure rubric items map to all 6 CJMM layers, not just assessment and intervention.
5. **SBAR rubric granularity** — break SBAR into scorable components with exemplar language, not just "delivers SBAR adequately".
6. **Psychometric defensibility** — specify cut-score method and inter-rater calibration plan (pre-OSCE rater training).

## Success criteria
- Learning objectives are measurable and each maps to at least one AACN Essential and one NCLEX category.
- SP script includes both symptom presentation AND scripted responses to at least 8 anticipated student questions.
- Rubric items are behaviorally anchored (not "good/poor" but observable actions).
- Total scorable items match the 8-min time budget (~1 item per 30 sec is upper limit).
- Cut-score method is named and defended.
- Accommodations and remediation pathways are present and specific.
- SBAR exemplar demonstrates proper structure with clinical data from the case.

## Scope target
At 15 minutes, v1 should include: station metadata, learning objectives with mappings, candidate instructions, SP backstory and vitals, a draft rubric covering the major domains (even if items are rough), and a SBAR exemplar. Debrief prompts, accommodations, remediation, and cut-score psychometrics are expected to be stubs for iteration.
