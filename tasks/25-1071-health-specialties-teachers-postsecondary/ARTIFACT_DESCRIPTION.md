# BSN Year 3 OSCE Station Design: Acute Heart Failure Assessment & SBAR Handoff

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 25-1071
- **Title:** Health Specialties Teachers, Postsecondary

## Scenario
Dr. Okafor, PhD, RN, CNE, is a tenure-track Associate Professor of Nursing at St. Catherine University's CCNE-accredited BSN program. She coordinates NURS 3410 (Adult Health II: Cardiopulmonary & Renal) for a cohort of 64 third-year BSN students split across 4 clinical sections rotating through a tertiary academic medical center. She must design one station for the spring high-stakes summative Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE), specifically assessing acute decompensated heart failure (ADHF) focused assessment and SBAR handoff to a charge nurse. The OSCE follows a 10-station circuit (8-min encounter + 2-min transition). This station will be standardized-patient (SP) based with a trained SP; faculty raters will score live. The station result contributes 15% of the course grade and is a programmatic progression checkpoint (a student who fails two stations cannot progress to NURS 4410 Capstone Practicum). The program is preparing for CCNE re-accreditation in 2027 and must document psychometrically defensible assessment practices.

## Inputs
- **Accreditation / program framing:**
  - AACN Essentials (2021): Domain 2 (Person-Centered Care), Domain 6 (Interprofessional Partnerships), Domain 9 (Professionalism), Domain 10 (Personal, Professional, & Leadership Development)
  - NCSBN NCLEX-RN Test Plan (2023): Physiological Adaptation; Safety & Infection Control; Management of Care
  - QSEN competencies: Patient-Centered Care, Teamwork & Collaboration, Safety, Informatics
  - Program-level BSN outcomes: "Apply clinical judgment using the NCSBN Clinical Judgment Measurement Model (CJMM)"
- **Course context:**
  - Prior week: cardiac A&P review, HF pathophysiology lecture (left- vs right-sided), pharmacology (loop diuretics, ACE-I, beta-blockers, SGLT2 inhibitors)
  - Cohort clinical performance data: median students weak on (a) lung-sound interpretation, (b) structured handoff
  - Simulation lab: Laerdal SimMan 3G available; Lippincott DocuCare for simulated charting; 2 SPs trained
- **Case seed:** Mr. R, 68 y/o, h/o HFrEF (EF 30%), hypertension, T2DM, on furosemide 40 mg PO daily + metoprolol succinate 50 mg PO daily + lisinopril 10 mg PO daily + empagliflozin 10 mg PO daily. Presents with 3-day worsening dyspnea, 2-pillow orthopnea, 4-lb weight gain over 3 days, bilateral lower-extremity pitting edema (+2), bibasilar crackles to mid-lungs, SpO2 88% room air, BP 158/94, HR 108 regular, RR 26 labored, T 98.2F, JVD present at 45 degrees. States he "ran out of water pills" 4 days ago and "ate some salty ham" over the weekend.
- **Station logistics:**
  - 8-minute encounter, 2-minute transition to next station.
  - Student reads door stem for 1 minute before entering (not counted in the 8 min).
  - Student enters with a hand-off from ED nurse (written 1-page brief).
  - Student must: (1) perform focused cardiopulmonary assessment, (2) identify ADHF indicators, (3) prioritize immediate nursing interventions (oxygen, positioning, anticipate loop diuretic), (4) deliver SBAR to charge nurse within the 8 min.
  - Charge nurse role played by faculty rater who enters at minute 6 for SBAR.
- **Evaluation:** Dual scoring — faculty rater with analytic rubric + SP global rating scale. Raters must complete a 2-hour calibration training before test day.
- **Existing infrastructure:** The program has a station-development template used for prior OSCEs; a post-encounter note (PEN) bank; and an item-review committee that meets monthly.

## Artifact specification
Complete OSCE station packet using **Miller's Pyramid (Shows How level)** and **standardized-patient simulation best practices (ASPE Standards of Best Practice)**.

Required sections:
1. **Station metadata**: station number, title, length, setting (ED bay vs. med-surg), rater type.
2. **Learning objectives** mapped to AACN Essentials, QSEN, and NCLEX-RN test plan categories. Use measurable verbs (Bloom's analyze/apply/evaluate).
3. **Candidate instructions** (what student reads at door, <150 words, realistic stem).
4. **SP role/script**:
   - Demographics, back-story, affect
   - Symptoms and vital signs to verbalize / permit palpation
   - Scripted responses to likely student questions
   - Progression rules (what changes if student forgets X)
   - Safety/boundary notes for the SP
5. **Setup checklist**: room, equipment (stethoscope, BP cuff, pulse-ox, SCDs, IV pole), moulage (pitting edema pads, JVD markings), documentation access.
6. **Analytic scoring rubric** (minimum 15 items across domains):
   - Focused history (HPI, PMH, meds, allergies)
   - Focused physical (cardiac, pulmonary, peripheral)
   - Clinical judgment (recognizes ADHF, prioritizes oxygen + diuretic anticipation)
   - Communication (therapeutic, closed-loop)
   - SBAR structure (S/B/A/R each scored)
   - Safety (hand hygiene, two identifiers)
7. **Global rating scale** (SP/Faculty): professionalism, empathy, overall competency (5-pt anchored scale).
8. **Cut-score / pass-fail rationale**: borderline regression method or compensatory model stated.
9. **Debrief prompts** (Debriefing with Good Judgment / PEARLS framework): 4-6 questions, post-circuit.
10. **Accommodations plan**: testing accommodations for students with documented disabilities (extended time, reader).
11. **Remediation pathway** for failing candidates.

Pedagogical conventions: Miller's Pyramid; CJMM (Recognize Cues -> Analyze Cues -> Prioritize -> Generate Solutions -> Take Action -> Evaluate); ASPE SOBP for SP methodology; PEARLS for debrief.

## Output format
Single Markdown file, ~1,800-2,500 words. Must include a rubric table. SBAR handoff exemplar included as an appendix.

## Iteration targets
1. **Discriminate competent from borderline** — v1 rubrics often only separate novice vs. expert; refine items so the borderline candidate (safe but slow) can be distinguished from unsafe.
2. **Right-size the 8-minute scope** — cut history items if the station cannot realistically be completed; don't design an impossible station.
3. **SP standardization** — add explicit triggers for SP behavior change so scoring variance drops across 64 candidates over a day.
4. **CJMM alignment** — ensure rubric items map to all 6 CJMM layers, not just assessment and intervention.
5. **SBAR rubric granularity** — break SBAR into scorable components with exemplar language, not just "delivers SBAR adequately".
6. **Psychometric defensibility** — specify cut-score method and inter-rater calibration plan (pre-OSCE rater training).

## Success criteria
- Learning objectives are measurable and each maps to at least one AACN Essential and one NCLEX category.
- SP script includes both symptom presentation AND scripted responses to at least 8 anticipated student questions.
- Rubric items are behaviorally anchored (not "good/poor" but observable actions).
- Total scorable items match the 8-min time budget (~1 item per 30 sec is upper limit).
- Cut-score method is named and defended.
- Accommodations and remediation pathways are present and specific.
- SBAR exemplar demonstrates proper structure with clinical data from the case.

## Scope target
At 15 minutes, v1 should include:
- Station metadata (title, length, setting).
- Learning objectives with at least one mapping each to AACN Essential, QSEN, and NCLEX-RN category.
- Candidate instructions (door stem).
- SP backstory, vitals, and at least 5 scripted responses to likely questions.
- A draft rubric covering the major domains (at least 10 items, behaviorally anchored even if rough).
- A SBAR exemplar showing S/B/A/R structure with the case data.

Debrief prompts, full accommodations pathway, remediation pathway, cut-score psychometrics, and inter-rater calibration plan are expected to be stubs for iteration.

## Evaluator notes (for fixture use)
- Strong v1 writes candidate instructions <150 words and clinically realistic. Weak v1 writes a 400-word explanatory paragraph.
- Strong v1 SP script includes **progression rules** (what the SP does if the student fails to ask a key question by minute 4). Weak v1 describes only static symptoms.
- Strong v1 rubric items are **observable** ("student auscultates posterior lung fields with stethoscope on skin") not evaluative ("student does good assessment").
- Strong v1 rubric fits within the 8-min window (roughly <24 items; 1 item per ~30 sec maximum).
- Strong v1 names a **cut-score method** (Angoff, Borderline Regression, Hofstee, or simple compensatory with minimums). Weak v1 says "pass is 70%".
- Strong v1 addresses **rater training** (2-hour calibration) as part of psychometric defensibility.
- Strong v1 SBAR exemplar reads aloud in ~60 seconds and demonstrates closed-loop communication.
- Strong v1 anticipates **safety-critical failures** (e.g., student places patient supine with SpO2 88% — immediate fail) separate from minor technique issues.
- Strong v1 connects to **CJMM** throughout (not only at one step) — recognize cues (abnormal lung sounds, edema) -> analyze (ADHF vs. PNA vs. MI) -> prioritize (oxygen first) -> generate (diuretic anticipation, positioning, monitoring) -> act -> evaluate.

## SBAR exemplar (starter — for the downstream model to refine)
- **S (Situation)**: "Charge nurse, this is [student name]. I'm at bedside with Mr. R in Bay 3. He's a 68-year-old with known HFrEF, EF 30%, here for acute worsening shortness of breath over 3 days."
- **B (Background)**: "His home meds are furosemide 40 PO daily, metoprolol, lisinopril, and empagliflozin. He stopped his furosemide 4 days ago. He's had 4 pounds of weight gain and now has 2-pillow orthopnea. Past history includes HTN and T2DM."
- **A (Assessment)**: "Vitals: BP 158/94, HR 108 regular, RR 26 labored, SpO2 88% on room air, afebrile. Exam: bibasilar crackles to mid-lungs, JVD at 45 degrees, +2 pitting edema to knees. I'm concerned he's in acute decompensated heart failure."
- **R (Recommendation)**: "I've placed him in high-Fowler's and started oxygen at 2 L nasal cannula, now 94%. I'd like you to call the provider so we can get orders for an IV loop diuretic, labs including BNP and troponin, and a chest X-ray. I'll stay with him and monitor vitals every 15 minutes."

## Pedagogical vocabulary glossary
- **OSCE**: Objective Structured Clinical Examination — circuit of timed, standardized clinical stations used in health professions assessment.
- **SP**: Standardized Patient; lay person trained to portray a patient consistently.
- **SBAR**: Situation-Background-Assessment-Recommendation; structured handoff communication (Kaiser Permanente origin).
- **CJMM**: NCSBN Clinical Judgment Measurement Model — 6-layer cognitive framework underlying Next-Gen NCLEX.
- **Miller's Pyramid**: Knows -> Knows How -> Shows How -> Does; OSCE operates at "Shows How".
- **Bloom's taxonomy**: Remember -> Understand -> Apply -> Analyze -> Evaluate -> Create.
- **Borderline Regression**: Defensible cut-score method correlating analytic rubric scores with global judgments (borderline candidates).
- **PEARLS**: Promoting Excellence and Reflective Learning in Simulation; debriefing framework.
- **ASPE SOBP**: Association of Standardized Patient Educators Standards of Best Practice.
- **AACN Essentials (2021)**: Accreditation-framing competencies for nursing education, 10 domains.
- **QSEN**: Quality and Safety Education for Nurses; 6 core competencies.
- **Moulage**: Simulated injury/illness visual effects (edema, wounds, cyanosis) applied to SP.
- **Closed-loop communication**: Speaker says message, receiver repeats back, speaker confirms; core TeamSTEPPS practice.

## Iteration loop expectation
This artifact will be drafted, evaluated, and iterated over 4-6 rounds focused on: rubric discrimination at the borderline, SP script robustness across 64 encounters, CJMM alignment, psychometric defensibility, and reasonable 8-minute scope.

## Anticipated student errors (for the downstream model to consider)
- Fails to elevate head of bed before oxygen application.
- Auscultates only anterior lung fields, missing posterior bibasilar crackles.
- Asks closed questions only ("Do you have chest pain?") instead of open ("Tell me what brought you in today.").
- Interprets BP 158/94 as "within normal limits" because student is anchored on 120/80.
- Forgets to ask about sodium intake or medication adherence.
- Delivers SBAR in free narrative instead of structured format.
- Recommends discharge teaching before recognizing acute decompensation.
- Skips hand hygiene on entry or exit.
- Fails to use two patient identifiers before touching the patient.
- Misses JVD because exam was performed with HOB flat or at 90 degrees (not 45).
- Gives oxygen at excessive FiO2 (15L NRB when 2L NC would have sufficed).

## Discrimination design (for rubric items)
Each rubric item should distinguish 4 levels. Sample item:
| Item: Auscultates posterior lung fields | 4 (competent+) | 3 (competent) | 2 (borderline) | 1 (unsafe/fail) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Posterior auscultation | Systematically auscultates all 6 posterior zones with stethoscope on skin, notes bibasilar crackles, interprets as ADHF evidence | Auscultates posterior fields and identifies crackles | Auscultates anterior only OR identifies wheezes incorrectly | Does not auscultate posterior OR auscultates over gown | 

## Faculty rater calibration plan (seed)
- Pre-OSCE 2-hour training: review rubric, score sample videos, discuss discrepancies, re-score.
- Target inter-rater agreement: kappa >= 0.70 on held-out video before test day.
- Retraining required if first-hour raters drift (on-site check after first 10 candidates).
- Item-level audit after test day to identify unclear anchors for next revision.

## Accommodations starter list
- ADA-approved testing accommodations for students with documented disabilities: 1.5x time on the 8-min station; separate testing environment; reader for door stem; ASL interpreter where applicable (interpreter does not translate clinical judgment).
- Psychological safety: pre-station orientation describing the day, the rater role, and the fact that errors are expected/normal.
- Post-station support: same-day debrief for any student who experienced distress; counseling referral available.
