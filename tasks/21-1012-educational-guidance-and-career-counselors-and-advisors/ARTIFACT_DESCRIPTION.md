## Artifact specification
Produce a written **Junior-Year Academic and College-Career Plan** for Maya R., formatted as a counselor-authored planning document suitable for the student's cumulative file, the guardian conference, and handoff to the dual-enrollment partner counselor.

Required sections, in order:
1. **Header block** — student pseudonym, ID, grade, date, counselor name and credential, next review date.
2. **Strengths and interests summary** — synthesize the Strong Interest Inventory, ONET, and extracurricular engagement using strengths-based, person-first language. Do not speculate about diagnoses or clinical conditions.
3. **Academic standing and course plan** — current performance, credit audit against Oregon diploma requirements (24 credits; 4 English, 3 Math, 3 Science, 3 Social Studies, 2 Second Language or CTE, 1 PE, 1 Health, 6 electives; Essential Skills demonstration), and a proposed senior-year schedule with rationale.
4. **Post-secondary options analysis** — at minimum three distinct pathways (e.g., four-year direct admit, community college transfer, direct-entry CTE/apprenticeship or healthcare certificate), each with named programs. Use real post-secondary options appropriate to the Pacific Northwest (e.g., OHSU School of Nursing, Oregon Health & Science University, University of Portland, Oregon State, Portland State, Cascade Community College, Chemeketa CC, Linn-Benton CC, OHSU-PSU School of Public Health).
5. **Career exploration and next-step actions** — informational interviews, job shadows, certifications (e.g., Red Cross CPR/BLS, CNA via Cascade CC summer cohort), and a summer 2026 action list.
6. **Testing and application timeline** — SAT/ACT retake recommendation with dates, AP exam considerations, FAFSA/ORSAA opening (Oct 1, 2026), Common App, OSAC scholarship deadlines (typical March 1 deadline for the 2027 cycle), and letter-of-recommendation requests.
7. **Supports and accommodations** — math support plan (peer tutoring, office hours, formative self-checks), any family-requested language support for meetings, and how progress will be monitored.
8. **Family partnership and student voice** — a short paragraph capturing Maya's own stated goals in her words (paraphrased from prior notes) and the guardian's stated priorities, plus how the plan reflects both.
9. **Signatures / acknowledgments block** — lines for student, guardian, counselor; note that this is a planning document, not a contract or a diagnosis.

**Tone and conventions**
- Person-first, strengths-based language throughout ("Maya, an 11th grader who...," not "a struggling math student").
- Avoid diagnostic or clinical speculation (no "appears to have anxiety," "seems ADHD-like," etc.). If emotional factors are relevant, name observable behaviors and refer to appropriate supports.
- Use ASCA Mindsets & Behaviors framing where it fits naturally (do not force citations into every bullet).
- Protect confidentiality: use the pseudonym, not any invented real name; no SSN, DOB, or address.
- Professional, parent-readable register. Plain-language explanations for acronyms on first use (PSAT, FAFSA, ORSAA, OSAC, NMSF, CTE, CNA, BLS).
- Honest about trade-offs: do not oversell any one pathway.

## Output format
Single Markdown file, approximately 600-900 words in the plan body (plus tables and the signature block). No external links required; program names may be referenced without URLs.

## Iteration targets
1. **Strengthen affordability analysis.** V1 likely names programs without naming concrete cost levers (OSAC, Oregon Promise for CC, Pell, OHSU Partnership transfer program, in-district tuition waivers). Add these by name and match them to each pathway.
2. **Make the math support plan specific and measurable.** Replace generic "get tutoring" with a weekly cadence, named resource (e.g., peer-mentor program), checkpoint dates before final exam, and a criterion for escalation (e.g., grade still below C at 3-week check).
3. **Tighten person-first, strengths-based language.** Audit the draft for any deficit framing ("struggling," "weak in math," "low scorer") and rewrite using observable behaviors and assets.
4. **Balance the three pathways.** V1 often over-weights the four-year option. Ensure the community-college-transfer and direct-entry CNA/healthcare pathways are described with equal specificity, including named programs, timelines, and licensure endpoints.
5. **Sharpen the testing timeline.** Give concrete 2026 SAT/ACT administration dates (pull realistic months: August, October, November, December 2026) and tie each to a decision point (e.g., ED/EA deadlines, OSU priority deadline Nov 1).
6. **Integrate student and guardian voice.** V1 frequently writes in counselor-only voice. Surface Maya's own stated interest in nursing and the guardian's affordability priority explicitly in the plan, not only in the appendix.

## Scope target
A 15-minute v1 should contain: the header block, a one-paragraph strengths/interests summary drawing on the Strong Interest Inventory and ONET results, a current-standing table plus a proposed senior-year schedule, at least three named post-secondary pathways with one or two sentences each, a bulleted testing/application timeline with real deadlines, a short math support paragraph, and a signature block. It is acceptable for v1 to be light on affordability specifics, to have uneven depth across the three pathways, and to under-quote student/guardian voice — these are the primary iteration targets.
