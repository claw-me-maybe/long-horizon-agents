# Account Plan: Brightshelf Market Holdings — FY26 Private-Label Coffee Expansion ($1.8M Opportunity)

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 41-4012
- **Title:** Sales Representatives, Wholesale and Manufacturing, Except Technical and Scientific Products

## Scenario
You are the Senior Account Executive for Midwest Grocery at Fernhollow Roasters, a mid-size specialty coffee roaster and private-label manufacturer ($112M revenue, based in Grand Rapids, MI). Brightshelf Market Holdings — a 38-store regional grocery chain across Michigan, Indiana, and Ohio — is expanding its private-label "Brightshelf Reserve" coffee program from 4 SKUs to 10 SKUs for FY26 reset (planogram lock June 26, 2026). The incumbent supplier, Westbrook Coffee Co., holds 3 of the 4 current SKUs; Fernhollow holds 1 (a single-origin Ethiopia). Your VP Sales, Marta Benedict, expects a written account plan by Monday, April 27, 2026, to secure internal ops, marketing, and exec alignment before your April 30 meeting with Brightshelf's Category Manager Darnell Pike. Annual opportunity if we take 6 of the 10 SKUs: ~$1.8M at run-rate (year 1 ramped), with follow-on cold-brew concentrate add at ~$420K in FY27.

## Inputs
The model starts with:

- **Account fact base (from CRM + prior trip reports):**
  - Brightshelf HQ: Kalamazoo, MI. CEO Lorraine Teague (operator-owner, 2nd-gen). Pvt-label coffee accounts for ~6.8% of total store dollars.
  - Last three buying meetings with Pike were cordial but not decisive; Pike is new (14 months in role, promoted from store-ops).
  - VP Merchandising Carl Yoon (Pike's boss) — strategic decision-maker, values "margin-neutral innovation," skeptical of supplier changes mid-program.
  - CFO Renata Shah — aggressive on COGS; signed off on a 3% price concession from Westbrook in Q3 2025.
  - Store-level insight: District 3 (Ohio, 11 stores) is underperforming category on coffee vs. other regions by ~9% per a Circana report Pike shared informally.
- **Product set we can offer:**
  - Brightshelf-spec 12oz ground blends: Classic, Breakfast, French Roast, Decaf Dark — FOB $5.80/unit at 2,400-unit MOQ per SKU per run.
  - Whole-bean 12oz single origin: Ethiopia Yirgacheffe (current SKU), Colombia Supremo, Sumatra Mandheling — FOB $7.10/unit.
  - Cold-brew concentrate 32oz: FOB $6.20/unit (new SKU for FY27 discussion).
  - Compostable pod 12-pack: FOB $4.95/unit — Westbrook does not currently offer this.
- **Commercial terms we can propose:**
  - Base list with volume rebate tiers at $1.2M / $1.6M / $2.0M annual spend (1.5% / 2.25% / 3.0% back).
  - Slotting allowance: up to $8K/SKU; marketing development funds (MDF) of 2% of net.
  - Free-freight threshold: 12 pallets per order to DCs in Toledo (OH), Plainwell (MI), Whitestown (IN).
- **Competitive intel:**
  - Westbrook pricing is ~3-5% below ours at spec parity on ground blends (per Pike's Dec '25 comment) but sourcing is single-origin Brazil-heavy — a known risk given 2025 frost damage in Minas Gerais.
  - Westbrook does not run a sustainability certification; Brightshelf shoppers over-index on "fair trade" per Brightshelf's own 2025 shopper study (provided by Pike).
  - Rainforest Alliance and Fair Trade USA both offered on our top 5 blends.
- **Relationship timeline:** Fernhollow has sold into Brightshelf since 2021; NPS from Pike's predecessor was 9; our on-time-in-full (OTIF) last 12 months is 97.4% vs. Westbrook's reported 91% (Pike's complaint, Feb).

## Artifact specification
A written Account Plan for internal distribution to the Fernhollow deal team (VP Sales, VP Ops, CMO, CFO), used to align on asks and commitments before the April 30 Brightshelf meeting. Required sections:
1. **Executive summary** — the $1.8M opportunity framed against FY26 quota; the specific SKU ask; the decision date.
2. **Account snapshot** — Brightshelf business, category context, where private-label coffee sits for them.
3. **Stakeholder map** — at minimum: Pike, Yoon, Shah, Teague; each with role, stance today (champion/neutral/skeptic/blocker), what they care about, and our next action.
4. **Opportunity sizing** — SKU-by-SKU mix, unit forecasts, revenue and gross margin, with a base/upside/downside case.
5. **Competitive displacement strategy** — why we can take SKUs from Westbrook, where we will not try, and where we add whitespace (e.g., compostable pods).
6. **Commercial proposal framing** — the rebate tier structure we will lead with, MDF/slotting we'll commit, and margin floor we will not cross. Include a "walk-away" position.
7. **Next-best-actions** — a dated 6–10 step plan through June 26 planogram lock, with owner and dependency.
8. **Risks and mitigations** — at least four, grounded in this account (not generic).
9. **Internal asks** — specific commitments needed from Ops (freight), CMO (MDF), CFO (tier approval).

Tone: professional, commercially confident, specific. Written for an internal audience that will hold you accountable for the numbers — so no puffery. Uses account language ("planogram," "reset," "MDF," "OTIF") correctly.

## Output format
Markdown document, ~4–6 pages rendered. At least three tables (SKU/pricing, stakeholder map, next-best-actions). Include a one-page summary at the top suitable for screen-sharing in a 10-minute internal call.

## Iteration targets
1. **Turn the stakeholder map into a real map, not a list.** v1 typically lists four names with one sentence each. Iterate to include stance (champion/neutral/skeptic/blocker), decision weight, and the *specific* next touch for each (e.g., "send Shah a COGS sensitivity chart by 4/22 via Pike").
2. **Make the opportunity sizing defensible SKU-by-SKU.** v1 tends to present one $1.8M number. Better: a SKU-by-SKU table with unit velocity assumption, price, margin, and a base/upside/downside that reconciles to $1.8M in the base.
3. **Ground the displacement plan in real levers.** v1 will write "better quality." Iterate to Westbrook's Brazil-sourcing risk, their 91% OTIF vs. our 97.4%, and the fair-trade-certification gap against shopper data.
4. **Give the commercial proposal a defensible floor.** v1 quotes list price. Iterate to: lead offer, a step-down, and a documented margin floor below which we walk, with justification.
5. **Date the next-best-actions and sequence them.** v1 writes a bulleted list. Iterate to a table with date, action, owner, dependency, and what it unlocks — sequenced to the June 26 planogram lock.
6. **Cover the Yoon objection head-on.** Yoon values "margin-neutral innovation" and dislikes mid-program supplier change. v1 ignores this. Iterate to a specific tactic — e.g., phased SKU add-in, not displacement, in Districts 1–2 and displacement only in District 3 where category is weak.

## Success criteria
(For evaluator use only.)
1. The plan identifies Pike, Yoon, Shah, and Teague by role, names a champion hypothesis, and gives a dated next-touch for each.
2. SKU-level sizing reconciles to the $1.8M base case with visible unit/price/margin math; an upside and downside case are also present.
3. At least two of the three displacement arguments (Westbrook OTIF gap, Brazil sourcing risk, fair-trade certification gap) are present with specifics.
4. Commercial proposal includes both a lead offer and a walk-away margin floor; rebate tiers from the input are referenced correctly.
5. The next-best-actions plan is sequenced toward the June 26 planogram lock with at least 6 dated actions and named owners.
6. The plan proposes a specific tactic to manage Yoon's "no mid-program change" concern (e.g., add-in in strong districts, displace only in underperforming District 3).

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: exec summary with the $1.8M opportunity and April 30 meeting anchor, an account snapshot paragraph, a 4-row stakeholder table with at least stance and top concern, a SKU mix table that sums to $1.8M (even if margins are placeholder), a displacement paragraph citing at least one Westbrook gap, a commercial proposal paragraph referencing the tier structure, and a 5-row next-best-actions list. Upside/downside cases, margin floor justification, and the Yoon-specific tactic can be iteration work.
