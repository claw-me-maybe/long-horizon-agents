# Mailroom and Records Team Shift Redesign Proposal — Caldwell Insurance Group

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 43-1011
- **Title:** First-Line Supervisors of Office and Administrative Support Workers

## Scenario
Caldwell Insurance Group (a mid-sized regional P&C insurer, HQ in Hartford, CT, ~2,100 employees) is shifting claims intake from 80% paper to 40% paper over Q2-Q3 2026 following a new e-FNOL (electronic First Notice of Loss) system launch. You are Alicia Herrera, supervisor of the 14-person Mailroom and Records team. The VP of Operations, Rashid Al-Mansouri, has asked you by April 30 to submit a shift redesign proposal that reduces mailroom staffing by ~20% through attrition while preventing any drop in SLA (current: 94% of incoming mail processed within 4 business hours). The proposal will be reviewed by Ops, HR, and Finance, and becomes the basis for a June 1 rollout.

## Required quantitative exhibits
The proposal must include at least the following calculations, shown not asserted:
1. **Peak-hour coverage math.** Volume during the 9:30-10:15a USPS window ÷ sorting throughput per staffed FTE during that window = coverage ratio. Must exceed 1.0 by a documented margin, with assumption about average pieces-per-sorter-per-hour stated.
2. **SLA preservation math.** Current 94% within 4 hrs baseline; new model projection with ramped e-FNOL and reduced paper. Show at least two projection points: July 1 (mid-ramp) and September 1 (post-ramp).
3. **OT reduction math.** Baseline $47,800 → target $35,850 (25% reduction = $11,950). Which schedule changes contribute how much. Honest acknowledgment if the full 25% is not reachable without concurrent process improvements.
4. **Headcount glide path.** 13.8 FTE today → Tanya retires 7/31 (−1.0) → identify-then-transition (−1.8) → 11.0 FTE by 9/30. Show the gap-closing path.
5. **Training hours budget.** If cross-training is part of the plan, allocate hours and identify who covers productive work during training.

These five exhibits may appear in the SLA model section, the rollout plan, or a dedicated appendix — but they must exist and must be arithmetically correct.

## Artifact specification
Produce a shift redesign proposal with the following sections:
1. **Executive summary** — 1 paragraph: current state, proposed state, staffing delta, expected SLA impact, expected cost impact.
2. **Current state baseline** — volume, staffing, SLA, OT spend, known friction.
3. **Drivers of change** — e-FNOL adoption curve, attrition, Finance target.
4. **Proposed shift schedule** — table, name-by-name, with start/end times, role, days.
5. **Workflow changes** — what happens differently (e.g., cross-train records clerks to scanning; eliminate duplicate misrouted-mail handling; staggered lunch coverage).
6. **Headcount glide path** — how you reach 11.0 FTE by 9/30 (Tanya 7/31, then who/when; no involuntary separations).
7. **Risk register** — 4-6 risks with mitigations (e.g., CBA notice requirements; Omar's role; two-person Records Room rule; scanner maintenance windows).
8. **SLA model** — simple calc showing why the new schedule preserves or improves the 94%-within-4-hrs metric.
9. **Rollout plan** — week-by-week from May 1 through June 15.
10. **Decision requested** — explicit asks of Rashid (approvals, HR partnering, budget).

Tone: direct, non-defensive, numbers-forward. You are advocating for a plan but acknowledging tradeoffs honestly.

## Iteration targets
1. **Tighten the SLA math** — v1 may assert SLA is preserved; v2 shows the calculation: volume during each window ÷ staffed processing capacity per window, with explicit assumptions about e-FNOL ramp.
2. **Name the second attrition** — v1 may leave the path to 11.0 vague; v2 proposes a concrete identification process (e.g., not naming a person but defining criteria: voluntary-first, role-redundancy-based, with a dated decision point) that respects HR policy.
3. **Address Omar directly, respectfully** — the underutilized coordinator should become a cross-trained scanner/intake hybrid, not a target for reduction without a fair look at repurposing.
4. **Honor the CBA** — v2 includes a clear 30-day-notice timeline and calls out which changes trigger bargaining.
5. **Quantify the OT reduction path** — show which schedule changes reduce OT by specific dollar amounts to reach the 25% target.
6. **Replace assertions with mitigations in the risk register** — e.g., don't just note "scanning bottleneck risk"; specify the trigger metric and the response action.

## Success criteria
- Every person currently on the team is placed, cross-trained, or (for Tanya) accounted for in the glide path — no one is invisible.
- The proposal reaches 11.0 FTE by 9/30 without involuntary separation.
- SLA calculation is shown, not asserted.
- CBA 30-day notice is visibly respected in the rollout timeline.
- Decision-requested section contains specific asks, not general "please approve."
- A reader who only reads the executive summary and the shift schedule table can understand the proposal.

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: executive summary, current-state baseline, a proposed shift schedule covering all 13 named team members (plus the vacant req), a basic risk register of 4+ items, and the rollout plan. Sharper SLA math, refined headcount glide path, and nuanced risk mitigations come in iteration.

## Example of v1 failure modes
- Staffing the proposal by headcount target without tying to the volume curve (Mondays 2,100 pieces vs avg 1,420).
- Writing "reduce attrition by 2" without identifying Tanya's 7/31 retirement as leg 1 of 2.
- Assuming e-FNOL growth is linear; the 120→600/day path by August is not linear, and the July step-up matters for OT planning.
- Proposing a shift that violates the 2-person Records Room rule after 5:30p (e.g., leaving Ellis solo 5:30-6:30 without a partner).
- Ignoring Claire's 0.8 FTE floater role — she is the PTO cushion and cannot simply be dropped.
- Treating "overtime cap 10 hrs/week/person" as a soft guideline rather than CBA-binding.
- Writing mitigations as aspirations ("we'll monitor") rather than triggers and responses ("if overnight scanning backlog exceeds 900 for 3 days, add Claire to morning scanning Tues/Thurs").

## Evaluator notes (context for scoring, not for the model)
- The 43-1011 supervisor role here is genuinely iteration-worthy because the proposal blends quantitative modeling (SLA math, OT spend), human judgment (Omar's role, the second attrition path), and constraint compliance (CBA, 2-person rule). A generic "restructure" proposal fails because it cannot simultaneously hit 11.0 FTE, preserve SLA, and respect CBA timing.
- The named team members are fictional. The e-FNOL ramp pattern (120→600 in ~90 days) and the paper-claims decline curve mirror real post-digitization transitions in mid-sized insurers.
- Downstream iteration feedback should focus on: (a) showing the SLA math, not asserting; (b) naming Omar's repurposing path; (c) applying the 30-day CBA clock visibly to the rollout calendar; (d) removing aspirational language from the risk register.

## Common pitfalls to avoid
- Do not name the second attrition target person; define criteria (voluntary, role-redundancy-based, HR-led) and a decision milestone.
- Do not propose cross-training as a one-time event; include capacity reserved in the new schedule for training time (e.g., 4 hrs/week for 6 weeks per retrainee).
- Do not use "streamline" or "optimize" without a specific mechanism and a number.
- Do not conflate the building 2-person-rule after 5:30p with the shift schedule; the rule is a constraint on who can be alone, not on who can work late.
- Do not skip the Kodak i5650 weekly calibration window when redesigning Fridays.
- Do not ignore Henry's 45-min standing-sort accommodation when assigning him continuous shifts.
- Do not schedule Claire to cover Thu/Fri without renegotiating her childcare constraint (cannot simply be imposed).
- Do not route Sam into schedule changes without documented CBA-compliant notice given his 2023 grievance history.
- Do not reorganize Jamie P. out of leadership without a growth-path conversation first; her "something new" signal is an opportunity, not an exit.
- Do not allow the intake coordinator role to be silently deleted; either redesign it and staff it, or surface the cut and account for Omar.
