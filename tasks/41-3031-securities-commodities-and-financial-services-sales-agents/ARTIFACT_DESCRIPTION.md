# Equity Idea Note: Cortevia Therapeutics (CVTX) Post-Q1-2026 Earnings — Client Distribution Draft

## Occupation
- **SOC:** 41-3031
- **Title:** Securities, Commodities, and Financial Services Sales Agents

## Scenario
You are a senior institutional equity salesperson on the Healthcare desk at Hartwell Petrie & Co., a mid-sized sell-side broker-dealer. It is Tuesday, April 14, 2026, the morning after Cortevia Therapeutics (NASDAQ: CVTX) reported Q1-2026 results and updated guidance on their lead Phase III asset, CVX-417 (oncology, 2L NSCLC). You need to produce a distribution-ready "Idea Note" that your sales team will push today to roughly 40 buy-side accounts, including three key holders — Meridian Grove Capital (long-only, $38B AUM, current 2.1% position in CVTX), Polaris Equity Partners (hedge fund, $4.2B, flat), and Allenwright Asset Management (insurance GA, $112B, no position). The note must be clearly framed as a sales/research artifact — not personalized investment advice — and must comply with the firm's Reg AC and Reg FD framework.

## Inputs
The model starts with:

- **Company snapshot (CVTX):**
  - Market cap: $6.4B; float: 88M shares; 30-day ADV: 1.9M shares.
  - Consensus pre-print: Q1 revenue $127M (covering marketed asset Relvintil); EPS ($0.44).
  - Reported: Q1 revenue $141M (+11% vs. consensus), EPS ($0.38).
  - Guidance: FY26 revenue raised from $540–$560M to $575–$595M; R&D spend held.
- **Pipeline event:** CVX-417 topline PFS readout pulled forward from Q3 '26 to "late Q2 '26 or early Q3 '26," per CEO remarks on the call.
- **Internal research desk view (Dr. Priya Ahuja, Sr. Biotech Analyst, Hartwell Petrie):**
  - Rating: Buy (unchanged). Price target $94 (up from $82). Shares closed 4/13 at $73.10; +7% after-hours.
  - Probability-of-success on CVX-417 PFS: desk models 58% (up from 52% after a competitor's negative Phase II in March).
  - Consensus Street PoS: ~45%.
  - Peak risk-adjusted sales on CVX-417: $1.9B in 2031.
- **Three comparable trades/flows seen on our desk in the last 5 sessions:**
  - Block 210,000 CVTX @ $71.44 (4/10, buyer natural, long-only).
  - Options flow: April $80 calls, 4,200 contracts bought to open 4/11.
  - Paired trade: clients pairing CVTX long vs. short in Eliant Biosciences (ELB).
- **Relevant client context (sanitized CRM):**
  - Meridian Grove: held CVTX since 2023, last trimmed Feb '26, PM Sana Okafor previously said "we want another entry if CVX-417 de-risks."
  - Polaris: shorted peer Eliant in March; looked at CVTX long at $68.
  - Allenwright: IG credit-oriented; equity team (Ben Doyle) recently started sleeve for specialty pharma.
- **Compliance posted to the wire 4/13:** standard Hartwell Petrie disclosures; firm makes a market in CVTX; Dr. Ahuja holds no personal position; no IB relationship in last 12 months.

## Artifact specification
A distribution-ready institutional "Morning Idea Note" written in a research-sales voice — tight, persuasive, but compliant. Required sections:
1. **Headline and one-line thesis** (≤25 words), with ticker, price, target, and time horizon.
2. **What's new / why now** — 3–5 bullets keyed to yesterday's print and the pulled-forward catalyst.
3. **Our desk's differentiated view** — where Hartwell Petrie's analyst (Ahuja) differs from Street consensus, with specific numbers (PoS, peak sales, price target).
4. **Trade construction / ways to express the view** — outright long; paired vs. ELB; options overlay (reference the observed April $80 call flow) — each with a one-sentence rationale and risk.
5. **Account-relevance callouts** — short, tailored sentences for Meridian Grove, Polaris, and Allenwright, grounded in each account's known context (without disclosing other clients).
6. **Key risks and what would change our view** — CVX-417 failure scenario, competitive readouts, guidance credibility.
7. **Catalysts calendar** — next 6 months, with dates or windows.
8. **Required disclosures** — Reg AC language, market-making, analyst ownership, IB relationship; a clear "not personalized advice; institutional audience only" framing.

Tone: professional, confident, sell-side-sales cadence (active voice, numbers up front), but compliant and non-advisory. No exclamation marks, no emojis, no retail-style pitch language. Use "we" for the desk view; attribute analyst numbers explicitly to Dr. Ahuja.

## Output format
Markdown, rendered length ~1.5 pages / 700–1,000 words. One catalysts table and one "ways to play" table. Headline capped at one line.

## Iteration targets
1. **Sharpen the differentiated view.** v1 often reads as a recap of the print. Iterate so Section 3 makes the 58% vs. ~45% PoS delta — and *why* our desk gets there — the centerpiece, with the competitor-readout link made explicit.
2. **Tailor the account callouts with real specificity.** v1 tends to write generic "long-only accounts should consider" lines. Better: reference Okafor's prior "de-risk" comment for Meridian; Polaris's existing ELB short as a natural pair; Allenwright's new specialty-pharma sleeve as a fit for a core entry.
3. **Make the trade-construction section actionable.** v1 lists "buy the stock." Iterate to at least three distinct expressions (outright, pair vs. ELB, April $80 call overlay referencing the observed flow) with a one-line risk for each.
4. **Add genuine risks, not fig-leaf risks.** v1 may list "market risk." Replace with: PFS miss implied downside to $48–$52 per Ahuja bear case; guidance raise may reflect stocking not demand; competitor's Phase II miss may not be read-through.
5. **Tighten disclosures without burying them.** v1 either over-disclaims or hides compliance at the bottom in a wall of text. Iterate to a clean, labeled disclosure block that a compliance officer would approve on first read.
6. **Get the catalysts calendar right.** v1 often lists vague "upcoming milestones." Iterate to dated or windowed events (Q2-end readout window, ASCO late-May abstract drop, Q2 print late-July).

## Success criteria
(For evaluator use only.)
1. The note is framed unambiguously as a sales/research distribution artifact for institutional accounts — not personalized investment advice — and includes Reg AC-style attribution and market-making disclosure.
2. Section 3 makes a specific, quantified differentiated call (58% vs. ~45% PoS; $94 vs. Street) and tells the reader *why* the desk view diverges.
3. At least three distinct trade expressions are given, including at least one paired and one options idea grounded in the observed flow.
4. Each of the three named accounts receives a callout that references that account's specific prior context without leaking other clients' positions.
5. The risks section names at least one scenario that would invalidate the thesis, with an implied price level or magnitude.
6. The catalysts table has dated or narrowly windowed events across the next 6 months, not vague milestones.

## Scope target
A v1 at ~15 minutes should contain: a one-line thesis with ticker/price/target, 3–4 "what's new" bullets from the Q1 print and pulled-forward catalyst, a first-pass differentiated-view paragraph citing the 58% PoS and $94 PT, one trade-construction table with at least the outright and the pair, brief account callouts for Meridian and Polaris (Allenwright can be a stub), a 3-bullet risks list, and a catalysts table with 3–4 events. Full disclosure block, options overlay detail, the Allenwright-specific callout, and the bear-case price level can be iteration work.
